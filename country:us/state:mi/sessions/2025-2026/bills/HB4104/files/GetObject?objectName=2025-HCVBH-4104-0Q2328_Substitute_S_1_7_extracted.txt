4 (a) "Occupational therapy assistant" means an individual who

6 to engage in practice as an occupational therapy assistant.
7 (b) "Occupational therapist" means an individual who is

11 provided to promote health and wellness, prevent disability,

23 functions, body structure, habits, routines, role performance,
24 behavior patterns, sensory motor skills, cognitive skills,
25 communication and interaction skills, and cultural, physical,
26 psychosocial, spiritual, developmental, environmental, and
27 socioeconomic contexts and activities that affect performance.

3 (d) "Practice as an occupational therapy assistant" means the
4 practice of occupational therapy under the supervision of an
5 occupational therapist licensed under this article.
6 (e) "Practice of occupational therapy" means the therapeutic
7 use of everyday life occupations and occupational therapy services
8 to aid individuals or groups to participate in meaningful roles and
9 situations in the home, school, workplace, community, and other
10 settings, to promote health and wellness through research and
11 practice, and to serve those individuals or groups who have or are
12 at risk for developing an illness, injury, disease, disorder,
13 condition, impairment, disability, activity limitation, or
14 participation restriction. The practice of occupational therapy
15 addresses the physical, cognitive, psychosocial, sensory, and other
16 aspects of performance in a variety of contexts to support
17 engagement in everyday life activities that affect a person's an
18 individual's health, well-being, and quality of life throughout his
19 or her the individual's life span. The practice of occupational
20 therapy does not include any of the following:
21 (i) The practice of medicine or osteopathic medicine and
22 surgery or medical diagnosis or treatment.
23 (ii) The practice of physical therapy.
24 (iii) The practice of optometry.
25 (2) In addition to the definitions in this part, article 1
26 contains general definitions and principles of construction

28 definitions applicable to this part.

18 occupational therapy as an occupational therapy assistant under the
19 occupational therapy licensure compact is considered an
20 occupational therapy assistant who is licensed under this part.

22 is created in the department and shall consist of the following 9
23 voting members who shall must meet the requirements of part 161: 5
24 licensed occupational therapists and 4 public members, 1 of whom
25 shall be is a physician licensed under part 170 or 175.
26 (2) The terms of office of individual members of the board
27 created under this section, except those appointed to fill
28 vacancies, expire 4 years after the appointment on December 31 of
29 the year in which the term expires.
