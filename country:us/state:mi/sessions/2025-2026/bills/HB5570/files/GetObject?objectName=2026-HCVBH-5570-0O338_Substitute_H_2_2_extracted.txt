Section 1:
SUBSTITUTE FOR
A bill to amend 1972 PA 230, entitled
"Stille-DeRossett-Hale single state construction code act,"
(MCL 125.1501 to 125.1531) by adding section 4j.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF MICHIGAN ENACT:
1 Sec. 4j. (1) A multiple-family dwelling that has not more than
2 4 levels above grade plane may have a single interior exit stairway
3 if all of the following conditions are met:
4 (a) The dwelling has not more than 4 units on each level.
5 (b) The net floor area of each level of the dwelling is not
6 more than 4,000 square feet.
7 (c) The exit access travel distance is not more than 125 feet
8 measured from the most remote point within a level of the dwelling
9 along the natural and unobstructed path of egress travel.
GSS H00659'25 (H-2) 465dkw
2
1 (d) Openings to the interior exit stairway enclosure are
2 limited to all of the following openings:
3 (i) Openings required for exit access into the enclosure from
4 normally occupied spaces.
5 (ii) Openings required for egress from the enclosure.
6 (iii) Openings to the exterior.
7 (e) An elevator does not open into the interior exit stairway
8 enclosure.
9 (f) The dwelling has a manual fire alarm system and an
10 automatic smoke detection system that activates an occupant
11 notification system. The manual fire alarm system, automatic smoke
12 detection system, and occupant notification system all must be
13 installed in accordance with the building code.
14 (g) Smoke detectors are located in common spaces outside of
15 the dwelling units, including, but not limited to, all of the
16 following areas:
17 (i) Gathering areas.
18 (ii) Laundry rooms.
19 (iii) Mechanical equipment rooms.
20 (iv) Storage rooms.
21 (v) Interior corridors.
22 (vi) Interior exit stairways.
23 (vii) Exit passageways.
24 (h) Regardless of the stairway construction type in the
25 dwelling, automatic sprinkler locations in the interior exit
26 stairway comply with the requirements for combustible stairways
27 under the "Standard for the Installation of Sprinkler Systems",
28 2025 edition, developed by the National Fire Protection
29 Association. If a standard described in this subdivision is revised
GSS H00659'25 (H-2) 465dkw
3
1 after the effective date of the amendatory act that added this
2 section, the department shall take notice of the revision and the
3 department shall promulgate rules to incorporate the revision by
4 reference.
5 (i) An electrical receptacle is not located in an interior
6 exit stairway.
7 (j) The interior exit stairway is not less than 48 inches
8 wide.
9 (2) This section does not apply if the department incorporates
10 by reference the International Code Council's publication of single
11 interior exit stairway for a multiple-family dwelling that is 4 or
12 more levels above grade plane into the code.
