MICHIGAN SENATE


Senate Resolution No. 93


Offered by Senators Singh, Brinks and Polehanki


A RESOLUTION TO RECOGNIZE FEBRUARY 2026

AS SCHOOL-BASED HEALTH CARE AWARENESS MONTH


WHEREAS, Michiganâs child and adolescent health centers (also known as school-based health centers) have delivered comprehensive primary health care, mental health care, and prevention services for over 30 years; and

WHEREAS, There are over 300 child and adolescent health centers and programs in Michigan that deliver a range of primary, preventive, early intervention, and mental health services to children and youth of all grade levels in urban, rural, and suburban schools across the state; and

WHEREAS, Youth served by child and adolescent health centers show improved rates of school attendance, enhanced scores on standardized tests, less absenteeism due to illnesses, and increased immunization rates, while having their wellness and health care needs addressed; and

WHEREAS, School-Based Health Care Awareness Month acknow-ledges the commitment and passion that child and adolescent health center staff and parents/guardians have for all Michigan children and youth; and

WHEREAS, We also recognize the critical role that these individuals play in improving the health and well-being of all Michigan children; now, therefore, be it

RESOLVED BY THE SENATE, That the members of this legis-lative body recognize February 2026 as School-Based Health Care Awareness Month.

Adopted by the Senate, February 4, 2026.
