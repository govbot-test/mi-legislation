SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 93 SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 93 Senators Singh, Brinks and Polehanki offered the following resolution: A resolution to recognize February 2026 as School-Based Health Care Awareness Month. Whereas, Michiganâs child and adolescent health centers (also known as school-based health centers) have delivered comprehensive primary health care, mental health care, and prevention services for over 30 years; and Whereas, There are over 300 child and adolescent health centers and programs in Michigan that deliver a range of primary, preventive, early intervention, and mental health services to children and youth of all grade levels in urban, rural, and suburban schools across the state; and Whereas, Youth served by child and adolescent health centers show improved rates of school attendance, enhanced scores on standardized tests, less absenteeism due to illnesses, and increased immunization rates, while having their wellness and health care needs addressed; and Whereas, School-Based Health Care Awareness Month acknowledges the commitment and passion that child and adolescent health center staff and parents/guardians have for all Michigan children and youth; and Whereas, We also recognize the critical role that these individuals play in improving the health and well-being of all Michigan children; now, therefore, be it Resolved by the Senate, That the members of this legislative body recognize February 2026 as School-Based Health Care Awareness Month.
