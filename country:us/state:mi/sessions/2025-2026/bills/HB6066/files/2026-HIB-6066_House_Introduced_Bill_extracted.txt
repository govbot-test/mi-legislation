PART 128. ONSITE WASTEWATER TREATMENT SYSTEMS Sec. 12801. (1) As used in this part and sections 12752 to 12758: (a) "Alternative system" means a category of onsite wastewater treatment systems that are not conventional systems and that provide for an equivalent or better degree of protection for public health and the environment than a conventional system. (b) "Approved onsite wastewater treatment system" means an alternative system or conventional system for which a construction permit and any required operating permits have been issued by the department or a local health department having jurisdiction and an inspection has found the system to be in compliance with all required permits. (c) "Authorized local health department" means a local health department authorized by the department to administer this part and part 127 under section 12803 for conventional systems and section 12805 for alternative systems. (d) "Baffle" means a precast or preconstructed component of a septic tank that serves to redirect, divert, or impede the flow of wastewater through the septic tank in a beneficial manner. (e) "Change in use or an increase in use" means a new or increased use of the groundwater of this state for the disposal of sanitary sewage through an onsite wastewater treatment system, through 1 or more of the following: (i) Connection of a new structure to an existing onsite wastewater treatment system. (ii) Increase in use of the structure resulting in an increased sanitary sewage flow rate that exceeds the system design capacity. (iii) Change in purpose for which a structure or portion of a structure is utilized or occupied between residential, nonresidential, or another type of nonresidential use or occupancy. (iv) Increase in the wastewater effluent strength. (v) Any other change or increase in use that may reasonably be expected to affect the ability of the onsite wastewater treatment system to function as intended or perform as designed. (f) "Construction permit" means a permit to install, construct, alter, or repair an onsite wastewater treatment system. (g) "Conventional system" means a category of onsite wastewater treatment system that contains a watertight septic tank and soil dispersal system that depends solely on subsurface soil for treatment and dispersal and does not include any components of an approved alternative technology. (h) "Department" means the department of environment, Great Lakes, and energy. (i) "Effluent" means liquid flowing out of a component or device. (j) "Elements of a nonconforming system" means the presence of 1 or more of the following that may, as determined by an authorized local health department, lead to a failure or improper functioning of the onsite wastewater treatment system: (i) Evidence of compaction or settling in the area of the onsite wastewater treatment system that limits drainage in the area of the system on the parcel, and rain and snow melt cannot percolate through the system. (ii) The soil dispersal system is hydraulically saturated. (iii) Evidence of missing or damaged appurtenances of the onsite wastewater treatment system. (iv) Excessive biomatting is noted in 2 or more core samples performed across the soil dispersal system that indicates that the soil is no longer accepting effluent at a sufficient rate of infiltration. (v) Evidence of sustained operating levels within the septic tank above the invert of the septic tank outlet. (vi) The system includes a proprietary product or nonproprietary technology that meets the definition of an alternative system but that was not included in the construction permit. (vii) The system is located too close to a drinking water supply well or surface waters as determined by the statewide sewage code. (viii) The system has insufficient soil depth below the soil dispersal system to provide adequate wastewater treatment before dispersal to the groundwater. (ix) Other conditions that may reasonably be expected to alter the intended functionality of the onsite wastewater treatment system. (k) "Evaluator" means an individual registered under section 12823. (l) "Experimental onsite wastewater treatment system" means a class of alternative onsite wastewater treatment system that is in experimental use and requires further testing in order to provide sufficient information to determine its acceptability. (m) "Failure" means a malfunction or condition of an onsite wastewater treatment system that consists of 1 or more of the following, as determined by an authorized local health department: (i) A discharge of sanitary sewage to the surface of the ground. (ii) A discharge of sanitary sewage or effluent into surface water, storm drains, or directly into groundwater. (iii) The inability of the onsite wastewater treatment system to accept sanitary sewage at the rate being discharged, resulting in the backup of sanitary sewage into a structure. (iv) A structural failure or leaking of a septic tank or tanks or other associated components and appurtenances. (v) A discharge of treated wastewater that does not comply with applicable standards established in the statewide sewage code. (vi) An illicit connection or illicit discharge. (vii) An absence of all or a key part of an onsite wastewater treatment system, as determined in accordance with the statewide sewage code. (viii) The discharge of sanitary sewage or effluent is endangering a public or private water supply. (ix) The inability to access key parts of the onsite wastewater treatment system. (x) The authorized local health department determines that the elements of a nonconforming system contribute to the overall failure of the onsite wastewater treatment system in accordance with the statewide sewage code. (n) "Fund" means the onsite wastewater treatment system administration fund created in section 12829. (o) "Groundwater" means water in the ground that is in the zone of saturation. (p) "Illicit connection" means a physical, constructed connection that allows the bypassing of required treatment by an onsite wastewater treatment system in violation of law. (q) "Illicit discharge" means any direct or indirect discharge of sanitary sewage in violation of law. (r) "Imminent danger" means a condition or practice that could reasonably be expected to cause death, disease, or serious physical harm immediately or before the imminence of the danger can be eliminated through enforcement procedures. (s) "Maintenance" means routine or periodic action taken to ensure proper system performance, extend system longevity, and ensure a system meets performance requirements as designed, including servicing the system through cleaning, septic tank pumping, effluent filter cleaning or replacements, flushing laterals, vegetation removal, effluent sampling if applicable, adjustments to subcomponents, and other actions as recommended by a product manufacturer and in accordance with the statewide sewage code. (t) "Management" means the siting, design, permitting, construction, installation, inspection, operation, maintenance, alteration, evaluation, rejuvenation, closure, and repair of onsite wastewater treatment systems to ensure that onsite wastewater treatment systems meet required performance standards established in the statewide sewage code that are protective of public health and the environment. (u) "Minor repair" means the act of fixing or replacing substandard or damaged subcomponents, such as septic tank access lids, safety devices, risers, septic tank outlet baffle or outlet devices, switches, solid piping located outside the soil dispersal system, pumps, valves, and other subcomponents as determined in accordance with the statewide sewage code. (v) "Nonproprietary technology" means a wastewater treatment or distribution technology, method, or product not subject to a patent or trademark. (w) "Onsite wastewater treatment system" or "system" means a system of components and appurtenances, also referred to as a septic system, septic tank disposal system, sewage system, sewage disposal system, or subsurface sewage disposal system, that is used, or intended to be used, to collect, hold, and treat less than 10,000 gallons per day of sanitary sewage through 1 of the following methods: (i) Subsurface discharge to a soil dispersal system. (ii) Subsurface discharge to another system that meets the requirements of the statewide sewage code. (iii) A holding tank, outhouse, innovative system, or other component that collects and stores sanitary sewage until being transported to an approved location for the necessary final dispersal or final treatment. (x) "Onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation" or "evaluation" means an assessment of the sanitary sewage handling methods where a premises or structure is not connected to a public sanitary sewer system or an evaluation of an onsite wastewater treatment system conducted under section 12821. (y) "Operating level" means the elevation of effluent in a tank. For a septic tank, operating level is determined by the invert of the outlet piping. (z) "Operating permit" means a renewable and revocable permit, if required, to operate and maintain an onsite wastewater treatment system in compliance with specific operational or performance requirements. (aa) "Outlet device" means any fixture on or around the outlet pipe of a septic tank that serves to slow down, divert, screen, or otherwise control the flow of effluent out of the septic tank. (bb) "Point of sale" means the time at which the sale or transfer of a parcel of real estate is complete. Point of sale does not include a transfer described under section 3 of the seller disclosure act, 1993 PA 92, MCL 565.953. (cc) "Proprietary product" means a treatment or distribution product held under a patent or trademark that significantly contributes to the treatment, performance, and attainment of effluent quality or dispersal objectives. (dd) "Public sanitary sewer system" means that term as defined in section 12751 that is operated by a local unit of government. (ee) "Sanitary sewage" means treated or untreated waste that contains 1 or more of the following: (i) Feces or other excreta from the human body. (ii) Waste generated as a result of food service establishment activities. (iii) Waste generated from sanitary conveniences, including, but not limited to, toilets, urinals, sinks, showers, bathtubs, dishwashers, garbage grinders, and household laundry fixtures available for use associated with office, industrial, commercial, recreational, institutional, or other purposes. (iv) Additional domestic flow generated as part of a household activity. (ff) Sanitary sewage does not include roof runoff, building footing drain, wastes from industrial or commercial processes, and stormwater. (gg) "Septic tank" means a watertight, covered receptacle for collection and treatment of sanitary sewage that does all of the following: (i) Receives the discharge of sanitary sewage. (ii) Separates settleable and floating solids from the liquid. (iii) Digests organic matter by anaerobic bacterial action. (iv) Stores digested solids through a period of detention. (v) Allows clarified liquid effluent to discharge for additional treatment by components and appurtenances, such as a soil dispersal system. (vi) Attenuates flows. (hh) "Soil dispersal system" means a method in which septic tank effluent or treated effluent is distributed by a network of piping or other approved material to an approved method of subsurface dispersal. (ii) "Statewide sewage code" means the rules promulgated under section 12817 that provide for the management of onsite wastewater treatment systems. (jj) "Structure in which sanitary sewage originates" or "structure" means a building in which toilet, kitchen, laundry, bathing, or other facilities that generate water-carried sanitary sewage are used or are available for use for household, commercial, industrial, or other purposes. (kk) "Surface water" means that term as defined in section 3112a of the natural resources and environmental protection act, 1994 PA 451, MCL 324.3112a. (ll) "Technical advisory committee" means the technical advisory committee established by the department under section 12815. (b) Standards for onsite wastewater treatment systems products, components, and devices. (c) Standards for the installation of onsite wastewater treatment systems. (d) Standards for wastewater effluent. (e) Standards for the continued use of an onsite wastewater treatment system. (f) Criteria for granting variances under sections 12803 and 12821. (g) Any other requirement necessary to implement and carry out this part and part 127. Sec. 12818. A person that does either of the following is guilty of a misdemeanor punishable by imprisonment for not more than 1 year or a fine of not more than $10,000.00 for each violation, or both: (a) Submits or provides to the department or authorized local health department false, misleading, or fabricated information related to an onsite wastewater treatment system. (b) Knowingly completes an onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation for purposes of compliance with section 12821(4) and is not an evaluator. Sec. 12821. (1) Beginning 45 days after the department promulgates rules under section 12817 and subject to subsection (12), an owner of a premises or structure in which sanitary sewage originates, that is not connected to a public sanitary sewer system operated by a local unit of government must obtain an onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation in accordance with the schedule established under subsections (2) and (3). (2) An onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation conducted under this part, including an evaluation conducted under a point of sale ordinance or regulation subject to section 12831, must be conducted by an authorized local health department or evaluator in accordance with this part and in accordance with the following timeline: (a) Not later than 10 years after the department promulgates rules under section 12817, if the onsite wastewater treatment system is 20 years old or older on or before the effective date of the amendatory act that added this section, and 1 or both of the following conditions exist: (i) The onsite wastewater treatment system is installed within 500 feet of any surface water or within a high risk erosion area, critical dune, or 100-year floodplain, as these terms are determined by the department. (ii) The department or local health department does not have a record of a construction permit being issued for the installation of an onsite wastewater treatment system. (b) Before a change in use or an increase in use of the existing onsite wastewater treatment system. (c) Any time in accordance with the statewide sewage code. (d) Any time, if the evaluation is arranged or allowed by the real property owner of a premises or structure. (e) Not later than 10 years after the department promulgates rules under section 12817, if the onsite wastewater treatment system is 30 years old or older on or before the effective date of the amendatory act that added this section. (3) After the first evaluation is conducted under subsection (2), an onsite wastewater treatment system must be evaluated in accordance with this section once every 10 years. Subject to the statewide sewage code, after the first evaluation is conducted under subsection (2), a local health department may determine that an onsite wastewater treatment system does not require a subsequent evaluation under this section if an approved onsite wastewater treatment system is installed or corrective action, remedy, or management steps are completed on the existing system. An owner of a premises or structure not connected to a public sanitary sewer that does not arrange for an evaluation under this subsection or subsection (2) is not excluded from the requirement to arrange for an evaluation. Beginning 10 years after the effective date of rules promulgated by the department under section 12817, an onsite wastewater treatment system must be evaluated every 10 years if either of the following applies: (a) The system is 30 years old or older. (b) If an evaluation was not conducted under subsection (2), the system is 20 years old or older and is installed within 500 feet of surface water or within a high-risk erosion area, critical dune, or 100-year floodplain, as these terms are determined by the department. (4) Not later than 10 years after the effective date of rules promulgated under section 12817, a local health department may submit an application on a form and in a manner prescribed by the department to request an extension to the timeline requirements under subsection (2). Not later than 60 days after a request for an extension under this subsection is received, the department shall grant or deny the extension. If the department denies the request for an extension under this subsection, the department must state the reasons for the denial in writing. An extension granted under this subsection is valid for 1 year. A local health department may apply for a subsequent extension in the same manner as an initial extension under this subsection but may apply for not more than 2 extensions in total. The department shall consider all of the following when determining whether to grant an extension under this subsection: (a) Documented lack of adequate funding to conduct evaluations. Documentation under this subdivision must include, but is not limited to, information on the financial standing of the local health department and efforts made to raise funding, including charging a fee as described under subsection (13). (b) Documented lack of adequate evaluator or local public health qualified staff to complete evaluations despite documented efforts to hire needed staff. (c) Documented inability to successfully complete the evaluations required under subsection (2). (5) An authorized local health department or evaluator shall conduct an onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation not later than 90 days after the owner of the system arranges for the evaluation. An authorized local health department or evaluator may work with individuals who are involved in the management of onsite wastewater treatment systems, in accordance with the statewide sewage code, to complete an evaluation under this subsection. The owner of the system shall not have the septic tank or tanks pumped out within 45 days before an onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation is arranged under this section. At the onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation, the authorized local health department or evaluator shall do all of the following: (a) Identify the size, age, and visible condition of the septic tank or tanks. (b) Before conducting any pumping, document the level of effluent, in relation to the operating level in the septic tank or tanks. (c) Document the level of scum and sludge by measuring the vertical column depth of each layer of stratified septic tank contents for each tank or compartment and arrange for the services of a septage waste servicing business licensed under part 117 of the natural resources and environmental protection act, 1994 PA 451, MCL 324.11701 to 324.11721, to pump out the septic tank or tanks, or recommend to the authorized local health department that the septic tank or tanks be pumped out, if 1 of the following applies: (i) The authorized local health department or evaluator that conducts the onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation determines that it is necessary to complete the evaluation. (ii) The sludge and scum accumulations within the septic tank or tanks exceed 30% of each individual tank's volume or are encroaching on the inlet or outlet. (d) Verify that all sanitary sewage-related plumbing fixtures are connected to the septic tank or tanks and are operational and identify any sources of nonsanitary sewage-related connections to the septic tank or tanks or the soil dispersal system that require disconnection. (e) Locate, expose, open, and assess the septic tank or tanks and pumping chambers associated with the system. If the lid of any septic tank or pumping chamber is greater than 12 inches below the ground surface, the authorized local health department or evaluator may require that the property owner uncover the lid or lids for ease of access. (f) If the system includes an alternative system or proprietary product, identify that the required operation and maintenance reporting is on file with the department or authorized local health department. If the operation and maintenance reporting are not up to date, notify the owner of the system of the need to arrange for the required operation and maintenance by a qualified service provider, subject to the statewide sewage code, and report the findings to the authorized local health department. The authorized local health department or evaluator shall assess and report any unresolved issues identified in the required operation and maintenance report. (g) To the extent reasonable, locate, determine the size of, and observe the subsurface dispersal system to determine its condition. (h) Document the location in relation to the site of any attributes including, but not limited to, drinking water wells, surface water, and other setbacks as required by the statewide sewage code. (i) Observe or find information regarding the location of a well, as that term is defined in section 12701, and assess the approximate distance between the nearest well and the onsite wastewater treatment system, if applicable. (j) Observe the general area that includes the treatment and dispersal system for evidence of system failure or elements of a nonconforming system. (k) Examine the premises for an illicit discharge to, including, but not limited to, the surface of the ground, surface water, groundwater, or drain. (l) Document conditions observed, evidence of failure, and elements of a nonconforming system. (m) Assess and report the ponding level found in inspection or observation ports located in the soil dispersal system. If inspection or observation ports are not found, or do not penetrate to the bottom of the soil dispersal system, collect at least 2 core samples across the soil dispersal system. (n) Estimate the current daily sanitary sewage flow, identify structure use type, and identity and collect information on the proposed new or increase in use. (o) Complete any other evaluation criteria set by the department in the statewide sewage code. (p) Prepare an evaluation report that contains the information gathered under this subsection, including appropriate photographic documentation taken at the evaluated premises. (6) Not later than 14 days after the onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation is completed, the authorized local health department or evaluator that conducts the evaluation shall do all of the following: (a) Prepare an evaluation report that details the findings of the onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation. An evaluation report must be an objective and fact-based summary of conditions observed on site at the time of the evaluation; must contain information that follows standard report forms prescribed by the department; and may contain recommendations to extend the life of the onsite wastewater treatment system and prevent premature failures of the system. (b) Provide a printed or electronic copy of the evaluation report to the owner of the onsite wastewater treatment system and an electronic copy of the evaluation report to the authorized local health department. An evaluation report must be provided under this subsection even if the evaluation report is incomplete or the fee provided for in subsection (13) or (14) was not paid. (c) Provide a printed or electronic copy of the minimum standards and criteria for conducting evaluations developed by the department under section 12825(1) to the owner of the onsite wastewater treatment system. (7) An evaluation completed by an evaluator must be reviewed by the authorized local health department. The authorized local health department shall determine the need for pumping, elements of a nonconforming system, determination of failure or imminent danger, or resolution. Not later than 21 days after an evaluator submits an evaluation report under subsection (6), the authorized local health department must do all of the following: (a) Based on the evaluation, determine the status of all of the following: (i) Whether the onsite wastewater treatment system is in failure. (ii) Whether elements of a nonconforming system are contributing to an overall failure of the onsite wastewater treatment system. (iii) Whether elements of a nonconforming system are not contributing to an overall failure and the onsite wastewater treatment system meets a sufficient level of compliance and can be managed in a manner to provide for the minimum level of protection of public health and the environment to allow for continued use. (iv) Whether the onsite wastewater treatment system meets a sufficient level of compliance and can be managed to provide for the minimum level of protection of public health and the environment to allow for continued use. (v) Whether the onsite wastewater treatment system is in compliance with this part and the statewide sewage code. (vi) Whether the evaluation report was unable to be completed and the owner of premises or a structure not connected to public sanitary sewer needs to take corrective actions to allow an evaluator to complete an evaluation. (b) Determine whether any corrective actions or management steps must be taken by the owner of the onsite wastewater treatment system to satisfy the requirements of the statewide sewage code and this section. (c) Determine whether the septic tank or tanks need to be pumped and review the provided report for any pumping, to determine if the relevant requirements of the statewide sewage code are satisfied. (d) Inform the owner of the onsite wastewater treatment system of any determination made under subdivisions (a) to (c) and specify an applicable time period for corrective actions to be completed. (8) If the authorized local health department determines that an evaluation report under subsection (6) identifies the need for the septic tank or tanks to be pumped, the owner of the onsite wastewater treatment system shall have the tank or tanks pumped by a septage waste servicing business licensed under part 117 of the natural resources and environmental protection act, 1994 PA 451, MCL 324.11701 to 324.11721, not later than 60 days after the evaluation report is received under subsection (6). (9) Subject to subsection (11), if the owner of an onsite wastewater treatment system or the owner of the premises or structure not connected to a public sanitary sewer fails to arrange for an onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation, fails to have the system evaluated in accordance with the requirements of this section, or fails to complete corrective actions identified under subsection (7)(d), the owner is subject to the following: (a) Beginning 60 days after the end of the applicable time period under subsection (2), (3), or (7) and in the absence of good cause shown by the owner under subsection (11), a civil fine of not more than $250.00 for each 30-day period that the system remains unevaluated or corrective actions remain incomplete. (10) Subject to subsection (11), if the authorized local health department determines that an evaluation report under subsection (6) identifies evidence of failure, the owner of the onsite wastewater treatment system or the owner of the premises or structure shall remedy the failure within 6 months after the evaluation report is received under subsection (6) or within a time period determined by the authorized local health department. The prosecutor of the county in which the violation occurred or the attorney general may bring an action to collect the fine and require remedy of the failure. (11) The department may waive any fine authorized under subsections (9) and (10) if the owner of the onsite wastewater treatment system or the owner of the premises or structure not connected to a public sanitary sewer demonstrates either of the following: (a) That the owner's income is at or below 400% of the federal poverty guidelines, as published annually in the Federal Register by the United States Department of Health and Human Services under its authority to revise the poverty line under 42 USC 9902, and that not later than 1 year after the owner had a completed evaluation under this section or remedied any failure identified in the onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation under subsection (7). An extension to the 1-year timeline described under this subdivision may be granted in accordance with the rules promulgated under section 12817. (b) That the payment of any fines under subsection (9) or (10), or both, would pose significant financial stress on the owner of the system and that not later than 1 year of the owner of the system completed an evaluation under this section or remedied any failure identified in the onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation under subsection (7). (12) In accordance with the statewide sewage code, if determined necessary by the department and on request of an owner of an onsite wastewater treatment system, the department may grant a variance from the onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation requirements under this section. (13) An authorized local health department or evaluator may charge a fee for conducting an onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation. (14) In addition to the fee charged for conducting an onsite treatment system evaluation under subsection (13), an authorized local health department or evaluator, as applicable, shall charge an additional $50.00 state administrative fee. (2) Beginning 45 days after the department promulgates rules under section 12817, a person may apply for registration of a proprietary product under subsection (1) by submitting an application on a form and in a manner prescribed by the department. The department may charge a fee of $3,000.00 to cover its costs in reviewing applications for registration under this section. Money received by the department must be forwarded to the state treasurer for deposit into the fund. Not later than 30 days after receipt of an application and fee, the department shall review the application and determine whether the application is administratively complete. Not later than 60 days after receipt of an administratively complete application, the department shall approve or deny the registration and notify the applicant in writing. In approving and registering a proprietary product for use in this state, the department shall consider the recommended standards and guidance provided by the technical advisory committee. A registration under this section is specific to a product model within a brand or manufacturer's line and is valid for 5 years. A registration must be renewed in the same manner as an initial registration under this subsection. Beginning 3 years after the effective date of the amendatory act that added this section, and once every 3 years thereafter, the department shall adjust the fee specified under this subsection by an amount determined by the state treasurer at the end of the preceding fiscal year to reflect the cumulative percentage change in the Detroit Consumer Price Index, and rounded to the nearest $100.00. As used in this subsection, "Detroit Consumer Price Index" means the most comprehensive index of consumer prices available for the Detroit-Warren-Dearborn area from the Bureau of Labor Statistics of the United States Department of Labor. (3) The department may deny, suspend, or revoke a registration under this section following a contested case hearing conducted in accordance with the administrative procedures act of 1969, 1969 PA 306, MCL 24.201 to 24.328, if the department finds any of the following: (a) The registration was obtained or was attempted to be obtained through misrepresentation or fraud. (b) The registrant transacted business under its registration using fraudulent, coercive, or dishonest practices. (c) The applicant or registrant failed to pay the required registration application review fees. (d) Field reviews, conducted in accordance with the statewide sewage code, determine that the proprietary product does not function or perform as designed. (e) The proprietary product fails to meet the requirements of the statewide code. (4) An owner of an onsite wastewater treatment system does not need to register a proprietary product under this section if both of the following conditions are met: (a) The proprietary product was installed as part of the construction of an approved onsite wastewater treatment system before the effective date of this part. (b) The authorized local health department determines the continued use of the proprietary product can be managed in accordance with the statewide sewage code. (5) The requirements of this section apply to a person that represents a proprietary product manufacturer that is applying for registration of a proprietary product under subsection (1) before an installation that occurs after the effective date of this part. Sec. 12813. The department shall develop minimum standards and criteria to assist local health departments in permitting different types of onsite wastewater treatment systems, including, but not limited to, the following categories: (a) Nonproprietary technologies such as sand filters. (b) Proprietary products such as aerobic treatment systems and packed bed filters. (c) Proprietary products such as subsurface dripline products or gravelless distribution products. (d) Systems that have not been installed or constructed but for which a construction permit was issued by the local health department before the effective date of the statewide sewage code. Sec. 12815. (1) The technical advisory committee is created in the department to advise and provide recommendations to the department regarding the rules promulgated under section 12817. The technical advisory committee may advise the department on all of the following: (a) Recommended standards and guidance for the management of onsite wastewater treatment systems. (b) Nonproprietary technologies and recommended standards and guidance for the use of nonproprietary technologies. (c) Testing and design standards used for proprietary product registration and recommended standards and guidance for use of proprietary products. (d) Recommended standards and guidance for alternative onsite wastewater treatment systems and the severity of a failure of a system. (e) Onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation elements and reporting. (f) Registration, training, and educational requirements for evaluators and other individuals involved in the installation, maintenance, and evaluation of onsite wastewater treatment systems that are consistent with trainings established by professional organizations that represent decentralized wastewater management professionals recognized by the department or recognized by another entity approved by the department. (g) Ethical standards for evaluators and other individuals involved in the installation, maintenance, and evaluation of onsite wastewater treatment systems that are consistent with the ethical standards established by professional organizations that represent decentralized wastewater management professionals recognized by the department. (h) Documentation required to be submitted to the department for qualifying and registering evaluators under section 12823. (i) Qualifications of individuals other than evaluators involved in the management of onsite wastewater treatment systems. (j) Continuing education requirements for individuals involved in the management of onsite wastewater treatment systems. (k) Recommended standards for implementing this part. (l) Guidance and standards for registering practitioners that construct and maintain onsite wastewater treatment systems. (m) Recommended standards and guidance for owners of onsite wastewater treatment systems for the maintenance of onsite wastewater treatment systems. (n) Recommended requirements for safety devices, which may include, but are not limited to, standards for safety devices for septic tanks that were installed or altered before the effective date of the amendatory act that added this section. (o) Recommended standards for septic tank access risers that extend to the ground surface. (p) Proposed conflict-of-interest system for evaluators and individuals who install, construct, alter, or repair onsite wastewater treatment systems. The conflict-of-interest system must not preclude evaluators from owning businesses involved in the installation, construction, alteration, or repair of onsite wastewater treatment systems and must provide a means for an evaluator or other individual to perform these services that may result from an evaluation. (q) Recommended requirements for evaluations that are in addition to the requirements imposed under section 12821, including but not limited to, all of the following: (i) Standard operating procedures to determine septic tank pumping requirements at the time of an evaluation and, if a septic tank is pumped out, within 45 days before an evaluation. (ii) Standard operating procedures for an evaluation conducted if compaction may limit drainage, if a structure is not occupied or in ordinary use, if the ground is frozen or snow covered, and if access to the onsite wastewater treatment system is not feasible. (iii) Tests and procedures for evaluators to perform to ensure the validity of onsite wastewater treatment system evaluations. (r) Proposed criteria for allowing the continued use of an onsite wastewater treatment system that was constructed before the effective date of the statewide sewage code if the use of an onsite wastewater treatment system can be managed in a manner that does all of the following: (i) Ensures an effective level of treatment of sanitary sewage determined to be necessary based on risk. (ii) Protects public health and the environment. (iii) protects surface waters of this state. (iv) Protects groundwater quality. (2) The technical advisory committee must consist of the following members who have technical or scientific knowledge applicable to onsite wastewater treatment systems: (a) All of the following voting members: (i) Four regional local health department representatives recommended by a state organization representing local health departments. Of the 4 regional local health department representatives appointed under this subdivision, 1 representative must be from the northern Lower Peninsula, 1 representative must be from the southeast Lower Peninsula, 1 representative must be from the southwest Lower Peninsula, 1 representative must be from the Upper Peninsula. The 4 members appointed under this subsection must be appointed by the department. (ii) One member appointed by the speaker of the house of representatives. (iii) One member appointed by the senate majority leader. (iv) All of the following members appointed by the governor: (A) One professional engineer. (B) One distributor or supplier of onsite system products and devices. (C) One onsite wastewater system installer who has completed training developed by the National Onsite Wastewater Recycling Association or other professional organization that represents decentralized wastewater management professionals recognized by the department. (D) One onsite wastewater system service provider that represents septage waste servicing business licensed under part 117 of the natural resources and environmental protection act, 1994 PA 451, MCL 324.11701 to 324.11721. (E) One individual with knowledge of the use of onsite wastewater treatment systems representing users of onsite wastewater treatment systems. (F) One representative from a state organization specializing in the protection of public health and the environment through improved water quality. (G) One individual who has performed at least 10 evaluations under preexisting onsite wastewater treatment system ordinances or regulations. (H) One soil scientist or academic representative specializing in soil science. (b) One nonvoting member who is a department representative appointed by the director of the department. (3) The members first appointed to the technical advisory committee must be appointed not later than 90 days after the effective date of the amendatory act that added this part. (4) Members of the technical advisory committee shall serve for terms of 4 years or until a successor is appointed, except that of the members first appointed, 6 shall serve for 2 years, and 8 shall serve for 3 years. (5) If a vacancy occurs on the technical advisory committee, an individual must be appointed in the same manner as the original appointment to fill the vacancy for the balance of the term. (6) The governor may remove a member of the technical advisory committee for incompetence, dereliction of duty, malfeasance, misfeasance, or nonfeasance in office, or any other good cause. (7) Not later than 120 days after the effective date of the amendatory act that added this section, the governor shall call the first meeting of the technical advisory committee. At the first meeting, the technical advisory committee shall create a charter or bylaws, which includes the amount of and frequency at which the technical advisory committee shall meet, as it considers necessary or appropriate. The technical advisory committee shall meet if requested by the department. (8) Seven of the members of the technical advisory committee constitute a quorum for the transaction of business at a meeting of the technical advisory committee. A majority of the members present and serving are required for official action of the technical advisory committee. (9) A meeting of the technical advisory committee may be conducted in whole or in part electronically by telephonic or video conferencing with remote participation. (10) A writing prepared, owned, used, in the possession of, or retained by the technical advisory committee in the performance of an official function is subject to the freedom of information act, 1976 PA 442, MCL 15.231 to 15.246. (11) Members of the technical advisory committee shall serve without compensation. However, members of the technical advisory committee may be reimbursed for their actual and necessary expenses incurred in the performance of their official duties as members of the technical advisory committee. (12) The technical advisory committee shall consider the following in its advice to the department: (a) Sufficient theory and applied research to document efficacy of onsite wastewater treatment systems. (b) Potential use, local soil conditions, and other factors that may affect the operation of onsite wastewater treatment systems. (c) Field performance data that confirm the product or technology functions when installed onsite as indicated by submitted documents. (d) Certification of onsite wastewater treatment systems by NSF International or another recognized certifying agency. (13) The technical advisory committee may consult with experts in the field of management of onsite wastewater treatment systems and other individuals with knowledge and expertise in the management of onsite wastewater treatment systems, which may include, but are not limited to, any of the following: (a) Hydrogeologists with hydrogeology field experience. (b) Microbiologists. (c) Other scientists or academics. (d) Onsite wastewater treatment systems product manufacturers. (e) Individuals whose premises or structure is served by an approved onsite wastewater treatment system. (f) Environmental organizations. (g) Financial organizations. (h) Home builders. (i) Real estate licensees. (j) Local units of government. (k) Conservation districts. (14) The department shall provide administrative support to the technical advisory committee. The technical advisory committee's initial advice and recommendations under subsection (1) may be provided to the department not later than 2 years after the effective date of the amendatory act that added this section. Sec. 12817. Not later than 3 years after the effective date of the amendatory act that added this part, the department, in consultation with the technical advisory committee as provided under section 12815, shall promulgate rules that establish a statewide sewage code that complies with sections 12751 to 12833 and contains provisions for the management of onsite wastewater treatment systems and performance-based standards for systems categorized as conventional and alternative onsite wastewater treatment systems and provide baseline protection for public health and the environment. The statewide sewage code must include all of the following provisions: (a) Qualifications of any person involved in the installation and management of onsite wastewater treatment systems. (b) Standards for onsite wastewater treatment systems products, components, and devices. (c) Standards for the installation of onsite wastewater treatment systems. (d) Standards for wastewater effluent. (e) Standards for the continued use of an onsite wastewater treatment system. (f) Criteria for granting variances under sections 12803 and 12821. (g) Any other requirement necessary to implement and carry out this part and part 127. Sec. 12818. A person that does either of the following is guilty of a misdemeanor punishable by imprisonment for not more than 1 year or a fine of not more than $10,000.00 for each violation, or both: (a) Submits or provides to the department or authorized local health department false, misleading, or fabricated information related to an onsite wastewater treatment system. (b) Knowingly completes an onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation for purposes of compliance with section 12821(4) and is not an evaluator. Sec. 12821. (1) Beginning 45 days after the department promulgates rules under section 12817 and subject to subsection (12), an owner of a premises or structure in which sanitary sewage originates, that is not connected to a public sanitary sewer system operated by a local unit of government must obtain an onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation in accordance with the schedule established under subsections (2) and (3). (2) An onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation conducted under this part, including an evaluation conducted under a point of sale ordinance or regulation subject to section 12831, must be conducted by an authorized local health department or evaluator in accordance with this part and in accordance with the following timeline: (a) Not later than 10 years after the department promulgates rules under section 12817, if the onsite wastewater treatment system is 20 years old or older on or before the effective date of the amendatory act that added this section, and 1 or both of the following conditions exist: (i) The onsite wastewater treatment system is installed within 500 feet of any surface water or within a high risk erosion area, critical dune, or 100-year floodplain, as these terms are determined by the department. (ii) The department or local health department does not have a record of a construction permit being issued for the installation of an onsite wastewater treatment system. (b) Before a change in use or an increase in use of the existing onsite wastewater treatment system. (c) Any time in accordance with the statewide sewage code. (d) Any time, if the evaluation is arranged or allowed by the real property owner of a premises or structure. (e) Not later than 10 years after the department promulgates rules under section 12817, if the onsite wastewater treatment system is 30 years old or older on or before the effective date of the amendatory act that added this section. (3) After the first evaluation is conducted under subsection (2), an onsite wastewater treatment system must be evaluated in accordance with this section once every 10 years. Subject to the statewide sewage code, after the first evaluation is conducted under subsection (2), a local health department may determine that an onsite wastewater treatment system does not require a subsequent evaluation under this section if an approved onsite wastewater treatment system is installed or corrective action, remedy, or management steps are completed on the existing system. An owner of a premises or structure not connected to a public sanitary sewer that does not arrange for an evaluation under this subsection or subsection (2) is not excluded from the requirement to arrange for an evaluation. Beginning 10 years after the effective date of rules promulgated by the department under section 12817, an onsite wastewater treatment system must be evaluated every 10 years if either of the following applies: (a) The system is 30 years old or older. (b) If an evaluation was not conducted under subsection (2), the system is 20 years old or older and is installed within 500 feet of surface water or within a high-risk erosion area, critical dune, or 100-year floodplain, as these terms are determined by the department. (4) Not later than 10 years after the effective date of rules promulgated under section 12817, a local health department may submit an application on a form and in a manner prescribed by the department to request an extension to the timeline requirements under subsection (2). Not later than 60 days after a request for an extension under this subsection is received, the department shall grant or deny the extension. If the department denies the request for an extension under this subsection, the department must state the reasons for the denial in writing. An extension granted under this subsection is valid for 1 year. A local health department may apply for a subsequent extension in the same manner as an initial extension under this subsection but may apply for not more than 2 extensions in total. The department shall consider all of the following when determining whether to grant an extension under this subsection: (a) Documented lack of adequate funding to conduct evaluations. Documentation under this subdivision must include, but is not limited to, information on the financial standing of the local health department and efforts made to raise funding, including charging a fee as described under subsection (13). (b) Documented lack of adequate evaluator or local public health qualified staff to complete evaluations despite documented efforts to hire needed staff. (c) Documented inability to successfully complete the evaluations required under subsection (2). (5) An authorized local health department or evaluator shall conduct an onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation not later than 90 days after the owner of the system arranges for the evaluation. An authorized local health department or evaluator may work with individuals who are involved in the management of onsite wastewater treatment systems, in accordance with the statewide sewage code, to complete an evaluation under this subsection. The owner of the system shall not have the septic tank or tanks pumped out within 45 days before an onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation is arranged under this section. At the onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation, the authorized local health department or evaluator shall do all of the following: (a) Identify the size, age, and visible condition of the septic tank or tanks. (b) Before conducting any pumping, document the level of effluent, in relation to the operating level in the septic tank or tanks. (c) Document the level of scum and sludge by measuring the vertical column depth of each layer of stratified septic tank contents for each tank or compartment and arrange for the services of a septage waste servicing business licensed under part 117 of the natural resources and environmental protection act, 1994 PA 451, MCL 324.11701 to 324.11721, to pump out the septic tank or tanks, or recommend to the authorized local health department that the septic tank or tanks be pumped out, if 1 of the following applies: (i) The authorized local health department or evaluator that conducts the onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation determines that it is necessary to complete the evaluation. (ii) The sludge and scum accumulations within the septic tank or tanks exceed 30% of each individual tank's volume or are encroaching on the inlet or outlet. (d) Verify that all sanitary sewage-related plumbing fixtures are connected to the septic tank or tanks and are operational and identify any sources of nonsanitary sewage-related connections to the septic tank or tanks or the soil dispersal system that require disconnection. (e) Locate, expose, open, and assess the septic tank or tanks and pumping chambers associated with the system. If the lid of any septic tank or pumping chamber is greater than 12 inches below the ground surface, the authorized local health department or evaluator may require that the property owner uncover the lid or lids for ease of access. (f) If the system includes an alternative system or proprietary product, identify that the required operation and maintenance reporting is on file with the department or authorized local health department. If the operation and maintenance reporting are not up to date, notify the owner of the system of the need to arrange for the required operation and maintenance by a qualified service provider, subject to the statewide sewage code, and report the findings to the authorized local health department. The authorized local health department or evaluator shall assess and report any unresolved issues identified in the required operation and maintenance report. (g) To the extent reasonable, locate, determine the size of, and observe the subsurface dispersal system to determine its condition. (h) Document the location in relation to the site of any attributes including, but not limited to, drinking water wells, surface water, and other setbacks as required by the statewide sewage code. (i) Observe or find information regarding the location of a well, as that term is defined in section 12701, and assess the approximate distance between the nearest well and the onsite wastewater treatment system, if applicable. (j) Observe the general area that includes the treatment and dispersal system for evidence of system failure or elements of a nonconforming system. (k) Examine the premises for an illicit discharge to, including, but not limited to, the surface of the ground, surface water, groundwater, or drain. (l) Document conditions observed, evidence of failure, and elements of a nonconforming system. (m) Assess and report the ponding level found in inspection or observation ports located in the soil dispersal system. If inspection or observation ports are not found, or do not penetrate to the bottom of the soil dispersal system, collect at least 2 core samples across the soil dispersal system. (n) Estimate the current daily sanitary sewage flow, identify structure use type, and identity and collect information on the proposed new or increase in use. (o) Complete any other evaluation criteria set by the department in the statewide sewage code. (p) Prepare an evaluation report that contains the information gathered under this subsection, including appropriate photographic documentation taken at the evaluated premises. (6) Not later than 14 days after the onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation is completed, the authorized local health department or evaluator that conducts the evaluation shall do all of the following: (a) Prepare an evaluation report that details the findings of the onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation. An evaluation report must be an objective and fact-based summary of conditions observed on site at the time of the evaluation; must contain information that follows standard report forms prescribed by the department; and may contain recommendations to extend the life of the onsite wastewater treatment system and prevent premature failures of the system. (b) Provide a printed or electronic copy of the evaluation report to the owner of the onsite wastewater treatment system and an electronic copy of the evaluation report to the authorized local health department. An evaluation report must be provided under this subsection even if the evaluation report is incomplete or the fee provided for in subsection (13) or (14) was not paid. (c) Provide a printed or electronic copy of the minimum standards and criteria for conducting evaluations developed by the department under section 12825(1) to the owner of the onsite wastewater treatment system. (7) An evaluation completed by an evaluator must be reviewed by the authorized local health department. The authorized local health department shall determine the need for pumping, elements of a nonconforming system, determination of failure or imminent danger, or resolution. Not later than 21 days after an evaluator submits an evaluation report under subsection (6), the authorized local health department must do all of the following: (a) Based on the evaluation, determine the status of all of the following: (i) Whether the onsite wastewater treatment system is in failure. (ii) Whether elements of a nonconforming system are contributing to an overall failure of the onsite wastewater treatment system. (iii) Whether elements of a nonconforming system are not contributing to an overall failure and the onsite wastewater treatment system meets a sufficient level of compliance and can be managed in a manner to provide for the minimum level of protection of public health and the environment to allow for continued use. (iv) Whether the onsite wastewater treatment system meets a sufficient level of compliance and can be managed to provide for the minimum level of protection of public health and the environment to allow for continued use. (v) Whether the onsite wastewater treatment system is in compliance with this part and the statewide sewage code. (vi) Whether the evaluation report was unable to be completed and the owner of premises or a structure not connected to public sanitary sewer needs to take corrective actions to allow an evaluator to complete an evaluation. (b) Determine whether any corrective actions or management steps must be taken by the owner of the onsite wastewater treatment system to satisfy the requirements of the statewide sewage code and this section. (c) Determine whether the septic tank or tanks need to be pumped and review the provided report for any pumping, to determine if the relevant requirements of the statewide sewage code are satisfied. (d) Inform the owner of the onsite wastewater treatment system of any determination made under subdivisions (a) to (c) and specify an applicable time period for corrective actions to be completed. (8) If the authorized local health department determines that an evaluation report under subsection (6) identifies the need for the septic tank or tanks to be pumped, the owner of the onsite wastewater treatment system shall have the tank or tanks pumped by a septage waste servicing business licensed under part 117 of the natural resources and environmental protection act, 1994 PA 451, MCL 324.11701 to 324.11721, not later than 60 days after the evaluation report is received under subsection (6). (9) Subject to subsection (11), if the owner of an onsite wastewater treatment system or the owner of the premises or structure not connected to a public sanitary sewer fails to arrange for an onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation, fails to have the system evaluated in accordance with the requirements of this section, or fails to complete corrective actions identified under subsection (7)(d), the owner is subject to the following: (a) Beginning 60 days after the end of the applicable time period under subsection (2), (3), or (7) and in the absence of good cause shown by the owner under subsection (11), a civil fine of not more than $250.00 for each 30-day period that the system remains unevaluated or corrective actions remain incomplete. (b) Beginning 1 year after the end of the applicable time period and in the absence of good cause shown, a civil fine of not more than $500.00 for each 30-day period that the system remains unevaluated or corrective action remains incomplete, up to $15,000.00 in total civil fines under this subsection. (10) Subject to subsection (11), if the authorized local health department determines that an evaluation report under subsection (6) identifies evidence of failure, the owner of the onsite wastewater treatment system or the owner of the premises or structure shall remedy the failure within 6 months after the evaluation report is received under subsection (6) or within a time period determined by the authorized local health department. An owner that violates this subsection is subject to a civil fine of not more than $1,000.00 for each 30-day period that the failure continues. The prosecutor of the county in which the violation occurred or the attorney general may bring an action to collect the fine and require remedy of the failure. (11) The department may waive any fine authorized under subsections (9) and (10) if the owner of the onsite wastewater treatment system or the owner of the premises or structure not connected to a public sanitary sewer demonstrates either of the following: (a) That the owner's income is at or below 400% of the federal poverty guidelines, as published annually in the Federal Register by the United States Department of Health and Human Services under its authority to revise the poverty line under 42 USC 9902, and that not later than 1 year after the owner had a completed evaluation under this section or remedied any failure identified in the onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation under subsection (7). An extension to the 1-year timeline described under this subdivision may be granted in accordance with the rules promulgated under section 12817. (b) That the payment of any fines under subsection (9) or (10), or both, would pose significant financial stress on the owner of the system and that not later than 1 year of the owner of the system completed an evaluation under this section or remedied any failure identified in the onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation under subsection (7). (12) In accordance with the statewide sewage code, if determined necessary by the department and on request of an owner of an onsite wastewater treatment system, the department may grant a variance from the onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation requirements under this section. (13) An authorized local health department or evaluator may charge a fee for conducting an onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation. An authorized local health department may charge a reasonable fee not to exceed the costs of conducting, administering, and reviewing onsite wastewater treatment system evaluations. (14) In addition to the fee charged for conducting an onsite treatment system evaluation under subsection (13), an authorized local health department or evaluator, as applicable, shall charge an additional $50.00 state administrative fee. An evaluator shall submit the administrative fee to the department at the same time an evaluation report is submitted under subsection (6). State administrative fees collected by a local health department must be forwarded to the department on a quarterly basis. Beginning 3 years after the effective date of the statewide sewage code, and every 3 years thereafter, the department shall adjust the state administrative fee in this subsection by an amount determined by the state treasurer to reflect the cumulative annual percentage change in the Consumer Price Index. As used in this subsection, "Consumer Price Index" means the most comprehensive index of consumer prices available for this state from the Bureau of Labor Statistics of the United States Department of Labor. (15) All state administrative fees and civil fines authorized and collected under this section must be forwarded to the state treasurer for deposit into the fund. (16) Beginning 2 years after the first civil fine is imposed under subsection (9)(a), the civil fine constitutes a recorded lien when filed at the register of deeds office on the real property owned by the owner of the onsite wastewater treatment system or the premises or structure. A recorded lien under this subsection is effective and has priority over all other liens and encumbrances except those filed or recorded before the date of the judgment only if notice of the lien is filed or recorded as required by state or federal law. A lien filed or recorded under this subsection is terminated in accordance with the procedures required by state or federal law not later than 14 days after the owner has the system evaluated and pays the civil fine. Sec. 12827. (1) Except as provided under section 12821(9) and (10), a person, an owner of an onsite wastewater treatment system, or an owner of a premises or structure that is not connected to a public sanitary sewer system that fails to comply with this part, part 127, a rule promulgated under part 127, or the statewide sewage code is subject to the following: (a) For a first violation, the person is guilty of a misdemeanor punishable by imprisonment for not more than 90 days, a fine of not less than $500.00 or more than $2,000.00, or both. (b) For a second or subsequent violation, the person is guilty of a misdemeanor punishable by imprisonment for not more 180 days, a fine of not less than $1,000.00 or more than $10,000.00, or both. (2) Each day that a violation continues constitutes a separate violation under subsection (1). (3) When the department or an authorized local health department determines that there are reasonable grounds to believe there has been a violation of this part, part 127, the rules promulgated under part 127, or the statewide sewage code, the authorized local health department shall investigate the violation. If the authorized local health department determines there has been a violation, the authorized local health department shall issue a notice and penalty, as applicable, under section 12803(1)(a)(ii) and order the responsible party to make corrections to be in compliance with this part, part 127, the rules promulgated under part 127, and the statewide sewage code. (4) The prosecutor of the county in which the violation occurred or the attorney general may bring an action to collect a fine authorized under this section and require compliance with this part, part 127, the rules promulgated under part 127, and the statewide sewage code. (5) A civil fine authorized and collected under this section must be forwarded to the state treasurer for deposit into the fund. (6) The department or an authorized local health department may request that the attorney general or prosecutor of the county in which the violation occurred commence a civil action for appropriate relief, including a permanent or temporary injunction, for a violation of this part, part 127, the statewide sewage code, or a provision of a permit or order issued under this part or part 127. An action under this subsection may be brought in the circuit court for Ingham County or for the county in which the defendant is located, resides, or is doing business. If requested by the defendant, within 21 days after service of process, the court shall grant a change of venue to the circuit court for Ingham County or for the county in which the alleged violation occurred, is occurring, or, in the event of a threat of violation, will occur. The court has jurisdiction to restrain the violation and require compliance. In addition to any other relief granted under this subsection, the court, except as otherwise provided in this section, shall impose a civil fine of not less than $2,500.00, and the court may award reasonable attorney fees and costs to the prevailing party. (7) Final decision-making authority with respect to this part, part 127, the rules promulgated under part 127, and the statewide sewage code remains with the department. An authorized local health department may perform delegated functions authorized by and with direction from the department in accordance with the statewide sewage code, but the department maintains ultimate enforcement authority. Sec. 12829. (1) The onsite wastewater treatment system administration fund is created in the state treasury. (2) The state treasurer may receive money or other assets from any source for deposit into the fund. The state treasurer shall direct the investment of the fund and credit to the fund interest and earnings from fund investments. (3) Money in the fund at the close of the fiscal year remains in the fund and does not lapse to the general fund. (4) The department is the administrator of the fund for auditing purposes. (5) The department shall expend money from the fund, on appropriation, only for the following purposes: (a) To administer this part. (b) For grants to local health departments to carry out their responsibilities under this part, including, but not limited to, for the digitization of records of locations relating to onsite wastewater treatment systems and the locations of drinking water supply sources, including, but not limited to, onsite water wells or a public water supply. As used in this subdivision: (i) "Public water supply" means that term as defined in section 2 of the safe drinking water act, 1976 PA 399, MCL 325.1002. (ii) "Well" means that term as defined in section 12701. (c) For grants or loans to homeowners who are below 400% of the federal poverty level for all associated costs applicable to remedy a failure of an onsite wastewater treatment system identified under section 12821 or to provide funds to compensate for an arranged onsite wastewater treatment system evaluation. As used in this subdivision, "federal poverty level" means the federal poverty guidelines published annually in the Federal Register by the United States Department of Health and Human Services under its authority to revise the poverty line under 42 USC 9902. (d) To administer a public outreach and educational campaign to inform individuals on the requirements of section 12821 and the importance of operation and maintenance of onsite wastewater treatment systems. Sec. 12831. (1) If a local unit of government, county, or local health department has a point of sale ordinance to require evaluations of onsite wastewater treatment systems, the point of sale ordinance must be consistent with or as stringent as the requirements under this part. Local ordinances must meet the requirements of sections 1115 and 2441. As used in this section, "local unit of government" means a township, city, or village in this state. (2) A point of sale ordinance may address occupancy in a manner that complies with the regulations of the local health department and is otherwise applicable to property of the same type. A point of sale ordinance shall not interfere with, condition, or prohibit the transfer of title to a property. (3) If an evaluation of an onsite wastewater treatment system cannot be performed before a sale or transfer of the property on which the onsite wastewater treatment system is situated, either the purchaser or the transferee shall notify the local health department that has jurisdiction of the property of the names of the purchasers and a timeline for the completion of an evaluation. Sec. 12833. The department, under part 41 of the natural resources and environmental protection act, 1994 PA 451, MCL 324.4101 to 324.4113, and this act, and local health departments under this act, have exclusive jurisdiction over the regulation, permitting, and evaluation of onsite wastewater treatment systems. Enacting section 1. This amendatory act takes effect 90 days after the date it is enacted into law.
