5 Sec. 2. As used in this act:
6 (a) "Department" means the department of labor and economic
7 opportunity.
8 (b) "Director" means the director of the department or the
9 director's designee.
10 (c) "Employ" means engage, permit, or allow to work.
11 (d) "Employer" means a person, firm, or corporation that
12 employs a minor, and includes this state or a political subdivision
13 of this state, an agency or instrumentality of this state, and an
14 agent of an employer.
15 (e) "Issuing officer" means the chief administrator of a
16 school district, intermediate school district, public school
17 academy, or nonpublic school, or a person authorized by that chief
18 administrator in writing to act on the chief administrator's
19 behalf.
20 (e) (f) "Minor" means a person under who is less than 18 years
21 of age.
22 (g) "Registration system" means the registration system
23 created under section 4b.
24 (f) (h) "Rule" means a rule promulgated under the
25 administrative procedures act of 1969, 1969 PA 306, MCL 24.201 to
26 24.328.

8 hours during which the minor will be employed, the wages to be

10 (b) Evidence showing that the minor is of the age required by

1 Sec. 7. (1) The issuing officer or the director, as
2 applicable, may revoke a permit based solely on the following
3 factors:
4 (a) Poor school attendance, characterized by repeated erratic
5 or unexcused absences, which results in consistent performance of
6 school work at a level lower than that which preceded the minor's
7 employment. The work permit may be revoked only after the permit
8 has been suspended. The suspension must take place upon notice to
9 the minor and the employer, and an opportunity to correct the
10 deficiency is afforded. The suspension must not exceed 30 days
11 after date of notification.
12 (b) The minor's employment is in violation of federal or state
13 law or of a regulation or rule promulgated under federal or state
14 law, and the issuing officer or the director, as applicable, is
15 informed of the violation.

17 keep a record of each refusal, suspension, or revocation and the
18 reasons for the action. Upon revocation, the minor must be informed
19 of the appeal process and be given instructions as to the
20 initiation of an appeal.

26 good standing.

7 failing grades or not meeting grade point average or class credit

10 suspensions, expulsions, or a record of repeated misconduct.
