Section 1:
SUBSTITUTE FOR
A bill to amend 1949 PA 300, entitled
"Michigan vehicle code,"
by amending section 676b (MCL 257.676b), as amended by 2021 PA 43.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF MICHIGAN ENACT:
1 Sec. 676b. (1) Subject to subsection (2), a person, an
2 individual, without authority, shall not block, obstruct, impede,
3 or otherwise interfere with the normal flow of vehicular,
4 streetcar, or pedestrian traffic upon on a public street or highway
5 in this state, by means of a barricade, object, or device, or with
6 his or her the individual's person. This section does not apply to
7 persons individuals maintaining, rearranging, or constructing
8 public utility or streetcar facilities in or adjacent to a street
9 or highway.

1 (2) Subsection (1) and any provision of the Michigan
2 Administrative Code that prohibits a person an individual from
3 standing in a roadway other than a limited access highway for the
4 purpose of soliciting a ride, employment, or business from the
5 occupant of any vehicle do not apply to a person an individual who
6 is soliciting contributions on behalf of a charitable or civic
7 organization during daylight hours, if all of the following are
8 satisfied:
9 (a) The charitable or civic organization complies with
10 applicable local government regulations. A local government may
11 enact or enforce regulations restricting, but not prohibiting, the
12 activity described in this subsection.
13 (b) The charitable or civic organization maintains at least
14 not less than $500,000.00 in liability insurance.
15 (c) The person individual is 18 years of age or older.
16 (d) The person individual is wearing high-visibility safety
17 apparel that meets current American standards promulgated by the
18 International Safety Equipment Association.
19 (e) The portion of the roadway upon on which the solicitation
20 occurs is not a work zone and is within an intersection where
21 traffic control devices are present.
22 (f) The solicitation does not block, delay, or otherwise
23 interfere with the movement of a streetcar on a streetcar track.
24 (3) A local government or road authority that has jurisdiction
25 over a roadway upon on which solicitation occurs as described in
26 subsection (2) is not liable for any claim for damages arising out
27 of the use of the roadway as described in subsection (2).
28 (4) A person Except as otherwise provided in subsection (5),
29 an individual who violates this section is responsible for a civil

2 (5) An individual who violates this section while
3 participating in an assembly on a highway in this state is guilty
4 of a misdemeanor punishable by imprisonment for not more than 93
