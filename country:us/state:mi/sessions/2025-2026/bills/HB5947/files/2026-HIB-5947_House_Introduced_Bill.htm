Sec. 17951. (1) As used in this part:

(a) "Apprentice" means an individual who is engaged in learning the practice of massage therapy through an apprenticeship program.

(b) "Apprenticeship program" means an apprenticeship program meeting the requirements of section 17959a.

(c) (a) "Feldenkrais method" means a system of somatic education in which touch and words are used to eliminate faulty habits, learn new patterns of self-organization and action, and improve a person's an individual's own functional movement patterns. Feldenkrais method is based on principles of physics, biomechanics, and an understanding of, or learning about, human development.

(d) (b) "Massage therapist" means an individual engaged who is licensed or otherwise authorized to engage in the practice of massage therapy under this article.

(c) "Polarity therapy" means diverse applications affecting the human energy system and includes energetic approaches to somatic contact, verbal facilitation, nutrition, exercise, and health education. Polarity therapy does not make medical claims, diagnose physical ailments, or allow prescription of medications.

Sec. 17959a. (1) Beginning on the effective date of the rules promulgated under this section, a massage therapist may, subject to this section, operate an apprenticeship program that is approved by the United States Department of Labor or is offered in conjunction with a school. A massage therapist who operates an apprenticeship program under this part shall ensure that the apprenticeship program complies with all of the following requirements:

(a) An apprentice is not required to be in attendance for more than 40 hours per week.

(b) A daily record of the attendance of each apprentice is maintained and a copy of the record is sent to the department on the department's request.

(c) A grading system for apprentices is established.

(d) The apprentice engages in the practice of massage therapy on a member of the public only under the supervision of the massage therapist. Before the apprentice engages in the conduct described in this subdivision, the massage therapist shall ensure that the apprentice possesses the appropriate education, experience, and skills to engage in the practice of massage therapy.

(2) Before a massage therapist accepts an individual as an apprentice, the massage therapist shall submit an application to the department for an apprenticeship permit. The application must be in a form and submitted in a manner required by the department, include the name and license number of the massage therapist who will be operating the apprenticeship program, and indicate whether the apprenticeship program is being offered through an apprenticeship program that is approved by the United States Department of Labor or is offered in conjunction with a school. An apprenticeship permit issued under this section must contain the name of the apprentice and the name and license number of the massage therapist operating the apprenticeship program. The massage therapist operating the apprenticeship program shall ensure that the apprenticeship permit is displayed in the same manner as a certificate of licensure under section 16191.

(3) To operate an apprenticeship program, a massage therapist must meet all of the following requirements:

(a) Have completed a number of hours of experience engaging in the practice of massage therapy that the board considers necessary to demonstrate the requisite knowledge and skills to train an apprentice.

(b) Hold a license under this part that is in good standing.

(c) Meet any other requirement established by the department by rule that reflects the massage therapist's ability to train an apprentice.

(4) A massage therapist who operates an apprenticeship program shall not charge a fee to an apprentice for the training of the practice of massage therapy. This subsection does not apply to any fee required by the United States Department of Labor or a school for the operation of an apprenticeship program.

(5) A massage therapist who operates an apprenticeship program where 1 apprentice is successfully trained may allow the training of additional apprentices at the location where the massage therapist operates, except that not more than 2 apprentices may be trained by a massage therapist at the same time. (6) The department may deny an application for or suspend or revoke an apprenticeship permit in the same manner as a license under part 161 for a violation of this section or a rule promulgated under this section, or for 1 or more of the grounds listed in section 16221.

(7) By 1 year after the effective date of the amendatory act that added this section, the department, in consultation with the board, shall promulgate rules to implement this section.
