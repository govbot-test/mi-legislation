Sec. 1117. (1) Except as otherwise provided in this section, barber services shall only must be rendered only in premises licensed by the department under this article. A If it is impractical or unsafe for an individual to travel due to frailty, age, injury, or illness, a barber may render services outside of a barbershop to a patient in a hospital, nursing home, home for the aged, or similar facility or to a person an individual in the person's individual's home. if it is impractical or unsafe for the patient or person to travel due to frailty, age, injury, or illness.

(2) The department may issue a temporary permit, valid for not more than 1 year, to a barber licensed under this article to render services outside of a premises licensed by the department under this article to a veteran at a veteran service organization. A barber who renders services under this subsection shall not charge a service fee.

(3) The department may issue a demonstration permit, valid for not longer more than 1 year, to allow demonstrations on premises that are not in use as a barbershop. The holder of a demonstration permit shall maintain health, safety, and sanitation standards as set forth in rules authorized promulgated under this article. The department may issue a demonstrator permit, valid for no longer not more than 1 week, to a person an individual who is not licensed in this state to perform barbering services solely for the purposes of demonstration, provided that the demonstrator is duly licensed or otherwise authorized to perform barbering services under the laws of another state, jurisdiction, or country.

(4) As used in this section and section 1217:

(a) "Service fee" means a charge to a customer for providing a service that includes fees for the act of providing the service, transportation to the site, labor, product, and tools.

(b) "Veteran service organization" means a veteran-led nonprofit organization with the primary mission of supporting veterans.
