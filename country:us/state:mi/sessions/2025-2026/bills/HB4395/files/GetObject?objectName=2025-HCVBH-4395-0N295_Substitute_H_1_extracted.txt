SUBSTITUTE FOR
A bill to amend 1980 PA 299, entitled
"Occupational code,"
by amending sections 1117 and 1217 (MCL 339.1117 and 339.1217),

Section 2:
section 1117 as amended by 2003 PA 207 and section 1217 as amended
by 2020 PA 20.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF MICHIGAN ENACT:
1 Sec. 1117. (1) Except as otherwise provided in this section,
2 barber services shall only must be rendered only in premises
3 licensed by the department under this article. A If it is
4 impractical or unsafe for an individual to travel due to frailty,
5 age, injury, or illness, a barber may render services outside of a
6 barbershop to a patient in a hospital, nursing home, home for the
7 aged, or similar facility or to a person an individual in the
VMP H01889'25 (H-1) byz5ip
2
1 person's individual's home. if it is impractical or unsafe for the
2 patient or person to travel due to frailty, age, injury, or
3 illness.
4 (2) The department may issue a temporary permit, valid for not
5 more than 1 year, to a barber licensed under this article to render
6 services outside of a premises licensed by the department under
7 this article to a veteran at a veteran service organization. A
8 barber who renders services under this subsection shall not charge
9 a service fee.
10 (3) The department may issue a demonstration permit, valid for
11 not longer more than 1 year, to allow demonstrations on premises
12 that are not in use as a barbershop. The holder of a demonstration
13 permit shall maintain health, safety, and sanitation standards as
14 set forth in rules authorized promulgated under this article. The
15 department may issue a demonstrator permit, valid for no longer not
16 more than 1 week, to a person an individual who is not licensed in
17 this state to perform barbering services solely for the purposes of
18 demonstration, provided that the demonstrator is duly licensed or
19 otherwise authorized to perform barbering services under the laws
20 of another state, jurisdiction, or country.
21 (4) As used in this section and section 1217:
22 (a) "Service fee" means a charge to a customer for providing a
23 service that includes fees for the act of providing the service,
24 transportation to the site, labor, product, and tools.
25 (b) "Veteran service organization" means a veteran-led
26 nonprofit organization with the primary mission of supporting
27 veterans.
28 Sec. 1217. A licensee that commits or has committed 1 or more
29 of the following is subject to the penalties set forth in article
VMP H01889'25 (H-1) byz5ip

1 6:
2 (a) Continued practice by an individual who knows he or she
3 the individual has an infectious or contagious disease.
4 (b) Except as otherwise provided in this subdivision,
5 practicing cosmetology on the public outside of the premises of a
6 licensed cosmetology establishment or school of cosmetology. A
7 licensed cosmetologist may perform cosmetology services for a
8 patron at a location that is not on the premises of a licensed
9 cosmetology establishment if the services are performed at any of
10 the following:
11 (i) A special event in which the cosmetology service is
12 required to be performed for an on-site participant of the event.
13 (ii) A nursing home, as that term is defined in section 20109
14 of the public health code, 1978 PA 368, MCL 333.20109, for a
15 patient or resident of that home.
16 (iii) A home for the aged, as that term is defined in section
17 20106 of the public health code, 1978 PA 368, MCL 333.20106, for a
18 patient or resident of that home.
19 (iv) An adult foster care facility, as that term is defined in
20 section 3 of the adult foster care facility licensing act, 1979 PA
21 218, MCL 400.703, for a patient or resident of that facility.
22 (v) A hospital, as that term is defined in section 20106 of
23 the public health code, 1978 PA 368, MCL 333.20106, for a patient
24 of that hospital.
25 (vi) If the licensed cosmetologist is issued a temporary permit
26 by the department, valid for not more than 1 year, a veteran
27 service organization for a veteran. A cosmetologist who performs
28 cosmetology services under this subparagraph shall not charge a
29 service fee.
