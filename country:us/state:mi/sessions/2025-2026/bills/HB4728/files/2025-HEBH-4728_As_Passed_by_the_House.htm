Sec. 1412. Beginning 2 years after the effective date of the amendatory act that added this chapter, the department shall renew a license for a guardian or conservator if the guardian or conservator, as applicable, does all of the following:

(a) Submits a completed renewal application to the department on a form prescribed by the department.

(b) Pays a renewal fee.

(c) Submits proof of having completed 30 hours of continuing education units in the previous 36-month period, as offered by a state or national trade association dedicated to the advancement of the guardianship profession.

(d) Submits a new background check as described in section 1413.

(e) Submits proof of valid professional liability insurance.
