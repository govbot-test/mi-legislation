appropriations for various state departments and agencies, the

ending September 30, 2025; to provide for certain conditions on

appropriations.

11 Sec. 201. In accordance with section 30 of article IX of the
12 state constitution of 1963, for the fiscal year ending September
13 30, 2025, total state spending from state sources under part 1 is
14 $100,000,000.00 and state spending from state sources to be paid to
15 local units of government is $100,000,000.00.
16 Sec. 202. The appropriations made and expenditures authorized
17 under this part and part 1 are subject to the management and budget
18 act, 1984 PA 431, MCL 18.1101 to 18.1594.
19 Sec. 203. If the state administrative board, acting under
20 section 3 of 1921 PA 2, MCL 17.3, transfers funds from an amount
21 appropriated under this article, the legislature may, by a
22 concurrent resolution adopted by a majority of the members elected
23 to and serving in each house, inter-transfer funds for the
24 particular department, board, commission, officer, or institution.

26 DEPARTMENT OF STATE POLICE
27 Sec. 301. (1) Funds appropriated in part 1 for storm disaster
28 relief grants must be allocated by the department to counties in
29 areas of this state that have realized a significant financial
SCS H02844'25 (H-1) ecpmsj
4
1 hardship caused by storm damage. The department shall allocate all
2 funds appropriated for storm disaster relief grants before
3 allocating the funds appropriated for disaster relief contingency
4 supplement. The department shall allocate the funds on a first-
5 come, first-served basis, as follows:
6 (a) After the effective date of this act, 50% of qualified
7 expenses submitted by a county.
8 (b) Six months after the effective date of this act, the
9 remaining 50% of the qualified expenses under subdivision (a). The
10 department shall prorate the remaining available funds, as
11 necessary.
12 (c) After the qualified expenses have been reimbursed under
13 subdivisions (a) and (b), remaining funds must be disbursed under
14 section 302.
15 (2) Funds appropriated in part 1 must be used by counties to
16 assist individual residents, businesses, and communities with
17 identifying and securing disaster assistance. Funding may be used
18 for various purposes, including, but not limited to, all of the
19 following:
20 (a) State match funding required for receipt and expenditure
21 of any federal funding made available for emergency management
22 assistance.
23 (b) Emergency actions needed to protect life, property, public
24 health, and public safety, including shelter, emergency supplies,
25 and debris clean-up, removal, and disposal.
26 (c) Energy assistance, including paying heating and
27 electricity bills, preventing shutoffs, and providing crisis
28 assistance.
29 (d) Repair or replacement of disaster-damaged public

1 infrastructure, facilities, and businesses.
2 (3) Funds received by an individual under subsection (2) must
3 be used by the individual to cover qualified storm damage related
4 expenses. Funding may be used for various purposes, including, but
5 not limited to, both of the following:
6 (a) Debris clean-up, removal, and disposal.
7 (b) Repair of damages to the individual's dwelling and
8 immediate curtilage.
9 Sec. 302. (1) Funds appropriated under part 1 for disaster
10 relief contingency supplement and any funds that remain after
11 awards have been granted for the purposes specified in section 301
12 shall be allocated for the following purposes:
13 (a) To fully reimburse expenses submitted by counties that
14 were prorated under section 301(1)(b).
15 (b) To address storm damage on land owned by the state and the
16 department of natural resources.
17 (c) To reimburse an electric cooperative regulated under the
18 electric cooperative member-regulation act, 2008 PA 167, MCL 460.31
19 to 460.39, for qualified expenses as a result of storm damage.
20 (2) The department shall allocate the funds under subsection
21 (1) on a first-come, first-served basis, as follows:
22 (a) Any remaining qualified expenses submitted by a county
23 that were prorated under section 301(1)(b).
24 (b) After all funds have been disbursed under subdivision (a),
25 50% of the qualified expenses submitted by the department of
26 natural resources or an electric cooperative under subsection (1).
27 (c) One month after the funds have been disbursed under
28 subdivision (b), the remaining 50% of the qualified expenses
29 submitted under subdivision (b). The department shall prorate the

6
1 remaining available funds, as necessary.
2 Sec. 303. The funds appropriated in part 1 for disaster relief
3 contingency supplement must not be expended until they are
4 transferred to the storm disaster relief grants line item in part 1
5 under section 393(2) of the management and budget act, 1984 PA 431,
6 MCL 18.1393.
7 Sec. 304. The unexpended funds appropriated in part 1 for
8 storm disaster relief grants and disaster relief contingency
9 supplement are designated as a work project appropriation.
10 Unencumbered or unallotted funds do not lapse at the end of the
11 fiscal year and are available for expenditures under this section
12 until the project has been completed. The following is in
13 compliance with section 451a of the management and budget act, 1984
14 PA 431, MCL 18.1451a:
15 (a) The purpose of the project is to assist individual
16 residents, businesses, and communities of this state with
17 identifying and securing disaster assistance.
18 (b) The project will be accomplished by utilizing state
19 employees, contracts with vendors, or both.
20 (c) The total estimated cost of the project is
21 $100,000,000.00.
22 (d) The tentative completion date is September 30, 2026.
