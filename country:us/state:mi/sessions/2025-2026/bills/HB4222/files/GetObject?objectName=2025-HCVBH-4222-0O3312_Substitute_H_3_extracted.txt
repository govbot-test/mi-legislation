SUBSTITUTE FOR
A bill to amend 1976 PA 451, entitled
"The revised school code,"
by amending section 1308b (MCL 380.1308b), as added by 2018 PA 436.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF MICHIGAN ENACT:

2 later than July 1, 2026, and at least biennially thereafter, every
3 3 school years thereafter, a school district, intermediate school
4 district, or public school academy, or, subject to subsection (10),
5 a nonpublic school, shall, in conjunction with at least 1 law
6 enforcement agency that has jurisdiction over the school district,
7 intermediate school district, or public school academy, or
8 nonpublic school, conduct a review of the emergency operations plan
9 developed or adopted under described in subsection (2), including a

6 a school district, intermediate school district, or public school
7 academy, or, subject to subsection (10), a nonpublic school shall
8 develop and adopt, and by not later than July 1, 2026 and every 3
9 school years thereafter, a school district, intermediate school
10 district, or public school academy shall update, with input from
11 the public, an emergency operations plan for each school building
12 operated by the school district, intermediate school district, or

14 than July 1, 2026 and every 3 school years thereafter, subject to
15 subsection (10), a nonpublic school shall update the emergency
16 operations plan for each school building operated by the nonpublic
17 school. The board of the a school district or intermediate school
18 district or the board of directors of the a public school academy
19 shall adopt the emergency operations plan by a majority vote of the
20 members serving on the board or board of directors at a public
21 meeting of the board or board of directors held in compliance with
22 the open meetings act, 1976 PA 267, MCL 15.261 to 15.275.
23 (3) The emergency operations plan developed and adopted or
24 updated under subsection (2) must include guidelines and procedures
25 that address at least all of the following:
26 (a) School violence and attacks.
27 (b) Threats of school violence and attacks.
28 (c) Bomb threats.
29 (d) Fire.

1 (e) Weather-related emergencies.
2 (f) Intruders.
3 (g) Parent and pupil reunification.
4 (h) Threats to a school-sponsored activity or event whether or
5 not it is held on school premises.
6 (i) A plan to train teachers on mental health and pupil and
7 teacher safety.
8 (j) A plan to improve school building security.
9 (k) An active violence protocol.

25 school district, or public school academy, or nonpublic school
26 shall also provide notice to the department of the adoption or
27 update of an emergency operations plan under subsection (2) by not
28 later than 30 days after the adoption or update of the emergency
29 operations plan, in a form and manner prescribed by the department.

1 (6) At least annually, the department shall provide to the
2 office of school safety within the department of state police a
3 list of each school district, intermediate school district, or
4 public school academy, or nonpublic school that has not developed
5 and adopted or updated an emergency operations plan under
6 subsection (2).
7 (7) The office of school safety within the department of state
8 police shall coordinate with a law enforcement agency that has
9 jurisdiction over a school district, intermediate school district,
10 or public school academy, or, subject to subsection (10), a
11 nonpublic school that has not developed and adopted or updated an
12 emergency operations plan under subsection (2) to notify the
13 governing body of the school district, intermediate school
14 district, or public school academy, or nonpublic school that the
15 school district, intermediate school district, or public school
16 academy, or nonpublic school has not developed and adopted or
17 updated an emergency operations plan.
18 (8) An emergency operations plan developed and adopted or
19 updated under subsection (2), any information provided to the
20 department under subsection (5), and any information provided to
21 the office of school safety within the department of state police
22 under subsection (6) is confidential and is exempt from disclosure
23 under the freedom of information act, 1976 PA 442, MCL 15.231 to
24 15.246.
25 (9) By not later than July 1, 2026, the board of a school
26 district or intermediate school district, board of directors of a
27 public school academy, or, subject to subsection (10), the
28 governing body of a nonpublic school shall ensure that each school
29 operated by the board, board of directors, or governing body has a

5
1 school crisis team that is responsible for handling a crisis in the
2 event of a safety incident. The school crisis team's
3 responsibilities include, but are not limited to, initiating a
4 lockdown procedure, assisting in evacuation of pupils and staff
5 from the school building, and overseeing parent and pupil
6 reunification. A school crisis team described in this subsection
7 must consist of at least the following members:
8 (a) The principal of the school.
9 (b) The assistant principal of the school, if available.
10 (c) A school resource officer, if available.
11 (d) School safety or security personnel who work at or for the
12 public school or nonpublic school, if available.
13 (e) Any other appropriate school personnel, as determined by
14 the principal of the school.
15 (10) The governing body of a nonpublic school may elect to
16 exempt the nonpublic school from the requirements of this section.
17 (11) (9) As used in this section, "school building" means any
18 of the following:
19 (a) A building intended to be used to provide instruction for
20 pupils, including an addition to an existing building.
21 (b) A recreational or athletic structure or field intended to
22 be used by pupils.
