Sec. 65101. As used in this part:

(a) "Carbon dioxide" means carbon dioxide that is produced by anthropogenic sources and is of such purity and quality that it will not compromise the safety of geologic storage and will not compromise those properties of a storage reservoir that allow the reservoir to effectively enclose and contain a stored gas.

(b) "Carbon dioxide capture project" means that term as defined in section 5501.

(c) "Carbon dioxide sequestration activity" or "sequestration activity" means the injection of 1 or more carbon dioxide streams into underground geologic formations for geologic storage. Carbon sequestration activity does not include an enhanced oil recovery activity or the transport by pipeline of carbon dioxide to a carbon dioxide sequestration project.

(d) "Carbon dioxide sequestration project" or "sequestration project" means 1 or more nonexperimental injection wells that connect a surface facility and equipment to a storage reservoir, the storage reservoir, and underground and surface facilities and equipment used or proposed to be used for sequestration activity. Carbon dioxide sequestration project does not include an enhanced oil recovery well or pipelines used to transport carbon dioxide to a carbon dioxide sequestration project.

(e) "Carbon dioxide sequestration project operator" or "operator" means a person that holds or is an applicant for a permit under this part.

(f) "Carbon dioxide storage reservoir" or "storage reservoir" means a subsurface sedimentary stratum or formation containing pore

Sec. 65102. (1) A person shall not engage in a carbon dioxide sequestration activity or construct or operate a carbon dioxide sequestration project except pursuant to a permit issued by the department pursuant to part 13. This prohibition does not apply to any sequestration project in existence and permitted by the United States Environmental Protection Agency before the effective date of the amendatory act that added this section or to any class VI well for which a class VI well permit application was filed with the United States Environmental Protection Agency and a completeness determination was received before the effective date of the amendatory act that added this section.

(2) An application to obtain a carbon dioxide sequestration permit under this part shall include, at a minimum, the following:

(a) A map and accompanying description that clearly identifies the location of all carbon dioxide sequestration activities for which a permit is sought.

(b) A map and accompanying description that clearly identifies the properties and any historic or culturally sensitive areas overlaying the sequestration activity.

(c) A description of how the operator will eliminate, minimize, or mitigate impacts on properties and historic or culturally sensitive areas overlaying the sequestration area.

(d) Copies of any permit and related application materials submitted to or issued by the United States Environmental Protection Agency in accordance with the federal class VI well program.

Sec. 65107. (1) Unless otherwise expressly provided by contract, bill of sale, deed, mortgage, deed of trust, or other legally binding document or by other applicable law, a carbon dioxide sequestration project operator holds title to or control of the carbon dioxide injected into and stored in a storage reservoir.

(2) The owner or operator of a carbon dioxide sequestration project may be liable for damage, including, but not limited to, wrongful death, bodily injury, or tangible property damage, caused by a release attributable to the sequestration activity, including, but not limited to, damage caused by carbon dioxide or other fluids released from the sequestration project, regardless of who holds title to the carbon dioxide, the pore space, or the surface estate. Liability for damage caused by a release attributable to the sequestration activity that is within a sequestration project or otherwise within an operator's control, including carbon dioxide being transferred from a pipeline to the injection well, may be joint and several with a third party adjudicated to have caused or contributed to such damage. A claim of subsurface trespass is not actionable against an operator or an owner of a sequestration project conducting sequestration activity in accordance with a class VI well permit and a permit issued under this part, unless the claimant proves that either of the following applies to injection or migration of carbon dioxide:

Sec. 65307. Operations conducted pursuant to an order for unit operations constitute a fulfillment of all the express and implied obligations of each lease or contract covering the lands in the unit area to the extent that compliance with the obligations is prevented by the order for unit operations.

Sec. 65308. (1) Property rights, leases, contracts, and all other rights and obligations shall be considered to be amended and modified to the extent necessary to conform to this part and to any valid and applicable plan for unit operations or order of the department made pursuant to this part.

(2) Except to the extent that the parties affected agree otherwise, an order for unit operations does not result in a transfer of all or part of the title of any person's pore space rights in any tract within the unit area.

Sec. 65309. (1) If the plan for unit operations so provides, a unit created under this part may, through its operator, sue, be sued, and contract as a unit in its own right.

Sec. 65310. After the effective date of an order for unit operations, the unit area defined in the order shall not be operated by persons other than the unit operator or persons acting under the unit operator's authority, or operated other than in the manner and to the extent provided in the plan for unit operations.

Sec. 65311. If the department or a board or officer of this state or of any agency or political subdivision of this state has control and management of land or pore space, that person may, on behalf of this state or the agency or political subdivision, consent to or participate in any plan for or program of unit operations initiated or adopted under this part.

Sec. 65312. (1) Except as provided under section 65304, the department shall not issue, put into effect, revoke, change, renew, or extend an order under this part, unless the department has held a public hearing on the proposal. The public hearing shall be held at such time, place, and manner as provided for in this part or by rules promulgated under this part, including notice as provided in subsection (2).
