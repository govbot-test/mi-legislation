Section 1:
SENATE SUBSTITUTE FOR
A bill to amend 1976 PA 451, entitled
"The revised school code,"
(MCL 380.1 to 380.1852) by adding section 1531k.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF MICHIGAN ENACT:
1 Sec. 1531k. (1) Beginning with the 2031-2032 school year, the
2 board of a school district or intermediate school district or board
3 of directors of a public school academy shall ensure that the
4 following individuals have successfully completed, as determined by
5 the department, the professional learning described in subsection
6 (2):
7 (a) Except as otherwise provided in this section, certificated
8 individuals who provide, support, or oversee instruction in grades
9 K to 5 that involves reading instruction or language arts,

2
1 including classroom teachers and teachers with any special
2 education endorsements. This section does not apply to a
3 certificated individual who solely teaches subjects outside of
4 reading instruction or language arts, including, but not limited
5 to, physical education, music, and art.
6 (b) All personnel responsible for the delivery, support, or
7 direct supervision of literacy instruction in grades K to 5, as
8 determined by the department.
9 (2) By not later than 90 days after the effective date of the
10 amendatory act that added this section, the department shall
11 annually approve at least 2 providers to offer the professional
12 learning required under subsection (1). The professional learning
13 described in this subsection must meet at least all of the
14 following requirements:
15 (a) Be offered through a system of training that provides
16 educators with the knowledge base to effectively implement any
17 class-wide, supplemental, or intervention reading approach and to
18 determine why some students struggle with reading, writing,
19 spelling, and language.
20 (b) Provide training activities that direct educators to
21 implement effective reading and spelling instruction supported by
22 scientifically based research and foster a direct explicit
23 instructional sequence that uses techniques to support teachers'
24 independence in using their newly learned skills with students in
25 the classroom.
26 (c) Include integrated components for educators and
27 administrators in grades K to 5 with embedded evaluation or
28 assessment of knowledge. Evaluation or assessment of knowledge
29 under this subdivision must incorporate evaluations of learning

3
1 throughout each unit and include a summative assessment that must
2 be completed to demonstrate successful course completion.
3 (d) Build teacher content knowledge and pedagogical knowledge
4 of the critical components of literacy, including how the brain
5 learns to read, phonological and phonemic awareness, letter
6 knowledge, phonics, advanced phonics, vocabulary and oral language,
7 fluency, comprehension, spelling and writing, and the organization
8 of language.
9 (e) Support educators in understanding how to effectively use
10 screening, progress monitoring, and diagnostic assessment data to
11 improve literacy outcomes through prevention and intervention for
12 reading difficulties in a multi-tiered system of supports. The
13 multi-tiered system of supports must include at least all of the
14 following essential components:
15 (i) Team-based leadership.
16 (ii) A tiered delivery system.
17 (iii) Selection and implementation of instruction,
18 interventions, and supports.
19 (iv) A comprehensive screening and assessment system.
20 (v) Continuous data-based decision making.
21 (f) Provide that all training, record-keeping systems, and
22 flexible scheduling options are administered by the professional
23 development provider.
24 (g) Include "train the trainer" facilitator professional
25 learning, to be implemented at the discretion of the department.
26 Participants enrolled in facilitator professional learning must
27 have demonstrated successful completion of the required
28 professional learning under subdivisions (a) to (e).
29 (h) Maintain capacity to provide all required training,

4
1 distribute materials, provide reports to the department, and
2 actively support all participants to mastery completion of
3 coursework.
4 (3) Beginning in the 2026-2027 school year, each school
5 district, intermediate school district, or public school academy
6 shall annually report to the department, in a form and manner
7 prescribed by the department, the number and percentage of
8 individuals described in subsection (1) who have completed the
9 required professional learning under this section, any costs
10 associated with providing the professional learning, and the
11 funding sources used. The department shall compile and publish an
12 annual report summarizing district-level implementation,
13 identifying areas of progress and need, and recommending policy or
14 funding adjustments as necessary to support full compliance by the
15 2031-2032 school year. The department shall submit this report to
16 the governor, state board of education, the house and senate
17 appropriations subcommittees on school aid, and the house and
18 senate education committees, and shall make the report publicly
19 available on the department's website.
20 (4) Beginning in the 2026-2027 school year, the professional
21 learning providers identified in subsection (2) shall annually
22 report all of the following information to the department, in a
23 form and manner prescribed by the department:
24 (a) The total number of individuals described in subsection
25 (1) who have completed the required professional learning,
26 organized by role group.
27 (b) The total number of individuals described in subsection
28 (1) who are enrolled in the required professional development
29 training, organized by role group.

5
1 (c) The total number of individuals described in subsection
2 (1) who enrolled but withdrew from the required professional
3 development prior to completion, organized by role group.
4 (d) The cost per individual described in subsection (1) to
5 enroll in the required professional learning.
6 (e) The total grant funds expended for all individuals who
7 completed or enrolled in the professional learning during the
8 fiscal year.
9 (f) Total unused grant funds for the fiscal year.
10 (5) The department shall begin a phased implementation of the
11 professional learning required under this section beginning in the
12 2026-2027 school year, and, subject to subsection (6), shall ensure
13 full compliance with this section by not later than the start of
14 the 2031-2032 school year. By not later than 90 days after the
15 effective date of the amendatory act that added this section, the
16 department shall create a statewide implementation plan for the
17 professional learning required under this section in collaboration
18 with statewide associations, literacy experts, and other key
19 partners. The department may issue a good cause extension for
20 compliance with this section to an individual. A good cause
21 extension issued under this subsection must include a deadline,
22 mutually agreed to between the department and the individual, for
23 when the individual must successfully complete the professional
24 learning required under this section.
25 (6) The department may consult with providers approved under
26 subsection (2) and make extensions to the implementation schedule
27 under subsection (5) as the department determines necessary.
28 Extensions made under this subsection may include, but are not
29 limited to, a 1-year extension for full compliance with this

6
1 section.
2 (7) The requirements of this section are subject to the
3 appropriation of an amount sufficient to comply with this section.
4 (8) The board of a school district or intermediate school
5 district or board of directors of a public school academy shall
6 ensure that an individual hired during or after the 2031-2032
7 school year into a role described in subsection (1) receives the
8 professional learning required under this section not later than 3
9 years following the start of the individual's employment.
10 (9) If an individual transfers or is moved into a role
11 described in subsection (1), that individual must complete the
12 professional learning required under this section by not later than
13 3 years following the start of the individual's employment in that
14 role.
15 (10) Before implementation of the professional learning
16 required under this section, and subject to subsection (11), a
17 school district, intermediate school district, or public school
18 academy shall create and post on its website a plan that outlines
19 the timeline and manner in which the required professional learning
20 will be completed, including, but not limited to, time expectations
21 beyond contractual work time and how time will be compensated.
22 (11) A school district, intermediate school district, or
23 public school academy shall consult and collaborate with the
24 individuals described in subsection (1) regarding the plan
25 described in subsection (10).
26 (12) The provisions of this section are subject to 1947 PA
27 336, MCL 423.201 to 423.217.
28 (13) This section does not apply to an individual with only an
29 interim teaching certificate under section 1531i.

3 section 1527.
4 (15) As used in this section, "multi-tiered system of
5 supports" means a comprehensive framework that includes 3 distinct
6 tiers of instructional support and is composed of a collection of
7 evidence-based strategies designed to meet the individual needs and
8 assets of a whole pupil at all achievement levels.
