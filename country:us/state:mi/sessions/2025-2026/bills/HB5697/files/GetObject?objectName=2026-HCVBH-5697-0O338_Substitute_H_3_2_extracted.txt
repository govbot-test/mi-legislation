Section 1:
SUBSTITUTE FOR
A bill to amend 1976 PA 451, entitled
"The revised school code,"
(MCL 380.1 to 380.1852) by adding section 1531k.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF MICHIGAN ENACT:
1 Sec. 1531k. (1) Beginning with the 2031-2032 school year, the
2 board of a school district or intermediate school district or board
3 of directors of a public school academy shall ensure that the
4 following individuals have successfully completed, as determined by
5 the department, the professional learning described in subsection
6 (2):
7 (a) Except as otherwise provided in this section, certificated
8 individuals who provide, support, or oversee instruction in grades
9 K to 5 that involves reading instruction or language arts,
CRM H02527'25 (H-3) 3jfxg5
2
1 including classroom teachers and teachers with any special
2 education endorsements.
3 (b) All personnel responsible for the delivery, support, or
4 direct supervision of literacy instruction in grades K to 5, as
5 determined by the department.
6 (2) By not later than 90 days after the effective date of the
7 amendatory act that added this section, the department shall
8 annually approve providers to offer the professional learning
9 required under subsection (1). The professional learning described
10 in this subsection must meet at least all of the following
11 requirements:
12 (a) Be offered through a system of training that provides
13 educators with the knowledge base to effectively implement any
14 class-wide, supplemental, or intervention reading approach and to
15 determine why some students struggle with reading, writing,
16 spelling, and language.
17 (b) Provide training activities that direct educators to
18 implement effective reading and spelling instruction supported by
19 scientifically based research and foster a direct explicit
20 instructional sequence that uses techniques to support teachers'
21 independence in using their newly learned skills with students in
22 the classroom.
23 (c) Include integrated components for educators and
24 administrators in grades K to 5 with embedded evaluation or
25 assessment of knowledge. Evaluation or assessment of knowledge
26 under this subdivision must incorporate evaluations of learning
27 throughout each unit and include a summative assessment that must
28 be completed to demonstrate successful course completion.
29 (d) Build teacher content knowledge and pedagogical knowledge

3
1 of the critical components of literacy, including how the brain
2 learns to read, phonological and phonemic awareness, letter
3 knowledge, phonics, advanced phonics, vocabulary and oral language,
4 fluency, comprehension, spelling and writing, and the organization
5 of language.
6 (e) Support educators in understanding how to effectively use
7 screening, progress monitoring, and diagnostic assessment data to
8 improve literacy outcomes through prevention and intervention for
9 reading difficulties in a multi-tiered system of supports. The
10 multi-tiered system of supports must include at least all of the
11 following essential components:
12 (i) Team-based leadership.
13 (ii) A tiered delivery system.
14 (iii) Selection and implementation of instruction,
15 interventions, and supports.
16 (iv) A comprehensive screening and assessment system.
17 (v) Continuous data-based decision making.
18 (f) Provide that all training, record-keeping systems, and
19 flexible scheduling options are administered by the professional
20 development provider.
21 (g) Include "train the trainer" facilitator professional
22 learning, to be implemented at the discretion of the department.
23 Participants enrolled in facilitator professional learning must
24 have demonstrated successful completion of the required
25 professional learning under subdivisions (a) to (e).
26 (h) Maintain capacity to provide all required training,
27 distribute materials, provide reports to the department, and
28 actively support all participants to mastery completion of
29 coursework.

1 (3) Beginning in the 2026-2027 school year, each school
2 district, intermediate school district, or public school academy
3 shall annually report to the department, in a form and manner
4 prescribed by the department, the number and percentage of
5 individuals described in subsection (1) who have completed the
6 required professional learning under this section. The department
7 shall compile and publish an annual report summarizing district-
8 level implementation, identifying areas of progress and need, and
9 recommending policy or funding adjustments as necessary to support
10 full compliance by the 2031-2032 school year. The department shall
11 submit this report to the governor, state board of education, the
12 house and senate appropriations subcommittees on school aid, and
13 the house and senate education committees, and shall make the
14 report publicly available on the department's website.
15 (4) Beginning in the 2026-2027 school year, the professional
16 learning providers identified in subsection (2) shall annually
17 report all of the following information to the department, in a
18 form and manner prescribed by the department:
19 (a) The total number of individuals described in subsection
20 (1) who have completed the required professional learning,
21 organized by role group.
22 (b) The total number of individuals described in subsection
23 (1) who are enrolled in the required professional development
24 training, organized by role group.
25 (c) The total number of individuals described in subsection
26 (1) who enrolled but withdrew from the required professional
27 development prior to completion, organized by role group.
28 (d) The cost per individual described in subsection (1) to
29 enroll in the required professional learning.

5
1 (e) The total grant funds expended for all individuals who
2 completed or enrolled in the professional learning during the
3 fiscal year.
4 (f) Total unused grant funds for the fiscal year.
5 (5) The department shall begin a phased implementation of the
6 training required under this section beginning in the 2026-2027
7 school year, and, subject to subsection (6), shall ensure full
8 compliance with this section by not later than the start of the
9 2031-2032 school year. The department may issue a good cause
10 extension for compliance with this section to an individual. A good
11 cause extension issued under this subsection must include a
12 deadline for when the individual must successfully complete the
13 professional learning required under this section.
14 (6) The department may consult with providers approved under
15 subsection (2) and make adjustments to the implementation schedule
16 under subsection (5) as the department determines necessary.
17 Adjustments made under this subsection may include, but are not
18 limited to, a 1-year extension for full compliance with this
19 section.
20 (7) The requirements of this section are subject to the
21 appropriation of an amount sufficient to comply with this section.
22 (8) The board of a school district or intermediate school
23 district or board of directors of a public school academy shall
24 ensure that an individual hired during or after the 2031-2032
25 school year into a role described in subsection (1) receives the
26 professional learning required under this section not later than 3
27 years following the start of the individual's employment.
28 (9) If an individual transfers or is moved into a role
29 described in subsection (1), that individual must complete the

6
1 professional learning required under this section by not later than
2 3 years following the start of the individual's employment in that
3 role.
4 (10) This section does not apply to an individual with only an
5 interim teaching certificate under section 1531i.
6 (11) The professional learning required under this section may
7 be provided as part of the professional development provided under
8 section 1527.
9 (12) As used in this section, "multi-tiered system of
10 supports" means a comprehensive framework that includes 3 distinct
11 tiers of instructional support and is composed of a collection of
12 evidence-based strategies designed to meet the individual needs and
13 assets of a whole pupil at all achievement levels.
