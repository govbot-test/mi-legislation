HOUSE BILL NO. 4295

A bill to allow certain employers to obtain a fair paycheck workplace certificate; to provide for the powers and duties of certain state governmental officers and entities; to require the promulgation of rules; to prohibit employers that do not possess a fair paycheck workplace certificate from claiming to possess a fair paycheck workplace certificate; and to prescribe civil sanctions.

the people of the state of michigan enact:

Sec. 1. This act may be cited as the "fair paycheck workplace certificate act".

Sec. 3. As used in this act:

(a) "Compensation" means all money paid to an employee for services performed by the employee, including commissions, bonuses, and tips. Compensation does not include the value of any of the following:

(i) A health, welfare, or retirement benefit.

(ii) Paid leave.

(iii) Separation or holiday pay.

(iv) Any other fringe benefit.

(b) "Department" means the department of labor and economic opportunity.

(c) "Director" means the director of the department or the director's designee.

(d) "Employee" means an individual who performs a service for wages or other remuneration for an employer.

(e) "Employer" means a person that employs 1 or more employees.

(f) "Fair paycheck workplace certificate" or "certificate" means a certificate issued by the director under section 5.

(g) "Person" means an individual or a partnership, corporation, limited liability company, association, governmental entity, or other legal entity.

(h) "Protected attribute or characteristic " means the religion, race, color, national origin, age, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity or expression, height, weight, familial status, marital status, or any other attribute or characteristic of an individual that is protected under the Elliot-Larsen civil rights act, 1976 PA 453, MCL 37.2101 to 37.2804.

Sec. 5. (1) To obtain a fair paycheck workplace certificate, an employer must submit a signed application and the application fee to the director in accordance with this section and the rules promulgated under section 11. An employer shall include all of the following information in the employer's application:

(a) The name and address of the employer.

(b) The total number of employees employed by the employer.

(c) If known by the employer, the protected attributes or characteristics of each of the employer's employees.

(d) Payroll records that show the gross amount of compensation paid to each of the employer's employees during the calendar year immediately preceding the year in which the employer submits the employer's application.

(e) Any other information that is necessary to determine whether to issue a certificate, as determined by the director.

(2) Not later than 15 days after the director receives an employer's application and application fee, the director shall issue a certificate to an employer if both of the following conditions are met:

(a) The employer includes all of the information listed in subsection (1) in the employer's application.

(b) The director determines, based on the information included in the employer's application, that less than a 5% difference exists between the average gross compensation the employer paid to employees with different protected attributes or characteristics.

(3) If the director rejects the employer's application, the director shall issue a statement to the employer that explains the reason for the rejection.

(4) An initial certificate expires 6 months after the date it is issued. A certificate other than an initial certificate expires 1 year after the date it is issued. An employer may renew a certificate by submitting a signed application and the application fee to the director in accordance with this section and the rules promulgated under section 11.

(5) Information about an individual that an employer provides to the director for the purpose of obtaining a certificate is exempt from disclosure under the freedom of information act, 1976 PA 442, MCL 15.231 to 15.246.

Sec. 7. (1) An employer shall not claim to possess a fair paycheck workplace certificate or otherwise indicate that the employer possesses a fair paycheck workplace certificate unless the employer possesses an unexpired fair paycheck workplace certificate.

(2) An employer that violates this section is subject to a civil fine of not more than $1,000.00. The prosecutor of the county in which the violation occurred or the attorney general may bring an action to collect the fine. A fine collected must be deposited in the general fund of this state.

Sec. 9. The director shall establish and maintain a database of employers that have been issued a fair paycheck workplace certificate under this act. The director shall include for each employer the employer's name and address and whether the employer's certificate is current or expired. The director shall post the database on a website that is accessible by the public at no cost.

Sec. 11. (1) A determination made under this act or an appeal of a determination made under this act must be made in accordance with the administrative procedures act of 1969, 1969 PA 306, MCL 24.201 to 24.328.

(2) The director shall promulgate rules to implement this act under the administrative procedures act of 1969, 1969 PA 306, MCL 24.201 to 24.328. The rules promulgated by the director must include, at a minimum, both of the following:

(a) The manner in which an application must be submitted under section 5.

(b) The application fee. The application fee must not be more than the amount that is necessary to administer this act, as determined by the director.
