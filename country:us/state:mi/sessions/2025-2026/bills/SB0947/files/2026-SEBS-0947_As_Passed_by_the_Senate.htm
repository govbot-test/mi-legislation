Sec. 31503. (1) "Emergency action plan" means a plan developed by the owner that establishes procedures for notification of the department, public off-site authorities, and other agencies of the emergency actions to be taken prior to before, during, and following after an impending or actual failure of a dam.

(2) "Enlargement" means any change in or addition to an existing dam which that raises or may raise the design flood elevation of the water impounded by the dam.

(3) "Failed dam" means a dam not capable of impounding water at its intended level due to a structural deficiency.

(4) "Failure" means an incident resulting in an unplanned or uncontrolled release of water from a dam.

(5) "Flood of record" means the greatest flow rate determined by the department to have occurred at a particular location.

(6) "Freeboard" means the vertical distance between the design flood elevation and the lowest point of the top of the dam.

(7) "Half probable maximum flood" means the largest flood that may reasonably occur over a watershed , and is derived from the combination of hydrologic runoff parameters and the half probable maximum storm precipitation that produces the maximum runoff.

(8) "Half probable maximum storm" precipitation" means the spatial and temporal distribution of the probable maximum precipitation, divided by 2, that produces the maximum volume of precipitation over a watershed.

(9) "Hazard potential classification" means a reference to the potential for loss of life, property damage, and environmental damage in the area downstream of a dam in the event of failure of the dam or appurtenant works.

(10) "Height" means the difference in elevation measured vertically between the natural bed of a stream or watercourse at the downstream toe of the dam, or, if it is not across a stream channel or watercourse, from the lowest elevation of the downstream toe of the dam, to the design flood elevation or to the lowest point of the top of the dam, whichever is less.

(11) "High hazard potential dam" means a dam located in an area where a failure may cause serious damage to inhabited homes, agricultural buildings, campgrounds, recreational facilities, industrial or commercial buildings, public utilities , main highways, or in areas with a population density greater than or equal to 5,000 people per square mile, interstate roads, other freeway roads, other principal arterial roads as defined in the National Functional Classification (NFC) system, class I carrier railroads, or where environmental degradation or natural resource damage would be significant, or where danger to individuals exists with the potential for loss of life.

Sec. 31504. (1) "Impoundment" means the water held back by a dam.

(2) "Inflow design flood" means the flood hydrograph entering the reservoir that is used to design or modify a specific dam and its appurtenant works, including, but not limited to, sizing the spillway and outlet works and evaluating the maximum storage, height of the dam, and freeboard requirements.

(3) (2) "Low hazard potential dam" means a dam located in an area where failure may cause damage limited to agriculture, uninhabited buildings, structures, or township or county minor collector roads, local roads, or nonrated roadways as defined in the National Functional Classification (NFC) system, where environmental degradation would be minimal, and where danger to individuals is slight or nonexistent.

(4) (3) "Maintenance" means the upkeep of a dam and its impoundment and appurtenant works but does not include alterations or repairs.

(5) (4) "One-hundred year flood" means a flood that has a 1% chance of being equaled or exceeded in any given year.

(6) (5) "Owner" means a person who that owns, leases, controls, operates, maintains, manages, or proposes to construct a dam.

(7) "Probable maximum flood" means the largest flood that may reasonably occur over a watershed and is derived from the combination of hydrologic runoff parameters and the probable maximum precipitation that produces the maximum runoff.

(8) (6) "Probable maximum precipitation" means the theoretically greatest depth of precipitation for a given duration that is physically possible over a given size storm area at a particular geographic location at a certain time of year.

Sec. 31505. (1) "Removal" means the physical elimination of a dam or impoundment.

(2) "Repair" means to substantially restore a dam to its original condition and includes only such restoration as that may directly affect the structural integrity of the dam.

(3) "Riparian owner" means a person who that has riparian rights.

(4) "Riparian rights" means rights which that accrue by operation of law to a landowner on the banks of an inland lake or stream.

(5) "Significant hazard potential dam" means a dam located in an area where its failure may cause damage limited to isolated inhabited homes, agricultural buildings, structures, secondary highways, short line or minor arterial roads or major collector roads as defined in the National Functional Classification (NFC) system, railroads not classified as class I, or public utilities in areas with a population density less than 5,000 people per square mile, where environmental degradation may be significant, or where danger to individuals exists.

(6) "Spillway" means a waterway in or about a dam designed for the discharge of water.

(7) "Spillway capacity" means the maximum rate of discharge that will pass through a spillway at design flood elevation.

(8) "Two-hundred year flood" means a flood that has a 0.5% chance of being equaled or exceeded in any given year.

Sec. 31518a. (1) For high hazard potential and significant hazard potential dams, an owner shall submit to the department a comprehensive evaluation report prepared by a licensed professional engineer that evaluates the overall safety of the dam. For low hazard potential dams, the department may require an owner to submit a comprehensive evaluation report as provided under this subsection. The comprehensive evaluation report must be submitted not less than once every 10 years on a schedule determined by the department.

(2) The comprehensive evaluation report must include, but is not limited to, a detailed evaluation and recommendation for all of the following:

(a) The dam's condition, spillway capacity, operational adequacy, and structural integrity.

(b) The adequacy of the dam's original design and construction.

(c) The adequacy of the dam's operation and maintenance procedures.

(d) Probable failure modes and risk reduction measures.

(e) Conformance with current dam safety practices.

(f) Any deficiencies that exist that could lead to failure of the dam.

(g) Maintenance, repair, and alterations of a dam that are necessary to eliminate the deficiencies described under subdivision (f).

Sec. 31518b. (1) An owner shall prepare, and keep current, a surveillance and monitoring plan and submit the plan to the department for review. The surveillance and monitoring plan must be consistent with standard engineering practices.

(2) A surveillance and monitoring plan must include, but is not limited to, all of the following information:

(a) The name, address, and telephone number of the owner, and any agent or employee of the owner responsible for the operation of the dam.

(b) Information necessary to evaluate probable failure modes of the dam.

(c) An assessment of current and past surveillance and monitoring data.

(d) Recommendations for any necessary analyses or remedial actions.

(3) The owner shall notify the department of any concerning findings of surveillance and monitoring data within 24 hours.

Sec. 31518c. An owner shall prepare, and keep current, an operation and maintenance plan and submit the plan to the department for review. An operation and maintenance plan must include all of the following information:

(a) A description of the dam.

(b) Contact information for the dam owner and any agent or employee of the owner that is necessary to the operation of the dam and each individual's responsibilities.

(c) An inspection schedule and checklist for routine dam safety inspections conducted by the owner.

(d) A description of normal and emergency dam operation procedures.

(e) A log of normal and emergency dam operation procedures.

(f) A description of normal and emergency dam maintenance procedures.

(g) A log of normal and emergency dam maintenance procedures.

(h) Contact information and notification procedures for upstream and downstream owners, if applicable.

Sec. 31518d. (1) An owner shall prepare and keep current a safety and security plan and submit the plan to the department for review. A safety and security plan must include all of the following information:

(a) A summary of all physical safety measures at the dam, including any signage, waterway barriers, safety fencing, railings, or other safety devices.

(b) A summary of all physical security measures at the dam, including security cameras, alarms, security fencing, gates, doors, barriers, or other security devices.

(c) A response plan for any safety and security issues at the dam, which must include, at a minimum, all of the following:

(i) Dam personnel detection and reporting procedures.

(ii) Notification flow charts and call lists.

(iii) Response procedures.

(iv) A safety and security incident report template.

(2) The owner shall notify the department of any safety or security incidents within 24 hours after the incident occurs.

Sec. 31520. (1) The owner or his or her agent shall advise the department and the affected off-site public authorities and safety agencies of any sudden or unprecedented flood or unusual or alarming circumstance or occurrence, whether existing or anticipated, that may affect the safety of the dam within 24 hours of after the flood, circumstance, or occurrence.

(2) The owner shall notify the department as soon as possible of any necessary emergency drawdowns, repairs, breaching, or other action being taken in response to an emergency condition.

Sec. 31528a. (1) The dam safety emergency fund is created within the state treasury. The state treasurer may receive money or other assets from any source for deposit into the fund. The state treasurer shall direct the investment of the fund and credit to the fund interest and earnings from fund investments. Money in the fund at the end of the fiscal year remains in the fund and does not lapse to the general fund. The department is the administrator of the fund for auditing purposes.

(2) On appropriation, the department shall expend money from the fund to take remedial or corrective actions that remove or reduce significant safety risks posed by dams when an owner is unable or unwilling to take remedial or corrective action. Except for the 5 years after a dam has come under the ownership of local government by means of tax foreclosure, the department and the department of attorney general may recover costs expended from the fund under this subsection from an owner. Recovered costs must be deposited in the fund.

(3) As used in this section, "fund" means the dam safety emergency fund created under subsection (1).

Sec. 31528b. (1) The department shall establish a dam risk reduction grant program as provided in this section.

(2) The program must provide grants to owners to complete any of the following projects:

(a) Repair or alteration of a dam.

(b) Removal of a dam.

(c) Scoping and engineering plans associated with any of the actions described in subdivisions (a) and (b).

(3) An owner shall apply to the program on a form and in a manner prescribed by the department. To be eligible to receive a grant from the program, the owner must be able to contribute matching funds of at least 10%.

(4) As used in this section, "program" means the dam risk reduction grant program created under subsection (1).
