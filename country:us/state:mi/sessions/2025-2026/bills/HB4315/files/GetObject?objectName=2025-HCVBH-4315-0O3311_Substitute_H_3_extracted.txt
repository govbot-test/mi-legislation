Section 1:
SUBSTITUTE FOR
A bill to amend 1976 PA 451, entitled
"The revised school code,"
(MCL 380.1 to 380.1852) by adding section 1308f.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF MICHIGAN ENACT:
1 Sec. 1308f. (1) The department of state police and the office
2 of school safety within the department of state police, in
3 coordination with the department and the department of attorney
4 general, shall develop school safety and security training
5 materials and best practices and provide annual training resources
6 on all of the following topics, as applicable:
7 (a) For school resource officers, both of the following:
8 (i) Position-specific training that is in addition to any
9 training received by the school resource officer to become a law

1 enforcement officer. The materials, best practices, and training
2 resources on the topics described in this subparagraph must be
3 developed by the department of state police and the office of
4 school safety within the department of state police.
5 (ii) Coverage of legal updates to aid in an officer's
6 understanding of the limits and scope of the officer's authority in
7 the school environment. The materials, best practices, and training
8 resources on the topics described in this subparagraph must be
9 developed by the department of attorney general.
10 (b) For members of a crisis response team described in section
11 1308b, all of the following developed by the department of state
12 police and the office of school safety within the department of
13 state police:
14 (i) Target hardening.
15 (ii) Familiarity with law enforcement response to school
16 emergencies.
17 (iii) Incident command systems.
18 (c) For all school personnel of a public school or, subject to
19 subsection (3), a nonpublic school who have regular contact with
20 pupils, all of the following:
21 (i) Mandatory threat reporting protocols, including how to
22 report concerns, what is appropriate to report, and warning signs
23 that may indicate that someone is at risk for potential harm to
24 themselves or others. The materials, best practices, and training
25 resources on the topics described in this subparagraph must be
26 developed by the department.
27 (ii) The confidential safety tip line process administered by
28 the OK2Say program or a successor state program. As used in this
29 subparagraph, "OK2Say program" means this state's student safety

1 program that allows students to confidentially report tips on
2 potential harm or criminal activities directed at students, school
3 employees, and schools. The materials, best practices, and training
4 resources on the topics described in this subparagraph must be
5 developed by the department of state police and the office of
6 school safety within the department of state police.
7 (iii) Emergency operations plans and emergency response
8 procedures. The materials, best practices, and training resources
9 on the topics described in this subparagraph must be developed by
10 the department of state police and the office of school safety
11 within the department of state police.
12 (d) For school safety and security personnel, the legal
13 authority and limitations of security personnel who are not
14 licensed under the Michigan commission on law enforcement standards
15 act, 1965 PA 203, MCL 28.601 to 28.615. The materials, best
16 practices, and training resources on the topics described in this
17 subdivision must be developed by the department of attorney
18 general.
19 (2) Any materials, best practices, and training resources
20 developed under subsection (1) must be provided to the department
21 of state police and the office of school safety within the
22 department of state police. The office of school safety within the
23 department of state police shall make all materials, best
24 practices, and training resources it receives publicly available on
25 its website.
26 (3) The governing body of a nonpublic school may elect to
27 exempt the nonpublic school from the requirements of this section.
