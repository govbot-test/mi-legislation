Sec. 36a. (1) Beginning on the effective date of the amendatory act that added this section, except as otherwise provided in this section, a foreign government or state-sponsored enterprise, or an individual operating on behalf of a foreign government or state-sponsored enterprise, shall not purchase or acquire farmland in this state. A foreign government or state-sponsored enterprise, or an individual operating on behalf of a foreign government or state-sponsored enterprise, that owns or holds farmland in this state before October 1, 2023 may continue to own or hold the farmland but shall not purchase or otherwise acquire additional farmland in this state.

(2) Subsection (1) does not apply to acquisitions or purchases of farmland in this state under a treaty of the United States.

(3) As used in this section:

(a) "Controlling interest" means direct or indirect ownership of 50% or more of an enterprise, company, or corporate entity.

(b) "Farmland" means land zoned for agricultural use and capable of any of the following:

(i) Creating agricultural crops.

(ii) Sustaining any of the following:

(A) Livestock or livestock products.

(B) Poultry or poultry products.

(C) Timber.

(D) Dairy or dairy products.

(E) Orchard or fruit productions.

(F) Ranching.

(G) Hemp production.

(c) "Foreign government" means a government, state, or state-controlled organization other than any of the following:

(i) The government of the United States.

(ii) The government of this state.

(iii) A local government of this state.

(iv) A local, state, or territorial government of a state or territory in the United States that is not this state.

(d) "Primary interest" means an interest in a company, enterprise, or corporate entity that serves the geopolitical aims of a foreign government or invests in a belligerent interest against the United States.

(e) "State-sponsored enterprise" means any enterprise, company, or corporate entity in which a foreign government has a primary interest or controlling interest.
