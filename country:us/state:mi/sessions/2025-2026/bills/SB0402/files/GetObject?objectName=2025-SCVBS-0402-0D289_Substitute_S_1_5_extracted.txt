SUBSTITUTE FOR
A bill to amend 1939 PA 280, entitled
"The social welfare act,"
by amending section 109 (MCL 400.109), as amended by 2024 PA 248.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF MICHIGAN ENACT:

2 medical services under this act:
3 (a) Hospital services that an eligible individual may receive
4 consist of medical, surgical, or obstetrical care, together with
5 necessary drugs, X-rays, physical therapy, prosthesis,
6 transportation, and nursing care incident to the medical, surgical,
7 or obstetrical care. The period of inpatient hospital service shall
8 must be the minimum period necessary in this type of facility for
9 the proper care and treatment of the individual. Necessary

5 service under this section, notwithstanding the mental health code,
6 1974 PA 258, MCL 330.1001 to 330.2106. The department must pay for
7 hospital services according to the state plan for medical
8 assistance adopted under section 10 and approved by the United
9 States Department of Health and Human Services.
10 (b) Physicians Physician services authorized by the
11 department. The services may be furnished in the physician's
12 office, the eligible individual's home, a medical institution, or
13 elsewhere in case of emergency. A physician must be paid a
14 reasonable charge for the service rendered. The department must
15 determine reasonable charges. Reasonable charges must not be more
16 than those paid in this state for services rendered under title
17 XVIII.

19 medical care facility, or other facility or identifiable unit of

21 established standards for a nursing home under the laws and rules

23 Services, to the extent found necessary by the attending physician,
24 dentist, or certified Christian Science practitioner. An eligible
25 individual may receive nursing home services in an extended care

4 must be determined according to the state plan for medical

8 facilities the "nursing class variable cost limit" must be

7 (g) Screening, laboratory services, diagnostic services, early

20 MCL 333.9137.
21 (j) Street medicine services, including prescriptions for
22 opioid use disorder by an eligible provider. As used in this
23 subdivision:
24 (i) "Eligible provider" means a nurse practitioner, licensed
25 physician's assistant, licensed physician, or peer recovery coach.
26 (ii) "Nurse practitioner" means an individual who is licensed
27 as a registered professional nurse under part 172 of the public
28 health code, 1978 PA 368, MCL 333.17201 to 333.17242, who has been
29 granted a specialty certification as a nurse practitioner by the

1 Michigan board of nursing under section 17210 of the public health
2 code, 1978 PA 368, MCL 333.17210.
3 (iii) "Peer recovery coach" means an individual who has lived
4 experience of recovery, has received training and supervision to
5 assist other individuals in initiating and maintaining recovery,
6 and is either of the following:
7 (A) A certified peer recovery mentor certified by the Michigan
8 certification board for addiction professionals.
9 (B) A certified peer recovery coach certified by the
10 department.
11 (iv) "Street medicine services" means health and social care
12 provided directly to a homeless individual in the individual's
13 environment.
14 (2) The director must provide notice to the public, according
15 to applicable federal regulations, and must obtain the approval of
16 the committees on appropriations of the house of representatives
17 and senate of the state legislature, of a proposed change in the
18 statewide method or level of reimbursement for a service, if the
19 proposed change is expected to increase or decrease payments for
20 that service by 1% or more during the 12 months after the effective
21 date of the change.
22 (3) As used in this act:
23 (a) "Title XVIII" means title XVIII of the social security
24 act, 42 USC 1395 to 1395lll.
25 (b) "Title XIX" means title XIX of the social security act, 42
26 USC 1396 to 1396w-7.1396w-8.
27 (c) "Title XX" means title XX of the social security act, 42
28 USC 1397 to 1397n-13.
