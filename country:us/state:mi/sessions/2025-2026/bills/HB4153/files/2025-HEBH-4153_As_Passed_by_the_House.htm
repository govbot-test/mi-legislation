Sec. 1531l. (1) The board of a school district or intermediate school district or board of directors of a public school academy may issue a local teaching certificate to an individual under this section. If the board of a school district or intermediate school district or board of directors of a public school academy elects to issue local teaching certificates under this section, the board of the school district or intermediate school district or board of directors of a public school academy must establish requirements that an individual must meet to be issued a local teaching certificate.

(2) Subject to federal law, the superintendent of public instruction shall establish a process for the board of a school district or intermediate school district or board of directors of a public school academy to grant an individual a local teaching certificate. The process must not allow for the granting of a local teaching certificate under this section for teaching special education.

(3) The process established under subsection (2) must provide that if a board of a school district or intermediate school district or a board of directors of a public school academy elects to issue a local teaching certificate, the board of the school district or intermediate school district or board of directors of the public school academy will, subject to section 1809, grant a local teaching certificate to an individual who meets at least all of the following:

(a) Holds a bachelor's, master's, doctorate, or professional degree in the subject area that the individual will teach from a regionally accredited college or university.

(b) Received a grade point average of at least 3.0 on a 4.0 scale or the equivalent on another scale, as determined by the board of the school district or board of directors of the public school academy, upon earning the degree required under subdivision (a). If the individual has earned more than 1 degree required under subdivision (a), the highest grade point average earned by the individual may be used in determining the grade point average under this subdivision.

(c) Any requirements established by the board of the school district or intermediate school district or board of directors of the public school academy.

(4) A local teaching certificate issued under this section is valid for not more than 5 years from the initial date of issuance. To continue serving as a teacher in a public school after the expiration of a local teaching certificate under this subsection, an individual must obtain a teaching certificate issued by the superintendent of public instruction under section 1531 or an interim teaching certificate under section 1531i. This subsection does not apply to an individual who is serving as a teacher for an industrial technology career program or a career and technical education program.

(5) A local teaching certificate may not be issued under this section after July 1, 2035.

(6) As used in this section, "local teaching certificate" means a teaching certificate issued by a school district, intermediate school district, or public school academy that authorizes an individual to teach a specific subject area only in a school that is operated by the school district, intermediate school district, or public school academy that issued the teaching certificate.

Sec. 1809.
