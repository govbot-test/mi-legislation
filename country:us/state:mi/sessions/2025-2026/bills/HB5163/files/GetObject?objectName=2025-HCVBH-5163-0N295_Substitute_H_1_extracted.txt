SUBSTITUTE FOR
A bill to amend 1975 PA 238, entitled
"Child protection law,"
by amending section 2 (MCL 722.622), as amended by 2022 PA 67.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF MICHIGAN ENACT:
1 Sec. 2. As used in this act:
2 (a) "Adult foster care location authorized to care for a
3 child" means an adult foster care family home or adult foster care
4 small group home as those terms are defined in section 3 of the
5 adult foster care facility licensing act, 1979 PA 218, MCL 400.703,
6 in which a child is placed in accordance with section 5 of 1973 PA
7 116, MCL 722.115.
8 (b) "Attorney" means, if appointed to represent a child under
9 the provisions referenced in section 10, an attorney serving as the

4 (c) "Central registry" means a repository of names of
5 individuals who are identified as perpetrators related to a central
6 registry case in the department's statewide electronic case
7 management system.
8 (d) "Central registry case" means the department confirmed
9 that a person responsible for the child's health or welfare
10 committed serious abuse or neglect, sexual abuse, or sexual
11 exploitation of a child, or allowed a child to be exposed to or
12 have contact with methamphetamine production.
13 (e) "Centralized intake" means the department's statewide
14 centralized processing center for reports of suspected child abuse
15 and child neglect.
16 (f) "Child" means an individual under who is less than 18
17 years of age.
18 (g) "Child abuse" means harm or threatened harm to a child's
19 health or welfare that occurs through nonaccidental physical or
20 mental injury, sexual abuse, sexual exploitation, or maltreatment,
21 by a parent, a legal guardian, any other person responsible for the
22 child's health or welfare, a teacher, a teacher's aide, a member of
23 the clergy, or an individual 18 years of age or older who is
24 involved with a youth program.
25 (h) "Child care organization" means that term as defined in
26 section 1 of 1973 PA 116, MCL 722.111.
27 (i) "Child care provider" means an owner, operator, employee,
28 or volunteer of a child care organization or of an adult foster
29 care location authorized to care for a child.

3 child welfare licensing, the department of lifelong education,
4 advancement, and potential, or a successor state department that is
5 responsible for the licensing or registration of child care
6 organizations or the licensing of adult foster care locations
7 authorized to care for a child.
8 (k) "Child neglect" means harm or threatened harm to a child's
9 health or welfare by a parent, legal guardian, or any other person
10 responsible for the child's health or welfare that occurs through
11 either of the following:
12 (i) Negligent negligent treatment, including the failure to
13 provide adequate food, clothing, shelter, or medical care, though
14 financially able to do so, or by the failure to seek financial or
15 other reasonable means to provide adequate food, clothing, shelter,
16 or medical care, .or
17 (ii) Placing placing a child at an unreasonable risk to the
18 child's health or welfare by failure of the parent, legal guardian,
19 or other person responsible for the child's health or welfare to
20 intervene to eliminate that risk when that person is able to do so
21 and has, or should have, knowledge of the risk. Child neglect does
22 not include when a parent, legal guardian, or other person
23 responsible for the child's health or welfare refused a recommended
24 treatment plan for a child by a health professional because that
25 parent, legal guardian, or other person responsible for the child's
26 health or welfare is actively seeking a second opinion from,
27 followed the recommendation that treatment is not needed from, or
28 following another treatment plan prescribed by another health
29 professional.
