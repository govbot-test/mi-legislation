HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 309

Reps. Smit, Wortz, Price, Mentzer, Miller and Outman offered the following resolution:

A resolution to declare May 2026 as ALS Awareness Month in the state of Michigan.

Whereas, Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS), known by many as Lou Gehrigï¿½s disease, is a progressive fatal neurodegenerative disease in which a personï¿½s brain loses connection with the muscles, slowly taking away their ability to walk, talk, eat, and eventually breathe; and

Whereas, Every 90 minutes someone is diagnosed with ALS and someone passes away from ALS; and

Whereas, On average, patients diagnosed with ALS only survive two to five years from the time of diagnosis; and

Whereas, ALS has no cure; and

Whereas, People who have served in the military are more likely to develop ALS and die from the disease than those with no history of military service; and

Whereas, Securing access to new therapies, durable medical equipment, and communication technologies is of vital importance to people living with ALS; and

Whereas, Clinical trials play a pivotal role in evaluating new treatments, enhancing quality of life, and fostering assistive technologies for those living with ALS; and

Whereas, We celebrate the 10th Anniversary of the Ice Bucket Challenge through a renewed commitment to galvanize public awareness and support funding leading to significant investments in ALS research; and

Whereas, The ALS Association, as the largest philanthropic funder of ALS research globally, has committed over $154 million to support more than 550 projects across the United States and eighteen other countries; and

Whereas, Our commitment to accelerating the pace of discovery remains unwavering, fueled by the hope that one day, ALS will be a livable disease for everyone, everywhere, until we can cure it; and

Whereas, ALS Awareness Month increases the public's awareness of people with ALSï¿½ dire circumstances, and acknowledges the terrible impact this disease has, not only on the person but on his or her family and the community, and recognizes the research being done to eradicate this disease; now, therefore, be it

Resolved by the House of Representatives, That the members of this legislative body declare May 2026 as ALS Awareness Month in the state of Michigan. We call upon all Michiganders to join in supporting those diagnosed with ALS, those caring for those with ALS, and the ongoing research efforts that aim to find a cure for those affected by this relentless disease, advocating for increased funding, and standing in solidarity with those affected by this relentless disease.
