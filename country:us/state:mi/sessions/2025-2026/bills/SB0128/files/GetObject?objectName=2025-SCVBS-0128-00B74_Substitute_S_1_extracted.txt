Section 1:
SUBSTITUTE FOR
A bill to create a Michigan-Ireland trade commission within
the Michigan economic development corporation and to prescribe its
powers and duties; to create a fund; and to prescribe the powers
and duties of certain state officers and entities.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF MICHIGAN ENACT:
1 Sec. 1. (1) The Michigan-Ireland trade commission is created
2 in the Michigan economic development corporation.
3 (2) The commission consists of the following members,
4 appointed by the governor:
5 (a) One representative of a public institution of higher
6 education in this state.
7 (b) One representative of the chamber of commerce.
8 (c) Three members who represent Irish-American communities or

1 the interests of Irish-American communities.
2 (d) One member from a list submitted by the senate majority
3 leader that includes at least 2 individuals who meet either of the
4 following criteria:
5 (i) Have knowledge of or have current or past involvement in
6 organizations that promote Irish affairs.
7 (ii) Have interest in the well-being of trade relations between
8 this state and Ireland.
9 (e) One member from a list submitted by the senate minority
10 leader that includes at least 2 individuals who meet either of the
11 following criteria:
12 (i) Have knowledge of or have current or past involvement in
13 organizations that promote Irish affairs.
14 (ii) Have interest in the well-being of trade relations between
15 this state and Ireland.
16 (f) One member from a list submitted by the speaker of the
17 house of representatives that includes at least 2 individuals who
18 meet either of the following criteria:
19 (i) Have knowledge of or have current or past involvement in
20 organizations that promote Irish affairs.
21 (ii) Have interest in the well-being of trade relations between
22 this state and Ireland.
23 (g) One member from a list submitted by the house minority
24 leader that includes at least 2 individuals who meet either of the
25 following criteria:
26 (i) Have knowledge of or have current or past involvement in
27 organizations that promote Irish affairs.
28 (ii) Have interest in the well-being of trade relations between
29 this state and Ireland.

1 (3) The governor shall appoint the first members of the
2 commission not later than 90 days after the effective date of this
3 act.
4 (4) The term of a member of the commission is 2 years or until
5 a successor is appointed under subsection (2), whichever is later.
6 (5) If a vacancy occurs on the commission, the governor shall
7 appoint an individual to fill the vacancy for the balance of the
8 term.
9 (6) The governor may remove a member of the commission for
10 incompetence, dereliction of duty, malfeasance, misfeasance, or
11 nonfeasance in office, or any other good cause.
12 (7) The governor shall call the first meeting of the
13 commission. At the first meeting, the commission shall elect a
14 member as a chairperson and may elect other officers that it
15 considers necessary or appropriate. The commission shall meet at
16 least quarterly, or more frequently at the call of the chairperson
17 or at the request of 5 or more members.
18 (8) A majority of the members of the commission constitute a
19 quorum for transacting business. A vote in favor by a majority of
20 the members of the commission serving is required for any action of
21 the commission.
22 (9) The commission shall conduct its business in compliance
23 with the open meetings act, 1976 PA 267, MCL 15.261 to 15.275.
24 (10) A writing that is prepared, owned, used, possessed, or
25 retained by the commission in performing an official function is
26 subject to the freedom of information act, 1976 PA 442, MCL 15.231
27 to 15.246.
28 (11) A member of the commission is not entitled to
29 compensation for service on the commission, but the commission may

4
1 reimburse a member for actual and necessary expenses incurred in
2 serving.
3 (12) The commission shall do all of the following:
4 (a) Advance bilateral trade and investment between this state
5 and Ireland.
6 (b) Initiate joint action on policy issues of mutual interest
7 to this state and Ireland.
8 (c) Promote business and academic exchanges between this state
9 and Ireland.
10 (d) Encourage mutual economic support between this state and
11 Ireland.
12 (e) Encourage mutual investment in the infrastructure of this
13 state and Ireland.
14 (f) Address any other issues the commission determines.
15 (13) The commission shall report any findings and results
16 collected in the immediately previous year and recommendations to
17 the governor and the legislature by February 1, 2027, and each
18 February 1 thereafter.
19 (14) The commission is authorized to raise funds, through
20 direct solicitation or other fund-raising events, alone or with
21 other groups, and accept gifts, grants, and bequests from
22 individuals, corporations, foundations, governmental agencies,
23 public and private organizations, and institutions, to minimize the
24 commission's administrative expenses and to carry out its purposes
25 as set forth in this act. The commission shall report on its
26 publicly accessible website the receipt and details of any gifts,
27 grants, or bequests described in this subsection.
28 (15) The Michigan-Ireland trade commission fund is created in
29 the state treasury. The state treasurer shall deposit funds, gifts,

5
1 grants, and bequests received under subsection (14) in the fund.
2 The state treasurer shall direct the investment of money in the
3 fund and credit interest and earnings from the investments to the
4 fund. The department of treasury is the administrator of the fund
5 for audits of the fund. Money in the fund at the close of the
6 fiscal year does not lapse to the general fund. The department of
7 treasury shall expend money from the fund on an annual basis upon
8 appropriation only to minimize the commission's administrative
9 expenses and to enable the commission to carry out its purposes as
10 set forth in this act.
11 (16) As used in this section:
12 (a) "Commission" means the Michigan-Ireland trade commission
13 created under subsection (1).
14 (b) "Fund" means the Michigan-Ireland trade commission fund
15 created in subsection (15).
