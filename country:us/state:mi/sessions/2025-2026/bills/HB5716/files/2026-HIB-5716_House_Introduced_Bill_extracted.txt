Section 1:
HOUSE BILL NO. 5716 A bill to amend 1972 PA 230, entitled "Stille-DeRossett-Hale single state construction code act," by amending section 19 (MCL 125.1519), as amended by 2002 PA 721. the people of the state of michigan enact: Sec. 19. (1) The department shall promulgate rules establishing a procedure by which a premanufactured unit intended for use in this state may be issued a certificate of acceptability by the department at its place of manufacture. (2) The procedure shall must require that the manufacturer submit an application to the department with detailed plans and specifications for the premanufactured unit for approval as in compliance with the code. The manufacturer, in accordance with subsection (5), may submit detailed plans and specifications that were reviewed by an independent entity approved by the commission under rules promulgated by the department. The department may require that the manufacturer submit test results on the premanufactured unit or its components, any material or information the department considers relevant, or 1 or more of the premanufactured units for testing and evaluation by the department. (3) The department shall approve or deny a manufacturer's application for a certificate of acceptability under subsection (2) not later than 30 days after the date that the manufacturer submits the application. If the department fails to approve or deny the application within the 30-day period, the application is considered to be approved and the department shall issue a certificate of acceptability to the manufacturer. (4) (3) Each The department, or a qualified person approved by the department, shall inspect each premanufactured unit shall be inspected by the department, or a qualified person approved by the department, to determine that if the premanufactured unit has been was manufactured in accordance with plans and specifications submitted under subsection (2). The department may issue a certificate of acceptability for a premanufactured unit that bears the approved label of an independent, nationally recognized body having follow-up inspection service satisfactory to the commission, certifying that the premanufactured unit complies with plans and specifications submitted under subsection (2). (5) (4) Plans and specifications for 1- and 2-family dwelling premanufactured units may be reviewed by the department or by an independent entity approved by the commission under rules promulgated by the department. The Subject to the 30-day period described in subsection (3), the department shall establish submission procedures for plans and specifications that were reviewed by an independent entity approved by the commission under subsection (2). (6) (5) A local enforcing agency may also inspect a premanufactured unit at its place of manufacture to determine that it has been if the premanufactured unit was manufactured in accordance with plans and specifications submitted under subsection (2). and The local enforcing agency shall advise the state inspector and the commission in writing of any deviations found. (7) (6) An approved independent entity shall not conduct in-plant inspections of units for which it performed plan reviews. However, the manufacturer may request a variance from the commission if the literal application of the requirements of this section would result in an exceptional, practical difficulty relating to inspection of specific units. For purposes of As used in this subsection, "exceptional, practical difficulty" includes, but is not limited to, a geographic distance between the manufacturing facility where the premanufactured units are manufactured and the primary business location of the independent entity that conducts in-plant inspections on behalf of the manufacturer of more than 250 miles and is located in another state. (8) (7) If an application for a building permit specifying use of a premanufactured unit with a certificate of acceptability is submitted to an enforcing agency, and if the application, except for the part calling for use of a premanufactured unit with a certificate of acceptability, complies with applicable construction regulations, zoning laws, and local ordinances, the enforcing agency shall issue the building permit within the time specified in this act. (9) (8) At the time of installation, a premanufactured unit with a certificate of acceptability is subject only to the nondestructive tests approved by the department necessary to determine that it has not been damaged in transit or installation, and that it has been installed in accordance with the building permit and construction regulations. (10) (9) The fees established for a building permit when the application specifies use of a premanufactured unit with a certificate of acceptability, or for inspection of the installation of the premanufactured unit, shall must bear a reasonable relation to the costs incurred by the enforcing agency in issuing a permit or performing an inspection. (11) (10) Notwithstanding any other provision of this section, an enforcing agency may object to use of a premanufactured unit with a certificate of acceptability on the basis that because the premanufactured unit does not comply with the code. If an enforcing agency on receipt of an application for a building permit specifying the use of a premanufactured unit does object, objects to use of the premanufactured unit, it may set forth its objections in writing to the department before issuance of a building permit and within not later than 10 business days after receipt of the application. Within Not later than 10 business days after receipt of the objections, the commission, or a panel of 3 or more members designated for that purpose by its chairman, chairperson, shall hold a hearing on the objections in accordance with rules promulgated by the department. After the hearing, the commission, or its panel, within not later than 3 business days after the hearing shall determine 1 of the following: (a) The premanufactured unit does not comply with the code and order that the certificate of acceptability be voided. (b) The premanufactured unit requires additional testing and evaluation in which case the testing and evaluation shall must be conducted in accordance with this section. (c) The objections are not valid and order the enforcing agency to issue the building permit within not later than 3 business days after the order. (12) (11) A certificate of acceptability issued by the department shall must not be used for advertising purposes.
