Sec. 20107a. (1) A person that currently owns or operates property that has information that indicates a property is a facility shall, not later than 90 days after obtaining the information or not later than 90 days after the effective date of the amendatory act that amended this subsection, whichever is later, report the facility to the department, on a form and in a manner prescribed by the department. If a facility is reported to the department in accordance with a law or program other than this part, or has been previously reported under this part, notification is not required under this section. A person who that owns or operates property that he or she the person has knowledge is a facility shall do all of the following with respect to hazardous substances at the facility owned or operated by the person:

(a) Undertake measures as are necessary to prevent exacerbation.

(b) Exercise due care by undertaking response activity necessary to mitigate unacceptable exposure to hazardous substances, mitigate fire and explosion hazards due to hazardous substances, and allow for the intended use of the facility in a manner that protects the public health and safety.

(c) Take reasonable precautions against the reasonably foreseeable acts or omissions of a third party and the consequences that foreseeably could result from those acts or omissions.

(d) Provide reasonable cooperation, assistance, and access to the persons that are authorized to conduct response activities at the facility, including the cooperation and access necessary for the installation, integrity, operation, and maintenance of any complete or partial response activity at the facility. Nothing in this subdivision shall be is interpreted to provide any right of access not expressly authorized by law, including access authorized pursuant to in accordance with a warrant or a court order, or to preclude access allowed pursuant to in accordance with a voluntary agreement.

(e) Comply with any land use or resource use restrictions established or relied on in connection with the response activities at the facility.

(f) Not impede the effectiveness or integrity of any land use or resource use restriction employed at the facility in connection with response activities.

(g) Not later than 180 business days after a report is made to the department under this subsection, or not later than 180 days after the effective date of the amendatory act that added this subdivision if the facility was previously reported to the department, whichever is later, submit and implement a due care plan. A due care plan submitted under this subdivision must be on a form and in a manner prescribed by the department. The department may, on request and with good cause shown, grant an extension to the timeline specified under this subdivision if the property is not in use and access to the property is prohibited except as necessary to perform response activities. When a person submits a due care plan under this subdivision, the person may request that the department review and approve the due care plan before the person implements the due care plan. Not later than 90 business days after receipt of a request to review and approve a due care plan, the department shall review the due care plan to ensure that it contains sufficient information and approve, approve with conditions, or deny the due care plan. If the department denies the due care plan, the department shall provide to the person the reasons why the due care plan was not approved. A person that disagrees with the department's decision under this subdivision may submit a petition for review to the response activity review panel in accordance with section 20114e. The department may review a due care plan submitted under this subdivision to determine compliance with this section. The department shall inform the person that submits a due care plan of any deficiencies and provide a timeline to correct any deficiencies.
