Section 1:
SUBSTITUTE FOR
A bill to create the state digital service office; to require
reports; and to provide for the powers and duties of certain state
governmental officers and entities.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF MICHIGAN ENACT:
1 Sec. 1. This act may be cited as the "state digital service
2 office act".
3 Sec. 2. As used in this act:
4 (a) "Chief digital service officer" means the individual
5 appointed under section 7.
6 (b) "Chief information officer" means that term as defined in
7 section 2 of the cyber civilian corps act, 2017 PA 132, MCL 18.222.
8 (c) "Department" means the department of technology,
9 management, and budget.

1 (d) "Digital service office" means the state digital service
2 office created under section 3.
3 (e) "Director" means the director of the department.
4 (f) "Product manager" means an individual from a state
5 department or agency who is responsible for ensuring the product
6 developed from a software development project delivers measurable
7 value to users and achieves strategic objectives through evidence-
8 based decision making and continuous user engagement.
9 (g) "Standard report recipients" means the senate and house of
10 representatives appropriations subcommittees on the department
11 budget, the senate and house of representatives fiscal agencies,
12 the senate and house of representatives policy offices, and the
13 state budget office.
14 Sec. 3. (1) The state digital service office is created within
15 the department.
16 (2) The digital service office shall implement this act. The
17 digital service office shall exercise its powers, duties,
18 functions, and responsibilities independently of the director.
19 Sec. 4. (1) The digital service office shall do both of the
20 following:
21 (a) Develop strategies and practices that aim to achieve the
22 maximum effect on critical systems and programs.
23 (b) Work to foster cultural change in the technology workforce
24 of this state, conducive to implementing the adoption of modern
25 software development strategies and practices, as described in
26 subsection (4).
27 (2) The digital service office shall advise and collaborate
28 with the chief information officer, the department's central
29 procurement office, the department's enterprise portfolio

1 management office, and any office under the chief information
2 officer to adopt modern software development strategies and
3 practices, as described in subsection (4), and cybersecurity best
4 practices, for building and deploying technology and digital
5 services that improve the lives of the residents of this state.
6 (3) The digital service office shall advise and collaborate
7 with the department's central procurement office and the director
8 to study innovative procurement practices for the acquisition of
9 digital products by and for state departments and agencies to
10 facilitate the rapid provision of high-quality digital services
11 that are based on users' needs and that comply with the
12 department's best practices and policies regarding data privacy,
13 security, and accessibility.
14 (4) The digital service office shall advise and collaborate

17 departments and agencies to support adoption of modern software
18 development strategies and practices, including, but not limited
19 to, all of the following:
20 (a) Agile development methodologies.
21 (b) Open-source practices for the development and deployment
22 of new and existing digital services.
23 (c) Advocacy of modern software development tools and
24 practices that include, but are not limited to, all of the
25 following:
26 (i) Development, security, and operations.
27 (ii) Modular contracting.
28 (iii) Active product management.
29 (iv) User-centered design.

7 (xi) The modern best practices described in the 13 plays of the
8 Digital Services Playbook, published by United States Digital
9 Service.
10 Sec. 5. The digital service office shall do all of the
11 following:
12 (a) Except as otherwise provided in this subdivision, review
13 requests from state departments and agencies for software
14 development projects that have an estimated cost of $1,000,000.00
15 or more to evaluate and advise on the optimal design and
16 development approach, including development approaches that
17 encourage in-house development. The digital service office may
18 review software development projects that have an estimated cost of
19 less than $1,000,000.00 if requested by a state department or
20 agency or if the digital service office determines a review is
21 beneficial.
22 (b) Promote the most effective means for improving digital
23 services based on quantitative and qualitative assessments of user
24 needs.
25 (c) Coordinate training for state departments and agencies on
26 effective management and collaboration in software development
27 projects, including, but not limited to, training on the role of
28 product managers.
29 (d) Provide all of the following for information technology

22 7(4), including a description of the decision.

24 1 of each year, prepare a report on resident-centered outcomes for
25 projects that the digital service office reviewed under section

10 (a) The project is at risk of exceeding the project budget or

12 (b) The project has exceeded the project budget or schedule.
13 (c) There is a risk of significant disruption of efficient and
14 reliable services to residents of this state.
15 (4) The report described in subsection (3) must identify the
16 reason for the problem and provide recommendations to resolve the
17 problem.
18 Sec. 7. (1) The chief digital service officer shall lead the
19 digital service office. The governor shall appoint the chief
20 digital service officer with the advice and consent of the senate
21 majority leader and the speaker of the house of representatives.
22 (2) To be eligible for appointment as the chief digital
23 service officer, an individual must be a full voting member of the
24 board that governs the information technology investment fund
25 described in section 838 of 2025 PA 22.
26 (3) The chief digital service officer shall build and commit
27 to a talent strategy that attracts, recruits, competitively
28 compensates, and retains interdisciplinary expertise in
29 cybersecurity, technology, strategy, business, and management to

3 (4) The chief digital service officer may block or redirect
4 the advancement of a technology project that poses a risk of
5 significant unnecessary costs to this state or significant
6 disruption of efficient and reliable services to the residents of
7 this state.
8 Sec. 8. The digital service office shall exist for the 5-year
9 period immediately following the effective date of this act.
