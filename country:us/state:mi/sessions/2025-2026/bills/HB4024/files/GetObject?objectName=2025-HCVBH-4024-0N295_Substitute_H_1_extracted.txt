Section 1:
SUBSTITUTE FOR
A bill to require that certain educational institutions in
this state, when providing students with multiple occupancy
restrooms, changing areas, and similar facilities, do so in a
manner that ensures each student's privacy from individuals of the
opposite sex; and to provide for the powers and duties of certain
state and local governmental officers and entities.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF MICHIGAN ENACT:
1 Sec. 1. This act may be cited as the "student restroom privacy
2 act".
3 Sec. 2. As used in this act:
4 (a) "Educational institution" means a public educational
5 institution or a separate school or department of a public
6 educational institution, including, but not limited to, an academy,

2
1 college, elementary or secondary school, extension course,
2 kindergarten, nursery, local school system, or university; a
3 business, nursing, professional, secretarial, technical, or
4 vocational school; any educational institution that receives funds
5 from this state; or an agent of an educational institution.
6 (b) "Multiple occupancy restroom or changing area" means an
7 area in an educational institution designed or designated to be
8 used by more than 1 student at a time, where students may be in
9 various stages of undress in the presence of other students or
10 individuals. Multiple occupancy restroom or changing area includes,
11 but is not limited to, a restroom, locker room, changing room, or
12 shower room.
13 (c) "Sex" means the biological indication of male or female,
14 as listed on an individual's original birth certificate that was
15 issued at or near the time of the individual's birth.
16 Sec. 3. (1) An educational institution shall require every
17 multiple occupancy restroom or changing area to be used by
18 individuals based on the individuals' sex.
19 (2) This section does not apply to an individual who enters a
20 multiple occupancy restroom or changing area designated for use by
21 the opposite sex if the individual enters for any of the following
22 circumstances:
23 (a) Custodial purposes.
24 (b) Maintenance or inspection purposes.
25 (c) To render medical assistance.
26 (d) To accompany a student needing assistance if the assisting
27 individual is an employee of the educational institution or the
28 student's parent or legal guardian or authorized caregiver.
29 (e) To receive assistance in using the area.
