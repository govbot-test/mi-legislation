HOUSE BILL NO. 5761 A bill to amend 1994 PA 451, entitled "Natural resources and environmental protection act," by amending sections 8505 and 8506 (MCL 324.8505 and 324.8506), as amended by 2015 PA 118. the people of the state of michigan enact: Sec. 8505. (1) A person shall not distribute a specialty fertilizer or soil conditioner unless it the specialty fertilizer or soil conditioner is registered with the department. An A person shall apply for a registration on a form and in a manner prescribed by the department. An application for registration listing must include all of the following: (a) A list of each brand and product name of each grade of specialty fertilizer or soil conditioner. shall be made on a form furnished by the director. An application shall be accompanied with the fees described (b) The fees required in subsection (4). for each brand and product name of each grade. Labels (c) The labels for each brand and product name of each grade shall accompany the application.of specialty fertilizer or soil conditioner. (2) If the specialty fertilizer or soil conditioner is a beneficial use by-product intended for beneficial use 3 under part 115, the application shall must also include the information identified in section 11551(7). (3) Upon On approval of an application by the director, a copy of the registration approval shall must be furnished provided to the applicant. All registrations expire on December 31 of each year. (4) A person applying that applies for a registration under subsection (1) shall pay the following annual fees for each brand and product name of each grade: (a) Registration fee of $25.00.$75.00 per product that is a specialty fertilizer or $50.00 per product that is a soil conditioner, as applicable. (b) Appropriate water quality protection fee provided for in section 8715. (5) A distributor is not required to register a brand of fertilizer that is registered under this part by another person, if the label does not differ in any respect. (6) A manufacturer or distributor of custom blend specialty fertilizers for home lawns, golf courses, recreational areas, or other nonfarm areas is not required to register each grade distributed but shall must license their the firm on an application furnished by the director for and in a manner prescribed by the department. The application for a license under this subsection must be accompanied by an annual fee of $100.00 and shall label the custom blend specialty fertilizer must be labeled as provided in section 8502. The label of each custom blend specialty fertilizer distributed under this subsection shall must be maintained by the manufacturer or distributor for 1 year for inspection by and made available to the director for inspection on request. (7) A manufacturer or distributor of soil conditioners blended according to specifications provided to a blender or blended as specifically requested by the consumer prior to before blending shall must either register each brand or blend distributed or license its firm on an application furnished by the director for and in a manner prescribed by the department. The application for a registration or license under this section must be accompanied by an annual fee of $100.00. and shall label the Each soil conditioner must be labeled as provided in section 8502, . The label of each soil conditioner distributed under this subsection shall and the label must be maintained by the manufacturer or distributor for 1 year for inspection by and made available to the director for inspection on request. (8) A registrant that operates from a business location outside this state shall do either 1 of the following: (a) Continuously maintain in this state a registered office and a resident agent. , which The resident agent may be an individual resident in this state whose business office or residence is identical with to the registered office, a domestic corporation or limited liability company, or a foreign corporation or limited liability company authorized to transact business in this state and having that has a business office identical with to the registered office. The registrant shall file with the department the name, address, and telephone number of the resident agent and shall maintain and make available to the department records required by this part and part 87. (b) Maintain and make available to the department records required by this part and part 87 and pay all costs incurred by the department in auditing the records if they the records are held at an out-of-state location. Sec. 8506. (1) An inspection fee of 35 cents per ton shall be paid to the department for all fertilizers or soil conditioners sold or distributed in this state. Each manufacturer of peat or peat moss , the inspection fee shall be 2 cents per cubic yard. This fee does is responsible for a $25.00 fee. The fees described in this subsection do not apply to registered specialty fertilizers or soil conditioners sold or distributed only in packages of 10 pounds or less. (2) Payment of the inspection fee shall be made on the basis of tonnage reports setting forth the number of tons of each grade of fertilizer and soil conditioner and the number of cubic yards of peat or peat moss sold or distributed in this state. The reports shall cover the periods of the year and be made in a manner specified by the director in rules, and shall be filed with the department not later than 30 days after the close of each period. The time may be extended for cause for an additional 15 days only on written request to, and approval by, the department. Remittance to cover the inspection fee shall accompany each tonnage report. Payments due of less than $5.00 are waived, and refunds of less than $5.00 will not be processed, unless requested in writing. For any report not filed with the department by the due date, a penalty of $50.00 or 10% of the amount due, whichever is greater, shall be assessed. Unpaid fees and penalties constitute a debt and become the basis of a judgment against the licensee. Records upon which the statement of tonnage is based, including those described in this section and section 8715, are subject to department audit. (2) (3) When more than 1 person is involved in the distribution of fertilizer or soil conditioners, the last person who that is licensed or has the fertilizer or soil conditioner registered and who that distributes to a nonlicensee or nonregistrant is responsible for reporting the tonnage and paying the inspection fee.fees required under this section. (3) Subject to subsection (4), if a manufacturer manufactures more than 142 tons of an agricultural use fertilizer, the manufacturer is responsible for a fee of $0.35 per ton for each ton that exceeds 142 tons, in addition to the fee required under subsection (1). (4) If a manufacturer manufactures more than 10,000 cubic yards of agricultural use composted products that make specific nutrient value claims, the manufacturer is responsible for a fee of $0.05 for each cubic yard that exceeds 10,000 cubic yards, in addition to the fee required under subsection (1). A manufacturer that pays a fee under this subsection is not required to pay the fee for agricultural use fertilizer under subsection (3). (5) (4) Money collected by the department under this section shall must be forwarded to the state treasurer for deposit into the fund. Enacting section 1. This amendatory act does not take effect unless all of the following bills of the 103rd Legislature are enacted into law: (a) House Bill No. 5762 (request no. H03967'25). (b) House Bill No. 5763 (request no. H03968'25).
