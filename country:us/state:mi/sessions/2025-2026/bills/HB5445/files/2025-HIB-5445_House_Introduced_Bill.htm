HOUSE BILL NO. 5445

A bill to amend 1961 PA 236, entitled

"Revised judicature act of 1961,"

by amending sections 1003, 1011, 1021, and 1023 (MCL 600.1003, 600.1011, 600.1021, and 600.1023), section 1003 as added by 1996 PA 388 and sections 1011, 1021, and 1023 as amended by 2002 PA 682, and by adding sections 1002 and 1012.

the people of the state of michigan enact:

Sec. 1002. A judge elected, appointed, or assigned to the family division of circuit court has the same power and authority as a circuit court judge in the same jurisdiction.

Sec. 1003. (1) Each judicial circuit shall have a family division of circuit court.

(2) Each judicial circuit with more than 1 circuit court judge shall have at least 1 judgeship permanently assigned to the family division of circuit court. The number of judgeships assigned to a family division of circuit court must allow for proper caseloads for judges as determined by a case weight study conducted by the supreme court.

(3) A circuit court or probate court judgeship existing as of January 1, 2028 may be assigned as a permanent family division of circuit court judgeship in accordance with the approved family court plan. If a judgeship is assigned to the family division of circuit court, the judgeship may only be reassigned with the approval of an updated family court plan that reduces the number of judgeships assigned to the family division of circuit court.

(4) If a judgeship is assigned to the family division of circuit court under subsection (3), the family court plan created under section 1011 must reflect that assignment and the judge currently sitting in that judgeship shall decide to be 1 of the following:

(a) Temporarily assigned to the family division of circuit court in accordance with subsection (5).

(b) Permanently assigned to the family division of circuit court in accordance with subsection (6).

(5) A judge who serves temporarily under this subsection has the powers and shall perform all of the duties of a permanent judge of the family division of circuit court. A judge who requests to be temporarily assigned to the family division of circuit court may transfer out of the family division of circuit court if a judgeship in the same jurisdiction that is not assigned to the family division of circuit court becomes available. After the temporary judge transfers out, the seat is a permanent family division of circuit court seat and must be designated as such for an appointment by the governor and on the ballot. If a judge under this subsection who transfers to another judgeship in the same jurisdiction has had substantial involvement in a case, whether or not the trial or a hearing has begun, that judge may keep the matter upon request of a party or by agreement of both of the judges involved until its conclusion and enter judgment or a final order.

(6) A judge who as of January 1, 2028 has a caseload of greater than 50% family cases may request to be designated as a permanent family division of circuit court judge in the court's family court plan, and the designation attaches to that judgeship permanently. A judge who requests a permanent family division of circuit court designation may vacate a current judgeship and run for a different judicial seat but cannot transfer to another judgeship. This subsection does not prevent a judge from temporarily providing coverage for another judge by assignment outside of the family division of circuit court in response to an urgent need for that assignment.

(7) An individual who runs for a judgeship assigned to the family division of circuit court must be nominated and elected under chapter XIX of the Michigan election law, 1954 PA 116, MCL 168.411 to 168.426.

(8) If a judicial circuit has more than 1 assigned judge of the family division of circuit court, the supreme court shall appoint a chief judge of the family division of circuit court from judges who are permanently assigned to the family division of circuit court. The chief judge of the family division of circuit court is primarily responsible for all administrative matters within the family division of circuit court.

Sec. 1011. (1) Not later than July 1, 2003, in each judicial circuit, the chief circuit judge and the chief probate judge or judges shall enter into an agreement that establishes a plan known as the "family court plan" that details how the family division will be operated in that circuit and how the services of the agencies listed in section 1043 will be coordinated in order to promote more efficient and effective services to families and individuals. If a probate court district includes counties that are in different judicial circuits, the chief judge of each judicial circuit that includes a county in the probate court district and the chief probate judge shall enter into a family court plan for each circuit.

(2) If, in any judicial circuit, the agreement required under subsection (1) is not entered into on or before July 1, 2003, the supreme court shall develop and implement the family court plan for that judicial circuit.

(3) A family court plan required under subsection (1) shall provide that a judge's service pursuant to the family court plan be consistent with the goal of developing sufficient judicial expertise in family law to properly serve the interests of the families and children whose cases are assigned to that judge. The chief judge of the circuit court shall have the authority and flexibility to determine the duration of a judge's service pursuant to the family court plan in furtherance of this goal.

(4) A judge serving pursuant to the family court plan shall receive appropriate training as required by the supreme court.

(5) A family court plan required under subsection (1) may provide that when a judge's service pursuant to the family court plan ends, the pending cases of that judge are to be reassigned to another judge or judges serving pursuant to the family court plan or are to be resolved by that judge.

(6) A family court plan required under subsection (1) shall specifically identify any probate judge serving pursuant to the family court plan.

(7) A family court plan required under subsection (1) shall be reviewed and revised periodically, as necessary, by the chief circuit judge or judges and the chief probate judge or judges, and shall be submitted for approval by the supreme court.

(1) Beginning January 1, 2028, each circuit court must revise its family court plan to comply with this act.

(2) If by April 1, 2028 a judicial circuit fails to come into compliance with an approved family court plan under this chapter, the supreme court shall develop and implement a family court plan for that circuit.

(3) A family court plan required under subsection (1) must do all of the following:

(a) Identify all judges who will serve in the family division of circuit court.

(b) Indicate if each judge under subdivision (a) is permanently assigned or temporarily assigned under section 1003 until the seat is vacated.

(c) Verify that all judges under subdivision (a) are trained in compliance with subsection (4).

(d) Ensure that a judge's service under the family court plan encourages the judge to develop sufficient judicial expertise in family law to properly serve the interests of the families and children whose cases are assigned to that judge.

(e) Require that matters in the family division of circuit court be heard in accordance with section 1023.

(4) A judge serving under the family court plan shall have appropriate training. Judges elected, assigned, or appointed to the family division of circuit court shall certify to the family court plan review board in section 1012 that they have 1 of the following prior to presiding in the family division of circuit court:

(a) At least 40 hours of family law training, 40 hours of children's law training, and 16 hours of domestic violence training.

(b) If a judge cannot certify prior training under subdivision (a), the judge shall participate in the hours of training necessary to meet the requirements under section 1019, including at least 40 hours of family law training before hearing family law cases, 40 hours of children's law training before hearing child welfare cases, and 16 hours of domestic violence training before hearing any cases.

(5) Referees serving in the family division of circuit court must have not less than 5 years of family law experience and children's law experience. If a referee is not experienced in both matters, the referee must receive at least 40 hours of training in the matter the referee is not experienced in before presiding over those matters.

(6) All subsequent changes to an approved family court plan must be reviewed and approved by the supreme court.

(7) The governor shall consider the education requirements of subsection (4) before appointing a family division of circuit court judge.

Sec. 1012. The supreme court shall be responsible for all of the following:

(a) Review the family court plans of each circuit court and ensure compliance with this chapter.

(b) Require changes to a family court plan for compliance with this chapter.

(c) Review changes to family court plans under subdivision (b), as necessary.

Sec. 1023. Cases in the family division of circuit court must be assigned to a judge and referee team by family. When 2 or more matters within the jurisdiction of the family division of circuit court involving members of the same immediate family are pending in the same judicial circuit, those matters , whenever practicable, shall must be assigned to the judge and referee team to whom the first such case was assigned. As used in this section, "immediate family" means spouse, parent, child, or sibling relationships.
