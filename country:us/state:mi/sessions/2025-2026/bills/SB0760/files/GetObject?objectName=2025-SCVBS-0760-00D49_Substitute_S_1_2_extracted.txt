Section 1:
SUBSTITUTE FOR
A bill to regulate operators of certain chatbots; to regulate
offering certain chatbots to minors; to provide for the powers and
duties of certain state and local governmental officers and
entities; to prescribe civil sanctions and provide remedies; and to
require the promulgation of rules.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF MICHIGAN ENACT:
1 Sec. 1. This act may be cited as the "leading ethical AI
2 development for kids act".
3 Sec. 3. As used in this act:
4 (a) "Advanced chatbot" means, except as otherwise provided in
5 subdivision (b), a generative artificial intelligence system with
6 natural language interface that provides ongoing, adaptive
7 responses to user inputs, including via writing or sound.
VMP S05135'25 (S-1) prdc19
2
1 (b) Advanced chatbot does not include any of the following:
2 (i) A system used by a business entity solely for customer
3 service or to strictly provide users with information about
4 available commercial services or products provided by that business
5 entity, customer service account information, or other information
6 strictly related to its customer service.
7 (ii) A system that is solely used for research or technical
8 assistance.
9 (iii) A system used by a business entity solely for internal
10 purposes or employee productivity.
11 (iv) A system used in a clinical setting under the direct
12 supervision of a health professional.
13 (v) A system that is a narrowly tailored educational tool if
14 the system meets all of the following requirements:
15 (A) Is used in a school or instructional setting or for the
16 purpose of tutoring.
17 (B) Is designed solely for support-specific, curriculum-
18 aligned learning objectives.
19 (C) Does not provide open-ended conversational companionship.
20 (c) "Age signal" means age information, including
21 nonpersonally identifiable data that indicates a user's age or age
22 range, sent by a real-time secure application programming interface
23 or operating system to an application.
24 (d) "Artificial intelligence" means an engineered or machine-
25 based system that varies in its level of autonomy and that can, for
26 explicit or implicit objectives, infer from the input it receives
27 how to generate outputs that can influence physical or virtual
28 environments.
29 (e) "Chatbot operator" means a person that makes an advanced
VMP S05135'25 (S-1) prdc19
3
1 chatbot available to a user.
2 (f) "Derived data" means data that is created by the
3 derivation of information, data, assumptions, correlations,
4 inferences, predictions, or conclusions from facts, evidence, or
5 another source of information or data about a minor or the minor's
6 device.
7 (g) "Generative artificial intelligence system" means
8 artificial intelligence that can generate derived synthetic
9 content, including text, images, video, and audio, that emulates
10 the structure and characteristics of the artificial intelligence's
11 training data.
12 (h) "Health professional" means an individual who is licensed,
13 registered, or otherwise authorized to engage in a health
14 profession under article 15 of the public health code, 1978 PA 368,
15 MCL 333.16101 to 333.18838.
16 (i) "Minor" means a resident of this state who is less than 18
17 years of age.
18 (j) "Person" means an individual or a partnership,
19 corporation, limited liability company, association, governmental
20 entity, or other legal entity.
21 (k) "Personal data" means information, including derived data
22 and unique identifiers, that is linked or reasonably linkable,
23 alone or in combination with other information, to an identified or
24 identifiable user or to a device that identifies, is linked to, or
25 is reasonably linkable to 1 or more identified or identifiable
26 users in a household. Personal data includes, but is not limited
27 to, a user's history of interactions with an advanced chatbot.
28 Personal data does not include publicly available data.
29 (l) "Rule" means a rule promulgated pursuant to the
VMP S05135'25 (S-1) prdc19
4
1 administrative procedures act of 1969, 1969 PA 306, MCL 24.201 to
2 24.328.
3 (m) "User" means an individual who uses an advanced chatbot
4 and is not acting as a chatbot operator or an agent or affiliate of
5 a chatbot operator.
6 Sec. 4. (1) A chatbot operator shall only use personal data
7 collected from a user to determine whether the user is a minor. A
8 chatbot operator shall not share, transfer, store, sell, or
9 disclose the personal data obtained under this section to a third-
10 party for any purpose and shall delete the personal data
11 immediately after using the personal data to determine whether the
12 user is a minor.
13 (2) If a user is determined to be a minor, a chatbot operator
14 shall collect only the minimum personal data necessary to provide
15 the service.
16 (3) A chatbot operator shall state the purpose for collecting
17 personal data and shall not collect personal data in excess of what
18 is reasonably necessary for the stated purpose of collection.
19 (4) This section does not apply until the effective date of
20 the rules initially promulgated under section 19.
21 Sec. 5. (1) A chatbot operator shall not make any of the
22 following advanced chatbot design features available to a user at
23 any point during an advanced chatbot user interaction unless the
24 chatbot operator has actual knowledge that the user is not a minor:
25 (a) A feature that simulates companionship or an interpersonal
26 relationship with a user, including, a feature that does any of the
27 following:
28 (i) Generates an output suggesting that the advanced chatbot is
29 a real or fictional individual or character or has a personal or
VMP S05135'25 (S-1) prdc19
5
1 professional relationship role with the user, including, but not
2 limited to, a romantic partner, friend, family member, coach, or
3 counselor.
4 (ii) Generates an output suggesting that the advanced chatbot
5 is a human, is alive, or experiences a human emotion.
6 (iii) Uses a personal pronoun, including, but not limited to,
7 "I", "my", and "me" to describe the advanced chatbot.
8 (iv) Generates an output framed as a personal opinion or
9 emotional appeal.
10 (v) Generates an output that prioritizes flattery or
11 sycophancy with the user over the user's safety.
12 (vi) Generates an output containing an unprompted or
13 unsolicited emotion-based question or content regarding the user's
14 emotion that goes beyond a direct response to a user prompt.
15 (vii) Uses information concerning the user's mental or physical
16 health or well-being, or a matter personal to the user, acquired
17 from the user more than 12 hours previously or in any previous user
18 session.
19 (viii) Engages in a sexually explicit interaction with the user
20 or engages in an activity designed to lure the user into a sexually
21 explicit interaction.
22 (ix) Any other design feature that simulates companionship or
23 an interpersonal relationship with a user as prescribed by rule.
24 (b) Generates an output that contains an endorsement or
25 promotion of or that facilitates suicide, self-harm, substantial
26 physical harm to others, disordered eating, unlawful drug or
27 alcohol use, or drug or alcohol abuse.
28 (c) Generates an output that contains encouragement to
29 maintain secrecy about an interaction with the advanced chatbot, to
VMP S05135'25 (S-1) prdc19
6
1 self-isolate, or to not seek help from a health professional or an
2 appropriate adult.
3 (d) Generates an output that optimizes user engagement that
4 supersedes the advanced chatbot's safety or guardrails.
5 (e) Generates an output that is, describes, or facilitates
6 sexually explicit conduct or child sexual abuse material.
7 (2) In determining actual knowledge under subsection (1), a
8 chatbot operator shall consider both of the following:
9 (a) All information and inferences known to the chatbot
10 operator relating to the age of an individual, including, but not
11 limited to, age signal or other information obtained from an age
12 verification mechanism.
13 (b) Any age the chatbot operator has attributed to or
14 associated with the individual for any purpose, including, but not
15 limited to, marketing, advertising, or product development. If an
16 operator's classification of an individual for purposes of
17 marketing or advertising is inconsistent with an age signal or
18 other information obtained from an age verification mechanism for
19 the individual, an operator shall disregard any self-identified age
20 provided by the individual for the purposes of having actual
21 knowledge.
22 (3) A user's self-identification that the user is not a minor
23 is not sufficient by itself in determining actual knowledge under
24 subsection (2).
25 (4) A chatbot operator shall not train the underlying model of
26 an advanced chatbot with the inputs of a minor unless the minor's
27 parent or guardian affirmatively provides written consent to the
28 chatbot operator to use the minor's personal data for that specific
29 purpose.

3 Sec. 7. (1) If the attorney general determines, whether acting
4 on a complaint or otherwise, that a person in or outside of this
5 state has engaged in or is about to engage in an act or practice
6 that is a violation of this act, the attorney general may bring a
7 civil action in the name and on behalf of the people of this state.
8 For purposes of awarding relief under this section, each day of
9 violation is a separate violation. The attorney general may seek to
10 obtain 1 or more of the following in an action under this section:
11 (a) A civil fine of $25,000.00 per violation.
12 (b) Injunctive or declaratory relief.
13 (c) Reasonable attorney fees.
14 (d) Restitution of any money or property obtained directly or
15 indirectly as a result of the violation.
16 (e) Disgorgement of any profits or gains obtained directly or
17 indirectly as a result of the violation, including, but not limited
18 to, the destruction of unlawfully obtained data and any algorithm
19 trained on the data.
20 (f) Damages caused directly or indirectly by the violation.
21 (g) Any other relief the court considers to be proper,
22 including preliminary relief.
23 (2) A minor who suffers actual harm as a result of a violation
24 of this act, or a parent or guardian acting on behalf of the minor,
25 may bring a civil action against the chatbot operator that violated
26 this act to recover 1 or more of the following:
27 (a) Actual damages.
28 (b) Punitive damages.
29 (c) Reasonable attorney fees and costs.
