HOUSE BILL NO. 5935 HOUSE BILL NO. 5935 A bill to amend 1972 PA 230, entitled "Stille-DeRossett-Hale single state construction code act," by amending section 11 (MCL 125.1511). the people of the state of michigan enact: Sec. 11. (1) The An enforcing agency shall examine an application for a building permit or an additional permit. If the application conforms to this act, the code, and the requirements of other applicable laws and ordinances, the enforcing agency shall approve the application and issue a building permit or an additional permit to the applicant, as applicable. An The enforcing agency shall grant the application, shall be granted, in whole or in part, or denied within deny the application not later than 10 business days , except that in case of after receipt of the application. However, if the application is for an unusually complicated building or structure, action shall be taken within the enforcing agency shall grant, in whole or in part, or deny the application not later than 15 business days after receipt of the application. Failure by an enforcing agency to grant, in whole or in part, or deny an application within these periods of time shall be deemed a denial of the application for purposes of authorizing the institution of an appeal to the appropriate board of appeals. The (2) Both of the following apply to an application for a building permit or an additional permit that an applicant submits to the department: (a) If the department denies the application for a building permit or an additional permit, the department must notify the applicant of the basis for the denial. The notice must include a citation to the provision of this act, the code, or the requirements of other applicable laws and ordinances on which the denial is based. (b) If the department determines that the application for a building permit or an additional permit is not a complete application, the department must notify the applicant in writing or make the information electronically available to the applicant not later than 7 days after receipt of the application. If the department fails to notify or make the information electronically available to the applicant within the 7-day time period, the application for a building permit or an additional permit is considered to be a complete application. (3) An enforcing agency shall approve changes in plans and specifications previously approved by it, if the changes require approval and if the plans and specifications when so changed remain in conformity with law. Except as otherwise provided in this act or the code, the construction or alteration of a building or structure shall must not be commenced until a building permit has been issued. The construction of a building or structure shall must comply with the approved application for a building permit, and the enforcing agency shall insure ensure such compliance in the manner provided in section 12 and in any other way it deems appropriate. (4) (2) The An enforcing agency may suspend, revoke, or cancel a building permit in case of failure or neglect to comply with the provisions of this act or the code, or upon a finding by it that a false statement or representation has been made in the application for the building permit. (5) As used in this section: (a) "Additional permit" means a permit for a particular kind of work as described in section 10(6). Additional permit includes, but is not limited to, all of the following permits: (i) An electrical permit. (ii) A plumbing permit. (iii) A mechanical or HVAC permit. (iv) A demolition permit. (v) A foundation-only permit. (vi) A temporary structure permit. (vii) A shell building permit. (b) "Complete application" means an application complete on its face that is submitted with any applicable permit fees as well as any other information, records, approval, security, or similar item required by law or rule from a local unit of government, federal agency, or private entity but not from another department or agency of this state.
