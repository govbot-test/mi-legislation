Section 1:
HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION U A joint resolution proposing an amendment to the state constitution of 1963, by amending section 21 of article V and section 5 of article VIII, to modify the nominating process for the secretary of state and attorney general, to provide for gubernatorial appointment of the members of the governing boards of the University of Michigan, Michigan State University, and Wayne State University by and with the advice and consent of the senate, to limit the terms of university board members, and to establish certain eligibility requirements for membership on those university governing boards, including a requirement that the university boards be bipartisan. Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives of the state of Michigan, That the following amendment to the state constitution of 1963, to modify the nominating process for the secretary of state and attorney general, to provide for gubernatorial appointment of the members of the governing boards of the University of Michigan, Michigan State University, and Wayne State University by and with the advice and consent of the senate, to limit the terms of university board members, and to establish certain eligibility requirements for membership on those university governing boards, including a requirement that the university boards be bipartisan, is proposed, agreed to, and submitted to the people of the state: ARTICLE V Sec. 21. (1) The governor, lieutenant governor, secretary of state, and attorney general shall be elected for four-year terms at the general election in each alternate even-numbered year. (2) The Except as otherwise provided in subsection (3), the lieutenant governor, secretary of state, and attorney general shall be nominated by party conventions in a manner prescribed by law. In the general election one vote shall be cast jointly for the candidates for governor and lieutenant governor nominated by the same party. (3) Beginning January 1, 2027, the secretary of state and attorney general shall be nominated by primary elections in the manner prescribed by law. (4) Vacancies in the office of the secretary of state and attorney general shall be filled by appointment by the governor. article VIII Sec. 5. (1) The regents of the University of Michigan and their successors in office shall constitute a body corporate known as the Regents of the University of Michigan; the trustees of Michigan State University and their successors in office shall constitute a body corporate known as the Board of Trustees of Michigan State University; the governors of Wayne State University and their successors in office shall constitute a body corporate known as the Board of Governors of Wayne State University. Each board shall have general supervision of its institution and the control and direction of all expenditures from the institution's funds. Each board shall, as often as necessary, elect a president of the institution under its supervision. He The president shall be the principal executive officer of the institution, be ex-officio a member of the board without the right to vote, and preside at meetings of the board. (2) The Until December 30, 2026, the board of each institution shall consist of eight members who shall hold office for terms of eight years and who shall be elected as provided by law, . The and the governor shall fill board vacancies by appointment. Each appointee shall hold office until a successor has been nominated and elected as provided by law.member of a board under this subsection is discharged on December 30, 2026. (3) Beginning December 31, 2026, the board of each institution includes nine members, with not more than five from the same political party. Except as provided in subsections (4) and (5), the governor shall appoint by and with the advice and consent of the senate members for one full term of eight years. At least one member of each board must be appointed from a list of three nominees provided by the institution's alumni association. At least one member of the Board of Trustees of Michigan State University must have practical farming expertise. Board members shall comply with state ethics laws. (4) If a vacancy occurs on a board, the governor shall appoint an individual for the remainder of the unexpired term by and with the advice and consent of the senate. A member appointed to fill a vacancy that serves less than four full years is eligible for appointment to one full eight-year term. (5) All of the following apply only to the first members appointed to each board after December 30, 2026: (a) Two must be appointed for terms ending December 31, 2028. (b) Two must be appointed for terms ending December 31, 2030. (c) Three must be appointed for terms ending December 31, 2032. (d) Two must be appointed for terms ending December 31, 2034. (e) The governor with a term expiring on January 1, 2027 shall appoint one board member under subdivision (a), one under subdivision (b), one under subdivision (c), and one under subdivision (d). The governor with a term beginning on January 1, 2027 shall appoint the remaining 5 board members under subdivisions (a) to (d). Resolved further, That the foregoing amendment shall be submitted to the people of the state at a special election to be held August 4, 2026 in the manner provided by law. Resolved further, That it is the intent of the legislature that when submitted to the people of the state at a special election to be held August 4, 2026, this amendment must be presented with the following question: "A PROPOSAL TO REQUIRE NOMINATION OF SECRETARY OF STATE AND ATTORNEY GENERAL AT PRIMARY ELECTIONS AND CREATE NEW BIPARTISAN AND TERM-LIMITED GOVERNING BOARDS FOR UNIVERSITY OF MICHIGAN, MICHIGAN STATE UNIVERSITY, AND WAYNE STATE UNIVERSITY. The proposed constitutional amendment would: - After 2026, require secretary of state and attorney general nomination at primary elections, not political party conventions. - Create new term limited 9-member boards to replace current 8-member boards for the University of Michigan, Michigan State University, and Wayne State University, bringing members under state ethics laws. - Prohibit more than 5 members per board from 1 political party. - Require alumni association nomination of 1 member per board and 1 MSU board member with practical farming experience. - Have current governor appoint 4 new board members for each university, new governor elected on November 3, 2026 appoint the remainder, with the appointments subject to senate advice and consent. Should this proposal be adopted? Yes [ ] No [ ]".
