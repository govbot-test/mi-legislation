(3) The governor shall appoint the members of the strategic advisory board described under subsection (2)(b) within 90 days after the effective date of the amendatory act that added this section.

(4) The term of a member of the strategic advisory board is 1 year or until a successor is appointed under subsection (2), whichever is later, but not to exceed the time period it takes the strategic advisory board to develop the strategic plan in accordance with section 9c.

(5) If a vacancy occurs on the strategic advisory board, the governor shall appoint an individual to fill the vacancy for the balance of the term.

(6) The governor may remove a member of the strategic advisory board for incompetence, dereliction of duty, malfeasance, misfeasance, or nonfeasance in office, or any other good cause.

(7) The individuals appointed under subsection (2)(b) shall call the first meeting of the strategic advisory board. At the first meeting, the nonvoting members appointed under subsection (2)(b) shall elect the voting members under subsection (2)(a) as follows:

(a) The nonvoting members described in subsection (2)(b)(i) shall elect 4 of its own members listed under subsection (2)(b)(i) to serve as voting members under subsection (2)(a)(i).

(b) The nonvoting members described in subsection (2)(b)(ii) shall elect 4 of its own members listed under subsection (2)(b)(ii) to serve as voting members under subsection (2)(a)(ii).

(c) The nonvoting members described in subsection (2)(b)(iii) shall elect 4 of its own members listed under subsection (2)(b)(iii) to serve as voting members under subsection (2)(a)(iii).

(d) The nonvoting members described in subsection (2)(b)(iv) shall elect 4 of its own members listed under subsection (2)(b)(iv) to serve as voting members under subsection (2)(a)(iv).

(e) The nonvoting members described in subsection (2)(b)(v) shall elect 4 of its own members listed under subsection (2)(b)(v) to serve as voting members under subsection (2)(a)(v).

(f) The nonvoting members described in subsection (2)(b)(vi) shall elect 4 of its own members listed under subsection (2)(b)(vi) to serve as voting members under subsection (2)(a)(vi).

(g) The nonvoting members described in subsection (2)(b)(vii) shall elect 4 of its own members listed under subsection (2)(b)(vii) to serve as voting members under subsection (2)(a)(vii).

(h) The nonvoting members described in subsection (2)(b)(viii) shall elect 4 of its own members listed under subsection (2)(b)(viii) to serve as voting members under subsection (2)(a)(viii).

(8) After the voting members have been elected at the first meeting, the voting members of the strategic advisory board shall elect a member as a chairperson and may elect other officers that it considers necessary or appropriate. The governor may appoint 1 of the voting members to serve as a co-chair of the strategic advisory board. The strategic advisory board shall meet at least quarterly, or more frequently at the call of the chairperson or co-chair if appointed or at the request of 10 or more members.

(9) A majority of the voting members of the strategic advisory board constitute a quorum for transacting business. A vote in favor by a majority of the voting members of the strategic advisory board serving is required for any action of the strategic advisory board.

(10) The strategic advisory board shall conduct its business in compliance with the open meetings act, 1976 PA 267, MCL 15.261 to 15.275.

(11) A writing that is prepared, owned, used, possessed, or retained by the strategic advisory board in performing an official function is subject to the freedom of information act, 1976 PA 442, MCL 15.231 to 15.246.

(12) A member of the strategic advisory board is not entitled to compensation for service on the strategic advisory board, but the strategic advisory board may reimburse a member for actual and necessary expenses incurred in serving.

(13) Only the voting members of the strategic advisory board shall develop a strategic plan in accordance with section 9c with advice and collaboration from the nonvoting members as described in this section.

(14) In addition, the strategic advisory board may do either of the following:

(a) Take any other action necessary to achieve the purposes of section 9c.

(b) Perform any other duties or exercise any other powers assigned to the strategic advisory board by law.

(15) Once the strategic advisory board develops and submits the strategic plan in accordance with section 9c, the strategic advisory board is dissolved and shall cease operations.

Enacting section 1. This amendatory act does not take effect unless Senate Bill No. 213 of the 103rd Legislature is enacted into law.
