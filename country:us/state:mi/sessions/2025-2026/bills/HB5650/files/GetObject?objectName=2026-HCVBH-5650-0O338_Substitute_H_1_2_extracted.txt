Section 1:
SUBSTITUTE FOR
A bill to require the attorney general to submit reports
related to certain litigation to certain committees of the
legislature; to provide for the powers and duties of certain state
governmental officers and entities; to provide sanctions for
failing to submit the reports; and to provide for remedies.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF MICHIGAN ENACT:
1 Sec. 1. As used in this act, "litigation" means either of the
2 following:
3 (a) An action at law brought by the attorney general on behalf
4 of this state or of the people of this state.
5 (b) An action at law in which the attorney general has
6 intervened and appeared on behalf of the people of this state.
7 Sec. 2. (1) If total state expenditures related to litigation

7 appropriations committee, and each member of the senate oversight
8 committee. Each report must include all of the following:
9 (a) An accounting of the expenditures, including an estimate
10 of future expenditures.
11 (b) An explanation of the litigation.
12 (c) Justification for the expenditures described in
13 subdivision (a).
14 (2) Not later than 60 days after a final order is entered in
15 litigation described in subsection (1) and all rights of appeal
16 have been exhausted or the time for appeal has expired, the
17 attorney general shall submit a final report regarding the
18 litigation to each member of the house of representatives
19 appropriations committee, each member of the house of

21 appropriations committee, and each member of the senate oversight
22 committee. The report must include both of the following:
23 (a) An accounting of the expenditures.
24 (b) Justification for the expenditures described in
25 subdivision (a).
26 Sec. 3. If the attorney general fails to submit a report in
27 accordance with section 2, both of the following apply:
28 (a) A member of the house of representatives appropriations

6 overdue. A fine collected under this subdivision must be deposited

8 must be paid from an appropriation made to the department of
