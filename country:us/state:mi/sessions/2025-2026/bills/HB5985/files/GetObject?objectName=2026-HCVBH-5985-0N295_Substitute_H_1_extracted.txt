21 health provider repayment program for designated professionals who

1 but the amount repaid in any 1 year shall must not exceed the

3 debt or expenses only for a designated professional who has entered
4 into a written contract with the department that requires the
5 designated professional to engage in the full-time practice of
6 health care services in a health resource shortage area to which he
7 or she the designated professional is assigned by the department
8 for a period equal in years to the number of years for which the
9 department has agreed in the contract to make a debt or expense
10 repayment, or for a period of 2 years, whichever is greater.
11 (2) A debt or expense repayment on behalf of a designated
12 professional under subsection (1) for fulfilling a service
13 obligation for a particular year must be paid in a lump sum at the
14 completion of the service obligation for that year. A designated
15 professional who does not fulfill a service obligation for a
16 particular year forfeits his or her the designated professional's
17 right to the debt or expense repayment or any part of it for that
18 year and the department may treat an agreement for further debt or
19 expense repayment in a subsequent year as void. In its sole
20 discretion, the department may make a debt or expense repayment
21 before or during each year of service if there are extenuating
22 circumstances. In its sole discretion, the department may pay a pro
23 rata amount of an agreed debt or expense repayment to a designated
24 professional or his or her the designated professional's estate if
25 1 of the following occurs before the completion of the designated
26 professional's service obligation:
27 (a) The designated professional dies.
28 (b) The designated professional is unable, by reason of
29 permanent disability, to render the service.

1 (c) Other circumstances prevail that are considered by the
2 department to constitute a compelling reason to consider the
3 service obligation fulfilled.
4 (3) In any year of a debt or expense repayment program, the
5 maximum amount of a debt or expense repayment is $40,000.00 per
6 year. The maximum amount of debt or expense repayment the
7 department may pay on behalf of a designated professional is
8 $300,000.00, paid over a period of 10 years or more. The written
9 contract described in subsection (1) must include the amount the
10 department shall pay on behalf of a designated professional and the
11 amount payable for each year of service.
12 (4) The department may accept funds from any source for the
13 operation of the essential health provider repayment program, and
14 the department shall distribute those funds in a manner consistent
15 with this section.
16 (5) The department shall give the essential health provider
17 repayment program created by this section priority over the other
18 programs created under this part.
