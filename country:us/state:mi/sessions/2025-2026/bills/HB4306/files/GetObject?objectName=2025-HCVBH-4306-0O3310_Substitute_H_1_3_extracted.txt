13 (iii) The epileptic seizure has an established pattern of being
14 purely nocturnal.
15 (iv) The epileptic seizure is secondary to provoking factors
16 that are unlikely to recur, including, but not limited to,
17 metabolic, infectious, or other acute illnesses.
18 (v) The epileptic seizure is related to an acute illness that
19 is reversible.
20 (b) The secretary of state receives from a physician who is
21 treating the individual a written recommendation to reduce or waive
22 the time period required under subsection (1) or (2). A written
23 recommendation under this subdivision may include, but is not
24 limited to, all of the following:
25 (i) Evidence that the individual's epileptic seizure resulted
26 from medical intervention or a medically supervised experiment with
27 a prescribed medication.
28 (ii) Documentation showing that the epileptic seizure occurred
29 as a result of a specific epilepsy syndrome and that the epileptic

13 acute illnesses.
14 (v) Other evidence or documentation that establishes a low

17 authorized to practice medicine or osteopathic medicine and surgery
18 under the law of any state.
