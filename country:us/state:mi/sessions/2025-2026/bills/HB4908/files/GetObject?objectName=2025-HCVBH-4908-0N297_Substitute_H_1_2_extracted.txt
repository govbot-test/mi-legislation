THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF MICHIGAN ENACT:
1 Sec. 1203a. (1) Subject to subsection subsections (5) and (7),
2 an individual shall not perform any form of cosmetology services,
3 with or without compensation, on any individual other than a member
4 of his or her the individual's immediate family without a license
5 under this article. However, this article does not apply to an
6 individual, person, or premises that is licensed under article 11
7 while performing the services of a barber.
8 (2) An individual who is licensed as a cosmetologist under
VMP H02601'25 (H-1) 8ag5tk
2
1 this article may perform hair care services, skin care services,
2 natural hair cultivation, and manicuring services as part of the
3 practice of cosmetology, but shall not perform electrology unless
4 he or she the individual is licensed as an electrologist.
5 (3) The department may license an individual to perform
6 manicuring services, natural hair cultivation, or skin care
7 services in accordance with his or her the individual's training.
8 An individual who is licensed as a manicurist, natural hair
9 culturist, or esthetician shall only perform that particular
10 service and shall not perform any other cosmetology service unless
11 he or she the individual is licensed for that service. An
12 individual who is licensed as a manicurist, natural hair culturist,
13 or esthetician shall not perform electrology unless he or she the
14 individual is licensed as an electrologist.
15 (4) An individual who is licensed as an electrologist shall
16 only perform electrology services and shall not perform any other
17 cosmetology service unless he or she the individual is separately
18 licensed to perform those services.
19 (5) An individual who is a cosmetology student may perform
20 shampoo services in a cosmetology establishment on members of the
21 public without a license under this article if all of the following
22 are met at the time the individual performs the shampooing
23 services:
24 (a) Except as provided in subsection (6), he or she the
25 individual is enrolled in a school of cosmetology.
26 (b) He or she The individual has completed at least not less
27 than the 350 hours of instruction in the school's general
28 cosmetology curriculum that is required under section 1205(5)(c).
29 1205a(1)(e). The instruction must include at least the minimum
VMP H02601'25 (H-1) 8ag5tk
3
1 number of practical applications established by the director by
2 rule.
3 (c) The cosmetology establishment that is employing the
4 individual to perform shampoo services has received written
5 verification from the school of cosmetology that the individual
6 attends, in the form of a letter on the school's letterhead, dated
7 and signed by the director or manager of that school, that states
8 all of the following:
9 (i) The individual's full name.
10 (ii) That the individual is currently enrolled in the school.
11 (iii) That the individual meets the requirements described in
12 subdivision (b).
13 (iv) His or her The individual's expected graduation date.
14 (6) An individual who is performing shampooing services under
15 subsection (5) without a license under this article may continue to
16 perform those services at the cosmetology establishment without a
17 license for a period of not more than 30 days after the date the
18 individual is scheduled to graduate from the school of cosmetology.
19 (7) An individual who is a cosmetology apprentice may perform
20 shampoo services in a cosmetology establishment on members of the
21 public without a license under this article if both of the
22 following are met at the time the individual performs the
23 shampooing services:
24 (a) The individual is enrolled in an apprenticeship program
25 conducted by a person licensed under this article.
26 (b) The individual has completed not less than the 350 hours
27 of instruction in the general cosmetology curriculum that is
28 required under section 1205a(1)(e). The instruction must include at
29 least the minimum number of practical applications established by
VMP H02601'25 (H-1) 8ag5tk
4
1 the director by rule.
2 (8) (7) A cosmetology establishment that employs a cosmetology
3 student or apprentice to perform shampoo services under subsection
4 (5) or (7) must do all of the following:
5 (a) Maintain records of the student's or apprentice's
6 employment and keep the records on file for at least not less than
7 3 years after the end of the employment relationship. The records
8 must include the verification letter described in subsection
9 (5)(c).
10 (b) Allow the department access to the records described in
11 subdivision (a).
12 (c) Ensure that the student or apprentice does not perform
13 cosmetology services other than shampoo services while employed by
14 the establishment.
15 (d) Ensure that a licensed cosmetologist is present in the
16 establishment when the student or apprentice is performing shampoo
17 services.
18 (9) (8) A school of cosmetology that provides a verification
19 letter described in subsection (5)(c) to a cosmetology
20 establishment must retain a copy of the letter in the student's
21 school record for at least not less than 3 years after the
22 student's expected graduation date.
23 (10) (9) As used in this section, "shampoo services" means
24 preparing a customer for a shampoo, or shampooing or blow-drying a
25 customer, for a licensed cosmetologist.
26 Enacting section 1. This amendatory act takes effect 90 days
27 after the date it is enacted into law.
