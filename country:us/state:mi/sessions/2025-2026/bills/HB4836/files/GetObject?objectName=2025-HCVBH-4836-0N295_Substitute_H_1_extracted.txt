SUBSTITUTE FOR
A bill to amend 1979 PA 94, entitled
"The state school aid act of 1979,"
by amending section 104b (MCL 388.1704b), as amended by 2025 PA 15.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF MICHIGAN ENACT:
1 Sec. 104b. (1) In order to receive state aid under this
2 article, a district shall comply with this section and shall
3 administer the Michigan merit examination to pupils in grade 11,
4 and to pupils in grade 12 who did not take the complete Michigan
5 merit examination in grade 11, as provided in this section. The
6 Michigan merit examination consists of a college entrance test,
7 work skills test, and the summative assessment known as the
8 Michigan student test of educational progress (M-STEP).
9 (2) For the purposes of this section, the department of

4 the following:
5 (a) Assessment instruments that measure English language arts,
6 mathematics, reading, and science, and are used by the majority of
7 colleges and universities in this state for entrance purposes. This
8 may include 1 or more writing components. In selecting assessment
9 instruments to fulfill the requirements of this subdivision, the
10 department may consider the degree to which those assessment
11 instruments are aligned to this state's content standards.
12 (b) One or more tests from 1 or more test developers that
13 assess a pupil's ability to apply at least reading and mathematics
14 skills in a manner that is intended to allow employers to use the
15 results in making employment decisions. The department of
16 technology, management, and budget and the superintendent shall
17 ensure that any test or tests selected under this subdivision have
18 all the components necessary to allow a pupil to be eligible to
19 receive the results of a nationally recognized evaluation of
20 workforce readiness if the pupil's test performance is adequate.
21 Beginning with the 2026-2027 school year, a pupil may elect not to
22 take a test described in this subdivision through a waiver that is
23 developed by the department, made available on the department's
24 website, and both signed by the pupil's parent or legal guardian
25 and submitted to the pupil's school district, intermediate school
26 district, or public school academy by not later than February 28 of
27 the school year in which the test will be administered. The
28 department shall consult with an individual who represents business
29 in this state, an individual who represents manufacturing in this

5 public school academy shall ensure that the informational letter is
6 provided to all pupils described in subsection (1) and the parents
7 or legal guardians of those pupils by not later than December 31 of
8 the school year in which the test will be administered.
9 (c) A social studies component.
10 (d) Any other component that is necessary to obtain the
11 approval of the United States Department of Education to use the
12 Michigan merit examination for the purposes of the no child left
13 behind act of 2001, Public Law 107-110, or the every student
14 succeeds act, Public Law 114-95.

16 of the following apply to the Michigan merit examination:

22 failed to meet expectations for each standard, to allow the pupil's
23 parents or legal guardians and teachers to assess and remedy
24 problems before the pupil moves to the next grade.
