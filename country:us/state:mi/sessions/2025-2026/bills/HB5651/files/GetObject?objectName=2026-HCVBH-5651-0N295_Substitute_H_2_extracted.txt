SUBSTITUTE FOR
A bill to amend 1949 PA 300, entitled
"Michigan vehicle code,"
by amending section 627a (MCL 257.627a), as amended by 2016 PA 446.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF MICHIGAN ENACT:
1 Sec. 627a. (1) As used in this section:
2 (a) "Regularly scheduled school session" means that part of a
3 day scheduled for student instruction until final dismissal of the
4 student body for that day.
5 (b) "School" means an educational institution operated by a
6 local school district or by a private, denominational, or parochial
7 organization. School does not include either of the following:
8 (i) An educational institution that the department of education
9 determines has its entire student population in residence at the

2 (ii) An educational institution to which all students are

4 (c) "School zone" means school property on which a school
5 building is located and the adjacent property. A school zone
6 extends not more than 1,000 feet from the school property line in
7 any direction. If 2 or more schools occupy the same property or
8 adjacent properties, 1 of the following applies, as applicable:
9 (i) If the hours of instruction at the schools are the same,
10 then a single combined school zone shall must be established.
11 (ii) If the hours of instruction at the schools are different,
12 overlapping school zones shall must be established.
13 (2) A school zone speed limit on a highway segment in a school
14 zone, which, except as otherwise provided in this subsection, shall
15 must be in force not more than 30 60 minutes before the first

3 (c) Subject to subsection (7), designate any of the following
4 as a an enforcement period during which the school zone speed limit
5 described in this subsection applies, but may not exceed 5 total
6 enforcement periods in 1 day:
7 (i) If a school has an off-campus lunch period, the off-campus
8 lunch period.
9 (ii) If a school has students go off-campus for a field trip or
10 for classes at another school, a career tech center, or a local
11 university, the period during which the students are leaving campus
12 or returning to campus.
13 (3) School zone speed limits shall do not apply to a limited
14 access highway or a highway segment over which a pedestrian
15 overhead walkway is erected, if the walkway is adjacent to school
16 property.
17 (4) Notwithstanding the requirements for a school zone as
18 defined in subsection (1)(c), if a school is located in an area
19 that requires school children to cross a state trunk line highway
20 or county highway that has a speed limit of 35 miles per hour or
21 more to attend that school, the school superintendent may submit a
22 request to the state transportation commission, county road
23 commission, or local authority having jurisdiction over the
24 roadway, as applicable, for a school crossing as permitted under
25 section 613a. If, based on the traffic engineering studies, the
26 road authority determines the need for a lower speed limit, the
27 road authority may designate the crossing as a school zone. Before
28 submitting a request, the school superintendent shall must have

4 immediately below the school zone sign.
5 (6) Louvered signs, digital message signs, and flashing lights
6 may be installed to supplement or replace permanent signs required
7 under this section. Signs erected and maintained as required under
8 this section shall must conform to the Michigan manual on uniform
9 traffic control devices.
10 (7) If additional enforcement periods are designated under
11 subsection (2)(c), a school zone sign with flashing lights
12 indicating that the school zone speed limit is in effect must be
13 installed and be activated during that additional enforcement
14 period.
