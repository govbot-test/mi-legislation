Section 1:
HOUSE SUBSTITUTE FOR
A bill to amend 1972 PA 230, entitled
"Stille-DeRossett-Hale single state construction code act,"
by amending section 28 (MCL 125.1528), as amended by 2020 PA 155,
and by adding section 4i.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF MICHIGAN ENACT:
1 Sec. 4i. (1) All of the following apply to the installation
2 and use of a temporary door locking device or system in a child
3 care center:
4 (a) The device or system must be portable and must not be
5 permanently affixed to the door. Individual parts of the locking
6 assembly of the device or system, including, but not limited to,
7 bolts, stops, brackets, and pins, that do not prevent normal
8 ingress and egress through the door may be permanently mounted on a
GSS S01273'25 (H-2) vistby
2
1 labeled fire door assembly.
2 (b) The locking means can be engaged without opening the door.
3 (c) The door can be unlocked and opened from outside the room
4 with the use of a required tool or key.
5 (d) The locking means does not modify the door closure, panic
6 hardware, or fire exit hardware.
7 (e) The device or system can be disengaged by an individual on
8 the interior side of the door without the use of a required tool or
9 key.
10 (f) Installation and operation of the fixed elements of the
11 device or system is in compliance with 1966 PA 1, MCL 125.1351 to
12 125.1356.
13 (g) A properly trained firefighter, law enforcement officer,
14 or child care center staff member is able to release the device or
15 system from outside the room.
16 (h) The device or system may provide notification of its
17 location and placement in the event of a lockdown.
18 (i) The device or system must not be installed on doors that
19 lead outside the child care center from a corridor.
20 (j) The device or system must be installed per the
21 installation instructions submitted under subsection (2)(a) or
22 (3)(a).
23 (k) Any fasteners or through bolt penetrations to a labeled
24 fire door assembly must be made of steel.
25 (l) Holes, bolts, or fasteners made or used to install the
26 device or system must be the same as stated in the installation
27 instructions submitted under subsection (2)(a) or (3)(a).
28 (2) Except as otherwise provided in subsection (5), the
29 operator of a child care center shall do all of the following:
GSS S01273'25 (H-2) vistby
3
1 (a) Before newly installing a device or system in the child
2 care center, submit to the enforcing agency 2 copies of the floor
3 plans, drawings, diagrams, and installation instructions for all of
4 the following:
5 (i) Plan review.
6 (ii) To obtain plan review approval.
7 (iii) To obtain a permit for installation, as provided in this
8 act and the fire prevention code, 1941 PA 207, MCL 29.1 to 29.33.
9 (iv) To notify the local fire department and the law
10 enforcement agency that has jurisdiction over the child care center
11 of the intended installation or use, and location, of the device or
12 system.
13 (b) Before using a newly installed device or system in the
14 child care center, notify the enforcing agency that the device or
15 system is ready for inspection and receive written approval from
16 the enforcing agency that the device or system is approved for use.
17 (c) In the child care center where the device or system is
18 installed or being used, provide in-service training to child care
19 center staff members on the use of the device or system and
20 maintain a record that verifies this training.
21 (d) Beginning 90 days after the effective date of the
22 amendatory act that added this section, ensure that the device or
23 system is engaged for only a finite period of time, as determined
24 by the operator of the child care center in accordance with an
25 emergency procedure plan adopted in accordance with licensing rules
26 for child care centers. The emergency procedure plan described
27 under this subdivision must include a description of the
28 installation and use of the device or system used by the operator
29 of the child care center.

1 (3) Except as otherwise provided in subsection (5), if a child
2 care center has an existing temporary door locking device or system
3 that was installed before the effective date of the amendatory act
4 that added this section, the enforcing agency shall approve the
5 device or system if all of the following requirements are met:
6 (a) Not later than 1 year after the effective date of the
7 amendatory act that added this section, the operator of the child
8 care center submits to the enforcing agency 2 copies of the
9 drawings, diagrams, and installation instructions showing that the
10 device or system meets the requirements of subsection (1).
11 (b) The operator of the child care center applies for and
12 obtains any necessary permits as described in subsection (2)(a).
13 (c) The enforcing agency inspects the installed device or
14 system and determines it meets the requirements of subsection (1).

16 temporary door locking device or system has been installed as
17 described under subsection (3) shall comply with the requirements
18 of subsection (2)(c) and (d) and notify the local fire department
19 and the law enforcement agency that has jurisdiction over the child
20 care center of the location of the device or system.
21 (5) Notwithstanding any rules, regulations, or guidelines
22 promulgated by the department, the operator of a child care center

25 architect or licensed professional engineer in accordance with

1 in a child care center.
2 (b) Receives approval for an existing temporary door locking
3 device or system that was installed in a child care center before
4 the effective date of the amendatory act that added this section.
5 (6) A temporary door locking device or system may be installed
6 in any child care center or addition to a child care center,
7 regardless of the number of stories of the building or addition, or
8 as a component in the construction, reconstruction, or remodeling
9 of a child care center or addition to a child care center,
10 regardless of the costs of that construction, reconstruction, or
11 remodeling.
12 (7) As used in this section:
13 (a) "Child care center" means that term as defined under
14 section 1 of 1973 PA 116, MCL 722.111.
15 (b) "Panic hardware" means fire door hardware, handles, or
16 push bars that allow for the opening of a door with a single
17 movement.
18 (c) "Temporary door locking device or system" or "device or
19 system" means an anchoring mechanism or system installed on the
20 interior side of a door that, when engaged, secures the door
21 against forced entry.
22 Sec. 28. (1) Any provision of any of the following that is
23 inconsistent or in conflict with this act is superseded to the
24 extent of the inconsistency or conflict:
25 (a) Section 34 of 1933 (Ex Sess) PA 18, MCL 125.684.
26 (b) Article 11 of the skilled trades regulation act, 2016 PA
27 407, MCL 339.6101 to 339.6133.

18 407, MCL 339.5901 to 339.5947.
19 (e) 1967 PA 227, MCL 408.801 to 408.824.

21 (4) A labelled labeled fire door assembly with a temporary
22 door locking device or system that is installed under in accordance
