HOUSE BILL NO. 5924

A bill to regulate the audio of commercial advertisements by video streaming services; to provide for the powers and duties of certain state governmental officers; and to prescribe civil sanctions.

the people of the state of michigan enact:

Sec. 1. This act may be cited as the "commercial advertising loudness mitigation act".

Sec. 3. As used in this act:

(a) "Commercial advertisement" means material that advertises the commercial availability or quality of property, goods, or services.

(b) "Person" means an individual or a partnership, corporation, limited liability company, association, governmental entity, or other legal entity.

(c) "Video content" means audiovisual material that is not a commercial advertisement and that a video streaming service makes available for viewing by a consumer.

(d) "Video programming" means that term as defined in 47 USC 613.

(e) "Video streaming service" means, except as otherwise provided in subdivision (f), a person that makes available directly to a consumer in this state, through a distribution method that uses internet protocol, either of the following:

(i) Video programming.

(ii) Video content.

(f) Video streaming service does not include a television broadcast station, cable operator, or other multichannel video programing distributor, or a person that serves video programming or video content without commercial advertisements.

Sec. 5. Beginning July 1, 2027, a video streaming service shall not transmit the audio of a commercial advertisement louder than the video programming or video content that the commercial advertisement accompanies.

Sec. 7. A video streaming service that violates this act is subject to a civil fine of not more than $10,000.00 for each violation. The attorney general may bring an action to collect the fine. A fine collected must be deposited in the general fund.
