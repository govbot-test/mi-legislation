Section 1:
SUBSTITUTE FOR
A bill to amend 1974 PA 258, entitled
"Mental health code,"
by amending section 273b (MCL 330.1273b), as added by 2020 PA 402.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF MICHIGAN ENACT:
1 Sec. 273b. (1) Subject to appropriation, the department shall
2 create and operate a competitive grant program to provide grants to
3 recovery community organizations. Each as provided in this section.
4 Subject to subsection (5), each year, from available funds, the
5 department shall award $5,000,000.00 in grants under this section
6 to the following:
7 (a) Not fewer than 19 certified recovery community
8 organizations to offer or expand recovery support center services
9 or recovery community center services to serve individuals seeking
EMR H05009'25 (H-1) d83j20
2
1 long-term recovery from substance use disorders.
2 (b) Nonprofit associations in this state that meet all of the
3 following requirements:
4 (i) Have recovery community organizations as members.
5 (ii) Have a primary purpose of educating about, advocating for,
6 and increasing awareness of recovery.
7 (iii) Connect recovery community organizations within this state
8 to support the certification of recovery community organizations
9 and to provide technical assistance to recovery community
10 organizations awarded funding under this section.
11 (2) Each Subject to subsection (5), each grant that the
12 department awards under this section shall not exceed $150,000.00
13 must be as follows:
14 (a) If the applicant is a recovery community organization, a
15 minimum of $250,000.00 per applicant. However, the department shall
16 not award a grant under this section to a recovery community
17 organization in an amount that exceeds 50% of the organization's
18 operating budget for the calendar year immediately preceding the
19 date of the application.
20 (b) If the applicant is a nonprofit association described in
21 subsection (1)(b), $250,000.00.
22 (3) In awarding a grant under this section, the department
23 shall place priority on prioritize recovery community organizations
24 that do meet all of the following requirements:
25 (a) Provide recovery support navigation that includes the
26 following:
27 (i) Multiple recovery pathways.
28 (ii) Assistance for individuals navigating recovery resources
29 such as detoxification, treatment, recovery housing, support
EMR H05009'25 (H-1) d83j20
3
1 groups, peer support, and family support.
2 (iii) The promotion of community wellness and engagement.
3 (iv) Recovery advocacy that provides hope and encourages
4 recovery.
5 (v) A peer-led, peer-driven organization that offers recovery
6 to any individual seeking recovery from addiction.
7 (b) Provide recovery outreach education that includes the
8 following:
9 (i) On-site recovery education in the workplace.
10 (ii) All-staff employee meetings.
11 (iii) On-site support for employees and family members.
12 (iv) Connections for employees and family members of employees
13 suffering from addiction to local recovery resources such as
14 treatment, recovery housing, and support groups.
15 (v) Connections with employers to provide recovery advocacy.
16 (c) Provide recovery activities and events that include the
17 following:
18 (i) Safe, ongoing recovery activities and events.
19 (ii) Opportunities to volunteer and participate in activities
20 and events.
21 (iii) Opportunities for family members and supporters of
22 recovery to be involved.
23 (iv) Meetings and activities on nutrition, health, and
24 wellness.
25 (v) Meetings and activities on mindfulness, meditation, and
26 yoga.
27 (d) Are a member of a nonprofit association described in
28 subsection (1)(b).
EMR H05009'25 (H-1) d83j20
4
1 (4) Beginning March 31, 2027, a grant recipient under this
2 section shall file an annual report with the chairperson of each
3 standing committee that has jurisdiction over health policy issues
4 and to the chairpersons of the house of representatives and senate
5 appropriations committees. The report must include all of the
6 following information:
7 (a) An explanation of how grant funds are being spent.
8 (b) If the grant recipient is a recovery community
9 organization, all of the following:
10 (i) The staff-to-participant ratio.
11 (ii) A description of the types of recovery support services
12 offered to participants.
13 (iii) The number of participants receiving recovery support
14 services during the calendar year and an explanation of how the
15 participants and services were calculated and reported.
16 (iv) The average rate of improvement in participant recovery
17 capital measures for participants served during the previous
18 calendar year.
19 (c) The operating budget of the grant recipient for the
20 calendar year covered by the report, with details on each source of
21 the grant recipient's income.
22 (5) If there are insufficient funds available for the grant
23 award amounts described in this section, the department may reduce
24 the total number of grants awarded under this section to ensure
25 that each grant awarded is at least $150,000.00.
26 (6) This section does not apply beginning October 1, 2031.
