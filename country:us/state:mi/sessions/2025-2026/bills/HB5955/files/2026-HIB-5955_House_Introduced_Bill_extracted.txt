Section 1:
HOUSE BILL NO. 5955 A bill to regulate temporary labor service agencies, the clients of temporary labor service agencies, and temporary laborers; to prohibit retaliation for exercising rights granted under this act; to provide for the powers and duties of certain state and local governmental officers and entities; to provide for the promulgation of rules; and to provide remedies and sanctions. the people of the state of michigan enact: Sec. 1. This act may be cited as the "temporary laborer rights act". Sec. 3. As used in this act: (a) "Client" means a person that contracts with a temporary labor service organization to obtain the services of temporary laborers. (b) "Department" means the department of licensing and regulatory affairs. (c) "Job site" means the physical location at which a temporary laborer performs work for a client under a contract between a temporary labor service agency and the client. (d) "Temporary labor" means work performed by a temporary laborer at a job site, the duration of which may be specific or undefined, under a contract between a temporary labor service agency and a client. Temporary labor does not include professional work. (e) "Temporary labor service agency" means a person engaged in the business of employing temporary laborers to provide services, for a fee, to or for a client under a contract with the client. (f) "Temporary laborer" means an individual who contracts for temporary labor with a temporary labor service agency. Sec. 5. (1) A temporary labor service agency shall provide a temporary laborer, at the time of dispatch to a client, with a notice that includes all of the following on a form approved by the department: (a) The name of each temporary laborer dispatched to the same job site. (b) The name and nature of the work to be performed and the types of equipment, protective clothing, and training that are required for the work. (c) The wage rate for the work. (d) The name and address of the client and job site. (e) The terms of the transportation to the job site. (f) Whether a meal or equipment is provided by the temporary labor service agency or the client and the cost of the meal and equipment. (2) If a temporary laborer is assigned to the same assignment for more than 1 day, the temporary labor service agency is required to provide the notice under subsection (1) on the first day and any day the terms of the notice change. (3) If a temporary laborer is not placed with a client or otherwise contracted to work for a day that the temporary laborer reports to the temporary labor service agency, the temporary labor service agency shall, on the request of the temporary laborer, provide the temporary laborer with confirmation that the temporary laborer sought work. The confirmation must be signed by an employee of the temporary labor service agency and include the name of the agency, the name and address of the temporary laborer, and the date and time that the temporary laborer received the confirmation. (4) A temporary labor service agency shall not provide a temporary laborer to a job site where a strike, lockout, or other labor dispute exists. (5) The department shall encourage a temporary labor service agency to hire employees who can communicate the information required under subsections (1) to (3) in the languages that are generally understood in the geographic area of the temporary labor service agency. Sec. 7. (1) A temporary labor service agency shall maintain a record of each transaction with a client that includes all of the following information: (a) The name, address, and telephone number of the client, the job sites to which temporary laborers were sent by the temporary labor service agency, and the date of the transaction. (b) All of the following information for each temporary laborer: (i) The temporary laborer's name and address. (ii) The job sites to which the temporary laborer was assigned. (iii) The number of hours worked by the temporary laborer and the temporary laborer's wage rate. (iv) The name and nature of the work performed by the temporary laborer. (c) The name and title of the employee responsible for the transaction. (d) The number of hours billed to the client. (e) The specific qualifications or attributes of a temporary laborer requested by the client. (f) Copies of all contracts with the client and copies of all invoices for the client. (g) Copies of all notices provided under section 5(1). (h) All deductions from each temporary laborer's compensation made by the client or temporary labor service agency, including, but not limited to, the temporary laborer's transportation, food, equipment, withheld income tax, and withheld Social Security payments. (i) Verification of the actual cost of any equipment or meal charged to a temporary laborer. (j) The race and gender of each temporary laborer, as provided by the temporary laborer. (k) Any other information as required by the department. (2) A client shall remit the information described in subsection (1) in its possession to a temporary labor service agency not later than 7 days after the last day of the work week worked by a temporary laborer. The failure of a client to remit this information to a temporary labor service agency is not a defense to a violation of this section. (3) A temporary labor service agency shall maintain records under subsection (1) for 7 years after the date the records are created. A temporary labor service agency shall make the records available for inspection by the department during normal business hours. A temporary labor service agency shall make the records described in subsection (1)(a), (b), (c), (g), (h), and (i) available for review or copying by the respective temporary laborer during normal business hours not later than 5 days after the temporary laborer requests the records. A temporary labor service agency shall make forms, in duplicate, for requests made under this subsection available to temporary laborers at the dispatch office, and shall provide a copy of the request form to the temporary laborer. (4) A person shall not make a false, inaccurate, or incomplete entry into a record required under this section or delete required information from a record. A client that does not remit records to the temporary labor service agency under subsection (2) violates this section unless the client was precluded from remitting the records for reasons beyond the client's control. Sec. 9. A temporary labor service agency or client shall not charge a temporary laborer for a meal that the temporary labor service agency or client provides to the temporary laborer and that the temporary laborer does not consume. If a temporary labor service agency or client provides a meal to a temporary laborer and the temporary laborer consumes, wholly or partially, the meal, the temporary labor service agency or client may charge the temporary laborer only the actual cost of the meal. The purchase of a meal must not be a condition of employment for a temporary laborer. Sec. 11. (1) A temporary labor service agency or client shall not charge a fee to transport a temporary laborer to or from a job site. A temporary labor service agency is responsible for the conduct and performance of a person that transports a temporary laborer to or from the temporary labor service agency to a job site, unless the person is any of the following: (a) A public mass transportation system. (b) A common carrier. (c) The temporary laborer providing the temporary laborer's own transportation. (d) Selected exclusively by and at the sole choice of the temporary laborer for transportation in a vehicle not owned or operated by the temporary labor service agency. (2) If a temporary labor service agency provides transportation to a temporary laborer or refers a temporary laborer to a person for transportation under subsection (3), the temporary labor service agency shall not allow a motor vehicle to be used for the transportation if the temporary labor service agency knows or should know that the motor vehicle is unsafe or not equipped as required under this act. This subsection does not apply to a motor vehicle that is any of the following: (a) The property of a public mass transportation system. (b) The property of a common carrier. (c) The temporary laborer's personal vehicle. (d) The vehicle of a temporary laborer used to transport other temporary laborers and that is selected exclusively by and at the sole choice of the temporary laborer for transportation. (3) A temporary labor service agency shall not refer a temporary laborer to a person for transportation to or from a job site unless that person is a public mass transportation system or will provide the transportation at no charge. Directing a temporary laborer to accept transportation from a specific temporary laborer as a condition of work is a referral by the temporary labor service agency under this subsection. Any mention or discussion by a temporary labor service agency of the cost of transportation provided by another temporary laborer is a referral under this subsection. Informing a temporary laborer of the availability of transportation provided by another temporary laborer is not a referral under this subsection. (4) A temporary labor service agency that provides transportation for a temporary laborer to a job site shall also provide the temporary laborer transportation from the job site, unless the temporary laborer agrees, before departure to the job site, to obtain an alternative means of transportation from the job site. Sec. 13. (1) Except as otherwise provided in this subsection, a temporary labor service agency or client shall not charge a temporary laborer for any safety equipment, clothing, accessory, or other item temporarily provided to a temporary laborer that is required by law, custom, or the client to perform the work. If the temporary laborer does not return the item, the temporary labor service agency or client may charge the temporary laborer not more than the market value of the item. (2) If a temporary labor service agency makes available to a temporary laborer for purchase an item other than those described in subsection (1), the temporary labor service agency shall not charge more than the actual market value for the item. Sec. 15. (1) A temporary labor service agency shall include on a temporary laborer's paycheck stub, or with the temporary laborer's paycheck stub on a form approved by the department, all of the following information: (a) The name, address, and telephone number of each client for which the temporary laborer worked. If this information is provided on the temporary laborer's paycheck stub, a code for each client may be used if the required information for each coded client is made available to the temporary laborer. (b) The number of hours worked by the temporary laborer at each client each day during the pay period. If the temporary laborer is assigned to work at the same job site of the same client for multiple days in the same work week, the temporary labor service agency may record a summary of hours worked at that client's job site if the first and last day of that work week are also identified. (c) The wage rate for each hour worked, including premium rates and bonuses. (d) The total pay period earnings. (e) All deductions from the temporary laborer's compensation made by the client or the temporary labor service agency, and the purpose for which deductions were made, including, but not limited to, transportation, food, equipment, withheld income tax, and withheld Social Security payments. (f) Any additional information as required by the department. (2) A client shall provide to a temporary laborer, at the end of a work day, a work verification form that includes the date, the temporary laborer's name, the job site location, and the hours worked on that day. The department shall prescribe a form for a client to use under this subsection. A client that violates this subsection may be ordered to pay a civil fine of not more than $500.00. For a second and any subsequent violation, a client may be ordered to pay a civil fine of not more than $2,500.00. Each violation of this subsection for each temporary laborer and for each day the violation continues is a separate and distinct violation. The prosecutor of the county in which the violation occurred or the attorney general may bring an action to collect the fine. (3) Not later than February 1 of each year, a temporary labor service agency shall provide a temporary laborer with an earnings summary for the immediately preceding calendar year. A temporary labor service agency shall, at the time of each wage payment, give notice to a temporary laborer of the availability of the annual earnings summary or, as an alternative, post a notice of the availability of the annual earnings summary in a conspicuous place in its public access area. (4) A temporary labor service agency or client shall not charge a temporary laborer for the expense of conducting a consumer report, as that term is defined in 15 USC 1681a(d), a criminal background check, or a drug test. (5) The total amount deducted from a temporary laborer's compensation by a temporary labor service agency or a client for meals or equipment must not cause a temporary laborer's hourly wage rate to fall below the minimum wage rate established under the improved workforce opportunity wage act, 2018 PA 337, MCL 408.931 to 408.945. (6) If a temporary laborer who is contracted by a temporary labor service agency to work at a client's job site does not, at the direction of the client, perform work for the client, the temporary labor service agency shall pay the temporary laborer an amount equal to not less than 4 hours of pay at the agreed on wage rate. However, if the temporary labor service agency contracts the temporary laborer to work at another job site during the same shift, the temporary labor service agency shall pay the temporary laborer an amount equal to not less than 2 hours of pay at the agreed on wage rate. Sec. 17. A temporary labor service agency shall attempt to place a temporary laborer into a permanent position with a client if the client informs the temporary labor service agency of its plan to hire a permanent employee for a position similar to the positions for which temporary laborers are being provided by the agency at the same job site. Sec. 19. A temporary labor service agency shall not restrict the right of a temporary laborer to accept a permanent position with a client to whom the temporary laborer has been referred for work or restrict the right of the client to offer employment to the temporary laborer. If the temporary laborer accepts a permanent position with the client, the temporary labor service agency may charge a placement fee to the client. The fee must not exceed an amount equal to the total daily commission rate the temporary labor service agency would have received over a 60-day period, reduced by an amount equal to the daily commission rate the temporary labor service agency is entitled to receive for each day the temporary laborer performed work for the temporary labor service agency in the immediately preceding 12 months. Sec. 21. A temporary labor service agency shall provide adequate seating in the public access area of the offices of the temporary labor service agency. All notice posting required under this act must be posted in the public access area. The public access area must allow for access to restrooms and water. Sec. 23. (1) A temporary labor service agency shall register with the department in accordance with this section and the procedures established by the department. A temporary labor service agency shall provide proof of an unemployment insurance agency account number issued under the Michigan employment security act, 1936 (Ex Sess) PA 1, MCL 421.1 to 421.75, and proof of insurance that complies with section 611 of the worker's disability compensation act of 1969, 1969 PA 317, MCL 418.611. If a temporary labor service agency's insurance coverage lapses, the temporary labor service agency shall report the lapse of coverage to the department, and the department shall suspend the temporary labor service agency's registration until the lapse ends. (2) The department may assess a temporary labor service agency a nonrefundable registration fee of not more than $1,000.00 per year and a nonrefundable fee of not more than $250.00 for each branch office or location where the temporary labor service agency regularly contracts with temporary laborers for services. The department may charge an additional fee if the temporary labor service agency issues or delivers a check to the department that is not honored by the financial institution on which it is drawn. (3) At the time of registration and every year after that, a temporary labor service agency shall submit to the department a report that includes the information listed in section 7(1), categorized by branch office, in the aggregate for all temporary laborers, on a form as prescribed by the department. The department shall aggregate the information submitted by all temporary labor service agencies and make the information available to the public only on a municipal and county basis. The department shall remove all identifying data before making the information available. The information and reports submitted to the department under this subsection are exempt from disclosure under the freedom of information act, 1976 PA 442, MCL 15.231 to 15.246. As used in this subsection, "identifying data" means information that does 1 or both of the following: (a) Provides information regarding a temporary laborer or temporary laborer's service agency's identity. (b) Identifies clients contracting with a temporary labor service agency or other information that can be traced to a specific temporary labor service agency or its client. (4) The department shall create and maintain all of the following at regular intervals on its public website: (a) A list of all registered temporary labor service agencies whose registration is in good standing. (b) A list of temporary labor service agencies whose registration has been suspended, including the reason for the suspension, the date the suspension was initiated, and, if known, the date the suspension will be lifted. (c) A list of temporary labor service agencies whose registration has been revoked, including the reason for the revocation and the date the registration was revoked. (5) A temporary labor service agency shall post at each of its branch offices, in a position easily accessible to all temporary laborers, notices as supplied and required by the department that include a copy or summary of the provisions of this act and a toll-free telephone number for persons to contact the department regarding this act. (6) A temporary labor service agency that violates subsection (1) may be ordered to pay a civil fine of not more than $500.00. For a continuing violation, each day the violation continues is a separate and distinct violation. The prosecutor of the county in which the violation occurred or the attorney general may bring an action to collect the fine. Sec. 25. A person is not eligible to register a temporary labor service agency under this act if the person or any of its officers, directors, partners, or managers or any owner of 25% or more of a beneficial interest has been involved, as an owner, officer, director, partner, or manager of a temporary labor service agency whose registration has been revoked or suspended, without being reinstated, within the 5 years immediately preceding the filing of the registration. Sec. 27. (1) The department shall enforce this act. The department shall conduct investigations in connection with the administration and enforcement of this act. To ensure compliance with this act, an investigator of the department may inspect, at any reasonable time, a location covered by this act or a contract for the employment of a temporary laborer entered into by a client. (2) The department shall conduct all hearings under this act in accordance with the contested case procedures of the administrative procedures act of 1969, 1969 PA 306, MCL 24.201 to 24.328, to determine whether a person violated this act. If the department determines that a person violated this act, the department may do any of the following: (a) Issue and cause to be served on a person an order to cease and desist from further violating this act. (b) Take appropriate action to eliminate the effect of the violation. (c) Deny, suspend, or revoke a registration under this act. Sec 29. An individual aggrieved by a violation of this act may do either of the following: (a) File a complaint with the department. (b) Bring a civil action for appropriate injunctive relief or damages, or both, in the circuit court for the county where the alleged violation occurred or where the person against whom the civil complaint is filed resides or where the person's principal place of business is located. Filing a complaint with the department under subdivision (a) is not a prerequisite or a bar to bringing an action under this subdivision. Sec. 31. (1) A client shall not enter into a contract with a temporary labor service agency unless the temporary labor service agency is registered under this act. A client shall verify a temporary labor service agency's registration with the department before entering into a contract with the temporary labor service agency, and on March 1 and September 1 of each year. A temporary labor service agency shall provide each of its clients with proof of valid registration at the time of entering into a contract. A temporary labor service agency shall notify, by telephone and in writing, each temporary laborer it employs and each client with whom it has a contract not later than 24 hours after a denial, suspension, or revocation of its registration. If a temporary labor service agency's registration is denied, suspended, or revoked, the temporary labor service agency may not enforce a contract with a client beginning on the date the denial, suspension, or revocation becomes effective and until the temporary labor service agency becomes registered and is considered in good standing by the department. (2) On the request of a client, the department shall provide to the client a list of registered temporary labor service agencies. The department shall provide on its public website a list of registered temporary labor service agencies. A client may rely on information provided by the department or maintained on the department's public website and must be held harmless if the information maintained or provided by the department was inaccurate. (3) A client that violates this section may be ordered to pay a civil fine of not more than $500.00. Each day during which a client contracts with a temporary labor service agency not registered under this act is a separate and distinct violation. The prosecutor of the county in which the violation occurred or the attorney general may bring an action to collect the fine. Sec. 33. A person shall not retaliate or discriminate against an individual because the individual does or is about to do any of the following: (a) File a complaint under this act. (b) Testify, assist, or participate in an investigation, proceeding, or action concerning a violation of this act. (c) Oppose a violation of this act. Sec. 35. The department may promulgate rules to implement this act under the administrative procedures act of 1969, 1969 PA 306, MCL 24.201 to 24.328.

Sec. 19. A temporary labor service agency shall not restrict the right of a temporary laborer to accept a permanent position with a client to whom the temporary laborer has been referred for work or restrict the right of the client to offer employment to the temporary laborer. If the temporary laborer accepts a permanent position with the client, the temporary labor service agency may charge a placement fee to the client. The fee must not exceed an amount equal to the total daily commission rate the temporary labor service agency would have received over a 60-day period, reduced by an amount equal to the daily commission rate the temporary labor service agency is entitled to receive for each day the temporary laborer performed work for the temporary labor service agency in the immediately preceding 12 months. Sec. 21. A temporary labor service agency shall provide adequate seating in the public access area of the offices of the temporary labor service agency. All notice posting required under this act must be posted in the public access area. The public access area must allow for access to restrooms and water. Sec. 23. (1) A temporary labor service agency shall register with the department in accordance with this section and the procedures established by the department. A temporary labor service agency shall provide proof of an unemployment insurance agency account number issued under the Michigan employment security act, 1936 (Ex Sess) PA 1, MCL 421.1 to 421.75, and proof of insurance that complies with section 611 of the worker's disability compensation act of 1969, 1969 PA 317, MCL 418.611. If a temporary labor service agency's insurance coverage lapses, the temporary labor service agency shall report the lapse of coverage to the department, and the department shall suspend the temporary labor service agency's registration until the lapse ends. (2) The department may assess a temporary labor service agency a nonrefundable registration fee of not more than $1,000.00 per year and a nonrefundable fee of not more than $250.00 for each branch office or location where the temporary labor service agency regularly contracts with temporary laborers for services. The department may charge an additional fee if the temporary labor service agency issues or delivers a check to the department that is not honored by the financial institution on which it is drawn. (3) At the time of registration and every year after that, a temporary labor service agency shall submit to the department a report that includes the information listed in section 7(1), categorized by branch office, in the aggregate for all temporary laborers, on a form as prescribed by the department. The department shall aggregate the information submitted by all temporary labor service agencies and make the information available to the public only on a municipal and county basis. The department shall remove all identifying data before making the information available. The information and reports submitted to the department under this subsection are exempt from disclosure under the freedom of information act, 1976 PA 442, MCL 15.231 to 15.246. As used in this subsection, "identifying data" means information that does 1 or both of the following: (a) Provides information regarding a temporary laborer or temporary laborer's service agency's identity. (b) Identifies clients contracting with a temporary labor service agency or other information that can be traced to a specific temporary labor service agency or its client. (4) The department shall create and maintain all of the following at regular intervals on its public website: (a) A list of all registered temporary labor service agencies whose registration is in good standing. (b) A list of temporary labor service agencies whose registration has been suspended, including the reason for the suspension, the date the suspension was initiated, and, if known, the date the suspension will be lifted. (c) A list of temporary labor service agencies whose registration has been revoked, including the reason for the revocation and the date the registration was revoked. (5) A temporary labor service agency shall post at each of its branch offices, in a position easily accessible to all temporary laborers, notices as supplied and required by the department that include a copy or summary of the provisions of this act and a toll-free telephone number for persons to contact the department regarding this act. (6) A temporary labor service agency that violates subsection (1) may be ordered to pay a civil fine of not more than $500.00. For a continuing violation, each day the violation continues is a separate and distinct violation. The prosecutor of the county in which the violation occurred or the attorney general may bring an action to collect the fine. Sec. 25. A person is not eligible to register a temporary labor service agency under this act if the person or any of its officers, directors, partners, or managers or any owner of 25% or more of a beneficial interest has been involved, as an owner, officer, director, partner, or manager of a temporary labor service agency whose registration has been revoked or suspended, without being reinstated, within the 5 years immediately preceding the filing of the registration. Sec. 27. (1) The department shall enforce this act. The department shall conduct investigations in connection with the administration and enforcement of this act. To ensure compliance with this act, an investigator of the department may inspect, at any reasonable time, a location covered by this act or a contract for the employment of a temporary laborer entered into by a client. (2) The department shall conduct all hearings under this act in accordance with the contested case procedures of the administrative procedures act of 1969, 1969 PA 306, MCL 24.201 to 24.328, to determine whether a person violated this act. If the department determines that a person violated this act, the department may do any of the following: (a) Issue and cause to be served on a person an order to cease and desist from further violating this act. (b) Take appropriate action to eliminate the effect of the violation. (c) Deny, suspend, or revoke a registration under this act. Sec 29. An individual aggrieved by a violation of this act may do either of the following: (a) File a complaint with the department. (b) Bring a civil action for appropriate injunctive relief or damages, or both, in the circuit court for the county where the alleged violation occurred or where the person against whom the civil complaint is filed resides or where the person's principal place of business is located. Filing a complaint with the department under subdivision (a) is not a prerequisite or a bar to bringing an action under this subdivision. Sec. 31. (1) A client shall not enter into a contract with a temporary labor service agency unless the temporary labor service agency is registered under this act. A client shall verify a temporary labor service agency's registration with the department before entering into a contract with the temporary labor service agency, and on March 1 and September 1 of each year. A temporary labor service agency shall provide each of its clients with proof of valid registration at the time of entering into a contract. A temporary labor service agency shall notify, by telephone and in writing, each temporary laborer it employs and each client with whom it has a contract not later than 24 hours after a denial, suspension, or revocation of its registration. If a temporary labor service agency's registration is denied, suspended, or revoked, the temporary labor service agency may not enforce a contract with a client beginning on the date the denial, suspension, or revocation becomes effective and until the temporary labor service agency becomes registered and is considered in good standing by the department. (2) On the request of a client, the department shall provide to the client a list of registered temporary labor service agencies. The department shall provide on its public website a list of registered temporary labor service agencies. A client may rely on information provided by the department or maintained on the department's public website and must be held harmless if the information maintained or provided by the department was inaccurate. (3) A client that violates this section may be ordered to pay a civil fine of not more than $500.00. Each day during which a client contracts with a temporary labor service agency not registered under this act is a separate and distinct violation. The prosecutor of the county in which the violation occurred or the attorney general may bring an action to collect the fine. Sec. 33. A person shall not retaliate or discriminate against an individual because the individual does or is about to do any of the following: (a) File a complaint under this act. (b) Testify, assist, or participate in an investigation, proceeding, or action concerning a violation of this act. (c) Oppose a violation of this act. Sec. 35. The department may promulgate rules to implement this act under the administrative procedures act of 1969, 1969 PA 306, MCL 24.201 to 24.328.
