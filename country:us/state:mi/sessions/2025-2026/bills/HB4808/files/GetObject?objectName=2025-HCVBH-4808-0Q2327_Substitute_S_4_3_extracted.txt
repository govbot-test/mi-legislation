SENATE SUBSTITUTE FOR
A bill to amend 1976 PA 267, entitled
"Open meetings act,"
by amending section 3a (MCL 15.263a), as amended by 2025 PA 54.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF MICHIGAN ENACT:

2 part, electronically by telephonic or video conferencing in
3 compliance with this section and, except as otherwise required in
4 this section, all of the provisions of this act applicable to a
5 nonelectronic meeting, is permitted by this act in the following
6 circumstances:
7 (a) Before March 31, 2021 and retroactive to March 18, 2020,
8 any circumstances, including, but not limited to, any of the
9 circumstances requiring accommodation of absent members described

6 emergency or state of disaster, this subdivision applies only as
7 follows:
8 (i) To permit the electronic attendance of a member of the
9 public body who resides in the affected area.
10 (ii) To permit the electronic meeting of a public body that
11 usually holds its meetings in the affected area.
12 (c) Subject to subdivisions (d) to (g), (h), after December
13 31, 2021, only in the circumstances requiring accommodation of
14 members absent due to military duty as described in section 3(2).

17 limited to, any of the circumstances requiring accommodation of

1 (2) A meeting of a public body held electronically under this
2 section must be conducted in a manner that permits 2-way
3 communication so that members of the public body can hear and be
4 heard by other members of the public body, and so that public
5 participants can hear members of the public body and can be heard
6 by members of the public body and other participants during a
7 public comment period. A public body may use technology to
8 facilitate typed public comments during the meeting submitted by
9 members of the public participating in the meeting that may be read
10 to or shared with members of the public body and other participants
11 to satisfy the requirement under this subsection that members of
12 the public be heard by others during the electronic meeting and the
13 requirement under section 3(5) that members of the public be
14 permitted to address the electronic meeting.
15 (3) Except as otherwise provided in subsection (8), a physical
16 place is not required for an electronic meeting held under this
17 section, and members of a public body and members of the public
18 participating electronically in a meeting held under this section
19 that occurs in a physical place are to be considered present and in
20 attendance at the meeting for all purposes.
21 (4) If a public body directly or indirectly maintains an
22 official internet presence that includes monthly or more frequent
23 updates of public meeting agendas or minutes, the public body
24 shall, in addition to any other notices that may be required under
25 this act, post advance notice of a meeting held electronically
26 under this section on a portion of the public body's website that
27 is fully accessible to the public. The public notice on the website
28 must be included on either the homepage or on a separate webpage
29 dedicated to public notices for nonregularly scheduled or

5
1 electronic public meetings that is accessible through a prominent
2 and conspicuous link on the website's homepage that clearly
3 describes its purpose for public notification of nonregularly
4 scheduled or electronic public meetings. Subject to the
5 requirements of this section, any scheduled meeting of a public
6 body may be held as an electronic meeting under this section if a
7 notice consistent with this section is posted at least 18 hours
8 before the meeting begins. Notice of a meeting of a public body
9 held electronically must clearly explain all of the following:
10 (a) Why the public body is meeting electronically.
11 (b) How members of the public may participate in the meeting
12 electronically. If a telephone number, internet address, or both
13 are needed to participate, that information must be provided
14 specifically.
15 (c) How members of the public may contact members of the
16 public body to provide input or ask questions on any business that
17 will come before the public body at the meeting.
18 (d) How persons with disabilities may participate in the
19 meeting.
20 (5) Beginning on October 16, 2020, if an agenda exists for an
21 electronic meeting held under this section by a public body that
22 directly or indirectly maintains an official internet presence that
23 includes monthly or more frequent updates of public meeting agendas
24 or minutes, the public body shall, on a portion of the website that
25 is fully accessible to the public, make the agenda available to the
26 public at least 2 hours before the electronic meeting begins. This
27 publication of the agenda does not prohibit subsequent amendment of
28 the agenda at the meeting.
29 (6) A public body shall not, as a condition of participating

6
1 in an electronic meeting of the public body held under this
2 section, require a person to register or otherwise provide the
3 person's name or other information or otherwise to fulfill a
4 condition precedent to attendance, other than mechanisms
5 established and required by the public body necessary to permit the
6 person to participate in a public comment period of the meeting.
7 (7) Members of the general public otherwise participating in a
8 meeting of a public body held electronically under this section are
9 to be excluded from participation in a closed session of the public
10 body held electronically during that meeting if the closed session
11 is convened and held in compliance with the requirements of this
12 act applicable to a closed session.
13 (8) At a meeting held under this section that accommodates
14 members absent due to military duty or a medical condition, only
15 those members absent due to military duty or a medical condition
16 may participate remotely. Any member who is not on military duty or
17 does not have a medical condition must be physically present at the
18 meeting to participate. This subsection does not apply to a meeting
19 of a public body held electronically under subsection (1)(d), (e),
20 or (f), or (g), or to a proceeding described in subsection
21 (1)(g).(1)(h).
22 Enacting section 1. This amendatory act does not take effect
23 unless all of the following bills of the 103rd Legislature are
24 enacted into law:
25 (a) Senate Bill No. 418.
26 (b) Senate Bill No. 421.
27 (c) House Bill No. 4805.
