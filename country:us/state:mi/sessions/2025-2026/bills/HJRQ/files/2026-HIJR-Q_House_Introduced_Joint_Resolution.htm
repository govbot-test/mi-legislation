HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION Q

A joint resolution proposing an amendment to the state constitution of 1963, by adding section 6a to article IV, to establish an elected public service commission in this state.

Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives of the state of Michigan, That the following amendment to the state constitution of 1963, to establish an elected public service commission in this state, is proposed, agreed to, and submitted to the people of the state:

ARTICLE IV

Sec. 6a. (1) Beginning January 1, 2029, the existing appointed Michigan public service commission is abolished and replaced with a nonpartisan elected Michigan public service commission in the legislative branch. The nonpartisan elected Michigan public service commission shall exercise all rights, privileges, and the jurisdiction in all respects as has been conferred by law and exercised by the existing appointed Michigan public utilities commission. Members of the Michigan public service commission shall be nominated by party conventions in a manner prescribed by law.

(2) An individual is not eligible to be a member of the Michigan public service commission unless the individual meets all of the following requirements:

(a) Is a citizen of the United States.

(b) Is a resident of this state.

(c) Is not pecuniarily interested in any public utility or person subject to the jurisdiction and control of the Michigan public service commission.

Resolved further, That the foregoing amendment shall be submitted to the people of the state at the next general election in the manner provided by law.
