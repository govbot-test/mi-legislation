SUBSTITUTE FOR
A bill to amend 1984 PA 270, entitled
"Michigan strategic fund act,"
(MCL 125.2001 to 125.2094) by amending the title, as amended by
2005 PA 225, and by adding section 9c.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF MICHIGAN ENACT:
1 TITLE
2 An act relating to the economic development of this state; to
3 create the Michigan strategic fund and to prescribe its powers and
4 duties; to transfer and provide for the acquisition and succession
5 to the rights, properties, obligations, and duties of the job
6 development authority and the Michigan economic development
7 authority to the Michigan strategic fund; to provide for the
8 expenditure of proceeds in certain funds to which the Michigan

4 create certain permanent funds; to exempt the property, income, and
5 operation of the fund and its bonds and notes, and the interest
6 thereon, from certain taxes; to provide for the creation of certain
7 centers within and for the purposes of the Michigan strategic fund;
8 to provide for the creation and funding of certain accounts for
9 certain purposes; to impose certain powers and duties upon certain
10 officials, departments, and authorities of this state; to make
11 certain loans, grants, and investments; to create the strategic
12 advisory board and prescribe its powers and duties; to provide for
13 the development of a certain strategic plan for this state; to
14 provide penalties; to make an appropriation; and to repeal acts and
15 parts of acts.
16 Sec. 9c. (1) Not later than 1 year after the appointment of
17 the members of the strategic advisory board, the strategic advisory
18 board shall develop a strategic plan for specific economic
19 development activities for the next 10 years for this state as a
20 whole. Upon completion, the strategic advisory board shall submit
21 the strategic plan to the governor, the senate majority leader, the
22 speaker of the house of representatives, and the MEDC.
23 (2) Not later than September 30 of the year after the year in
24 which the strategic plan is completed and submitted under
25 subsection (1) and September 30 of each year thereafter, the MEDC,
26 in cooperation with each of the departments that had a
27 representative on the strategic advisory board, shall update the
28 strategic plan.
29 (3) The strategic plan described in subsection (1) must

1 include all of the following:
2 (a) The identification of specific goals and objectives for
3 economic development and the development of quantifiable metrics

6 identified by the plan must include, but are not limited to, all of
7 the following:
8 (i) Encouraging growth that promotes economic opportunity and
9 improves the quality of life for all residents of this state.
10 (ii) Providing incentives that promote and encourage natural
11 resource-based industries, including agriculture, forestry,
12 fishing, mining, and recreational tourism.
13 (iii) Providing appropriate incentives for economic development
14 in rural areas.
15 (iv) Providing appropriate incentives for economic development

17 (v) Providing appropriate incentives for economic development

19 (vi) Determining and developing, in an orderly and fiscally
20 responsible manner, infrastructure needed for rural, suburban, and
21 urban development.
22 (vii) Providing a framework for the development and
23 rehabilitation of affordable housing in this state.
24 (viii) Ensuring that water is available to serve the needs of
25 the residents of this state and that beneficial uses of water are
26 considered and protected.

7 areas, recreational areas, and endangered, unique, and threatened
8 plant and animal species and their habitat.
9 (xii) Protecting and enhancing this state's natural, cultural,
10 and historic resources to maintain this state's heritage and
11 further its tourist economy.

22 educationally and economically sound, vigorous, progressive, and
23 coordinated system of higher education in this state.
24 (xvii) Ensuring the protection and safety of the residents of
25 this state from danger and harm.

27 of-government approach, to achieve the goals and objectives
