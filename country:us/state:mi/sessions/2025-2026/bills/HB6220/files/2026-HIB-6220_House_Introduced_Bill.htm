HOUSE BILL NO. 6220

A bill to amend 2023 PA 281, entitled

"Public officers financial disclosure act,"

by amending sections 3, 6, and 7 (MCL 15.703, 15.706, and 15.707), sections 3 and 7 as amended and section 6 as added by 2025 PA 3.

the people of the state of michigan enact:

Sec. 3. As used in this act:

(a) "Blind trust" means a qualified blind trust or qualified diversified trust as those terms are defined in 5 CFR 2634.403.

(b) "Consumer Price Index" means the most comprehensive index of consumer prices available for the Detroit-Warren-Dearborn area from the Bureau of Labor Statistics of the United States Department of Labor.

(c) "Department" means the department of state.

(d) "Dependent" means an individual claimed by a public officer or a public officer's spouse as a dependent for federal income tax purposes.

(e) (d) "Earned income" means salaries, wages, tips, bonuses, commissions, or other compensation or earnings from employment earned during the reporting period.

(f) "Fair market value" means the value of an item or service that is available to any member of the public at the time the item or service is acquired. The value must be determined on the basis of market prices for the item or service on the day the acquisition occurs. Price fluctuations of the item or service that occurred before or after the acquisition do not affect the fair market value determination.

(g) (e) "Form" means the financial disclosure form provided under section 6 and published by the department in accordance with section 11.

(h) (f) "Gift" means any item a public officer receives that is required to be reported by a lobbyist or lobbyist agent under 1978 PA 472, MCL 4.411 to 4.431.that term as defined in section 4 of 1978 PA 472, MCL 4.414. Notwithstanding section 4(1)(d) of 1978 PA 472, MCL 4.414, gift includes goods, beverages, travel-related payments or reimbursements, and tickets.

(i) (g) "Honorarium" means that term as defined in section 7 of the Michigan campaign finance act, 1976 PA 388, MCL 169.207.

(j) (h) "Liabilities" means what a person owes to another person, including, but not limited to, mortgages or other debts . For purposes of this act, a debt does not include including a revolving debt, an unsecured debt that is from a financial institution or the federal government, or a debt owed by a business entity.

(k) (i) "Lobbyist" means that term as defined in section 5 of 1978 PA 472, MCL 4.415.

(l) (j) "Lobbyist agent" means that term as defined in section 5 of 1978 PA 472, MCL 4.415.

(m) (k) "Public officer" means all of the following:

(i) A state representative.

(ii) A state senator.

(iii) The attorney general.

(iv) The governor.

(v) The lieutenant governor.

(vi) The secretary of state.

(vii) A member of the state board of education.

(viii) A regent of the University of Michigan.

(ix) A member of the board of trustees of Michigan State University.

(x) A member of the board of governors of Wayne State University.

(n) (l) "Report" means the financial disclosure report required under section 10 of article IV of the state constitution of 1963. The report must be in the form provided under section 6.

(o) (m) "Reporting period" means the preceding calendar year.

(p) (n) "Spouse" means an individual who is lawfully married to a public officer as described under 26 CFR 301.7701-18.

(q) (o) "Unearned income" means income that is not earned from employment, including, but not limited to, financial prize, unemployment benefits, annuities, stock dividends, deferred compensation, pension, profit sharing, or retirement income. Unearned income does not include inheritance money or a familial gift.

Sec. 7. (1) A report required under section 5 must include a complete statement of all of the following:

(a) The full name, mailing address, telephone number, and email address of the public officer.

(b) The name and address of the public officer's employer or employers and the positions held during the reporting period, if the public officer receives $1,000.00 or more in annual income from each position.

(c) The name of the spouse of the public officer and, if the public officer's spouse receives $1,000.00 or more in annual income, the occupation of the public officer's spouse and the name of the employer or employers of the public officer's spouse.

(d) Whether the public officer's spouse was registered as a lobbyist or lobbyist agent under 1978 PA 472, MCL 4.411 to 4.431, during the reporting period.

(e) A list of all positions currently held as an officer, director, trustee, partner, proprietor, representative, employee, or consultant of any organization, corporation, firm, partnership, or other business enterprise, nonprofit organization, labor organization, or educational or other institution other than this state. If this subdivision applies, the public officer shall include the name of the organization. For purposes of this subdivision, positions held in any religious, social, fraternal, or political entity, or positions that are solely of an honorary nature, are excluded.

(f) The source of earned income received during the reporting period by the public officer. For purposes of this subdivision, the public officer must disclose each source of income received during the reporting period that is $1,000.00 or more.

(g) Except as otherwise provided in this subdivision, a list of each asset, excluding a business asset, held by the public officer for investment or production of income with a fair market value of $1,000.00 or more during the reporting period, each asset, excluding a business asset, held by the public officer's spouse for investment or production of income with a fair market value of $50,000.00 or more during the reporting period, and any sources of unearned income that exceed $200.00 during the reporting period, including the origins and addresses of the sources of unearned income. The fair market value for the purpose of listing each asset, excluding a business asset, held for investment or production of income under this subdivision must be adjusted for inflation every 4 years using the Consumer Price Index, and rounded up to the nearest $1,000.00.

(h) A list of all liabilities that exceed $10,000.00 owed by the public officer to a creditor at any time during the reporting period.

(i) Except as otherwise provided in this subdivision, a list of any stocks, bonds, or other forms of securities held by the public officer, the public officer's spouse, or held jointly with the public officer's spouse during the reporting period, including the origin and address of the security, if the security has a total aggregate fair market value of $1,000.00 or more. The fair market value for the purpose of listing stocks, bonds, or other forms of securities under this subdivision must be adjusted for inflation every 4 years using the Consumer Price Index, and rounded up to the nearest $1,000.00. For purposes of this act, a public officer is not required to disclose a stock within a widely held investment fund, including, but not limited to, a mutual fund, regulated investment company, pension or deferred compensation plan, or other investment fund, if both of the following apply:

(i) Either the fund is publicly traded or the assets of the fund are widely diversified.

(ii) The public officer or the public officer's spouse does not exercise control over or have the ability to exercise control over the financial interests held by the fund.

(j) A list of any real property in which the public officer or public officer's spouse holds an ownership or other financial interest. For purposes of this subdivision, the public officer is required to include a real property in the report only if that real property has a fair market value of $1,000.00 or more during the reporting period. A public officer filing a report may exclude the street number of a parcel of real property under this subdivision.

(k) A list of any cryptocurrencies held by the public officer during the reporting period.

(l) (k) The date of, identity of parties to, and general terms of any agreements or arrangements made or in effect during the reporting period with respect to the following:

(i) Any future employment that would commence after the public officer leaves office.

(ii) A leave of absence from any former or current employer taken while serving as a public officer.

(iii) A continuation or deferral of payments by a former or current employer other than this state to the public officer, or the public officer's continuing participation in an employee welfare or benefit plan maintained by a former employer.

(m) (l) A list of all gifts received and required to be reported during the reporting period by a lobbyist or lobbyist agent under state law, including the name of the lobbyist or lobbyist agent that provided the gift. If the public officer receives either of the following gifts from a lobbyist, the public officer must report the fair market value of the gift:

(i) A ticket or offer of free or reduced-price attendance to a charity event that has the primary purpose of raising money for a nonprofit organization that is tax-exempt under section 501(c)(3) of the internal revenue code, 26 USC 501.

(ii) Admission to a conference or educational event that is connected to public business or the performance of the governmental duties of the public officer.

(n) A list of all gifts with a value of $1,000.00 or more received from an individual, partnership, corporation, association, or other legal entity.

(2) A public officer is not required to disclose the value of any disclosure under subsection (1).

(3) If a public officer required to file a report under this section holds a beneficial interest in a blind trust, the public officer is not required to include the interests or assets of the blind trust in the report. However, the public officer must indicate in the public officer's report that the public officer holds a beneficial interest in a blind trust. For purposes of this subsection, "beneficial interest" includes, but is not limited to, the interest in a trust of a qualified trust beneficiary or trust beneficiary as those terms are defined under section 7103 of the estates and protected individuals code, 1998 PA 386, MCL 700.7103.

(4) A report required under section 5 must include the following certification: "I certify that the statements I have made on this financial disclosure form are true, complete, and correct to the best of my knowledge and belief, and that I have not moved assets during the reporting period for the purpose of avoiding disclosure under the public officers financial disclosure act".
