A bill to amend 1984 PA 270, entitled

"Michigan strategic fund act,"

by amending sections 29, 29a, 29b, and 29d (MCL 125.2029, 125.2029a, 125.2029b, and 125.2029d), sections 29 and 29b as added by 2008 PA 75, section 29a as amended by 2011 PA 291, and section 29d as amended by 2020 PA 199; and to repeal acts and parts of acts.

the people of the state of michigan enact:

Sec. 29. As used in this chapter:

(a) "Applicant" means that term as defined in section 285 of the income tax act of 1967, 1967 PA 281, MCL 206.285.

(b) (a) "Commissioner" means the Michigan film commissioner created in section 29b.

(c) (b) "Council" means the Michigan film office advisory council created in section 29c.

(d) "Film and media" includes all of the following:

(i) Films.

(ii) Television programs.

(iii) Commercials.

(iv) Corporate videos.

(v) Commercial photography.

(vi) Digital media.

(e) (c) "Local film office" means an office, agency, bureau, or department of a political subdivision of this state that seeks to promote film production within the political subdivision and that is funded principally by the political subdivision.

(f) (d) "Office" means the Michigan film and digital media office created in section 29a.

(g) (e) "Promotion fund" means the Michigan film promotion fund created under section 29d.

(h) "Qualified Michigan vendor" means that term as defined in section 285 of the income tax act of 1967, 1967 PA 281, MCL 206.285.

(i) "Qualified personnel expenditure" means that term as defined in section 285 of the income tax act of 1967, 1967 PA 281, MCL 206.285.

(j) "Qualified production" means that term as defined in section 285 of the income tax act of 1967, 1967 PA 281, MCL 206.285.

(k) "Qualified production expenditure" means that term as defined in section 285 of the income tax act of 1967, 1967 PA 281, MCL 206.285.

(l) "State" means the state of Michigan.

(m) "Tax credit program" means the tax credits under sections 285 and 678 of the income tax act of 1967, 1967 PA 281, MCL 206.285 and 206.678.

Sec. 29a. (1) The Michigan film and digital media office is created in the fund. The office shall be is the successor to any authority, powers, duties, functions, or responsibilities of the Michigan film office under former section 21 of the history, arts, and libraries act, 2001 PA 63.

(2) The office may do all of the following:

(a) Promote and market locations, talent, crews, facilities, and technical production and other services related to film, digital media, and television film and media production in this state.

(b) Provide to interested persons descriptive and pertinent information on locations, talent, crews, facilities, and technical production and other services related to film, digital media, and television film and media production in this state.

(c) Provide technical assistance to the film, television, and digital media film and media industry in locating and securing the use of locations, talent, crews, facilities, and services in this state.

(d) Encourage community and Michigan film, digital media, and television film and media production industry participation in, and coordination with, state and local efforts to attract film, digital media, and television film and media production in this state.

(e) Serve as this state's chief liaison with the film, digital media, and television film and media production industry and with other governmental units and agencies for the purpose of promoting, encouraging, and facilitating film, digital media, and television film and media production in this state.

(f) Explain the benefits and advantages of producing films, digital media, and television productions film and media in this state.

(g) Assist film, digital media, and television film and media producers with securing location authorization and other appropriate services connected with film, digital media, and television film and media production in this state.

(h) Scout potential film locations for national and international film, digital media, and television prospects.

(i) Escort film, digital media, and television producers on location scouting trips.

(h) (j) Serve as a liaison between film, digital media, and television film and media producers, state agencies, local agencies, federal agencies, community organizations and leaders, and the film, digital media, and television film and media industry in this state.

(i) (k) Assist film, digital media, and television film and media producers in securing permits to film or shoot at specific locations in this state and in obtaining needed services related to the film and media production. of a film, digital media, or a television program.

(j) (l) Represent this state at film, digital media, and television film and media industry trade shows and film festivals.

(k) (m) Sponsor workshops or conferences on topics relating to filmmaking, including, but not limited to, screenwriting, film financing, and the preparation of communities to attract and assist film, digital media, and television film and media productions in this state.

(l) (n) Encourage cooperation between local, state, and federal government agencies and local film offices in the location and production of films, digital media, and television programming film and media in this state.

(m) (o) Coordinate activities with local film offices.

(n) (p) Facilitate cooperation from state departments and agencies, local governments, local film offices, federal agencies, and private sector entities in the location and production of films, digital media, and television programming film and media in this state.

(o) (q) Prepare, maintain, and distribute a directory of persons, firms, and governmental agencies available to assist in the production of films, digital media, and television programming film and media in this state.

(p) (r) Prepare, maintain, and distribute a digital library depicting the variety and extent of the locations within this state for film, digital media, and television film and media productions.

(q) (s) Prepare and distribute appropriate promotional and informational materials, in a frequently asked question format and other formats, that do all of the following:

(i) Describe desirable locations in this state for film, digital media, and television film and media production.

(ii) Explain the benefits and advantages of producing films, digital media, and television productions film and media in this state.

(iii) Detail services and assistance available from state government, from local film offices, and from the film, digital media, and television film and media industry in this state.

(r) (t) Solicit and accept gifts, grants, labor, loans, and other aid from any person, government, or entity. The film office shall disclose the identity and amount of all gifts, grants, and other donations on its website.

(s) (u) Employ technical experts, other officers, agents, or employees, permanent or temporary, paid from the funds of the office. The office shall determine the qualifications, duties, and compensation of those the office employs.

(t) (v) Contract for goods and services and engage personnel as necessary to perform the duties of the office under this chapter.

(u) (w) Study, develop, and prepare reports or plans the office considers necessary to assist the office in the exercise of its powers under this chapter and to monitor and evaluate progress under this chapter.

(v) (x) Exercise the duties and responsibilities vested in the office under this chapter and all of the following:

(i) Section 88d.

(ii) Section 88j(3)(e).

(iii) Section 4cc of the general sales tax act, 1933 PA 167, MCL 205.54cc.

(iv) Sections 455 to 459 of the Michigan business tax act, 2007 PA 36, MCL 208.1455 to 208.1459.

(y) Create and operate a film and digital media production assistance program to encourage film and digital media production throughout this state as provided in section 29h.

(iii) Sections 285 and 678 of the income tax act of 1967, 1967 PA 281, MCL 206.285 and 206.678.

(w) Do all of the following in connection with the tax credit program:

(i) Prescribe forms for applications, notifications, contracts, or other agreements.

(ii) Beginning on the effective date of the amendatory act that added this subparagraph and until the date that is 10 years after the effective date of the amendatory act that added this subparagraph, accept applications, determine eligibility, and issue qualified production certificates for tax credits.

(iii) Work with applicants, qualified Michigan vendors, and other vendors to ensure that all qualified personnel expenditures and qualified production expenditures are paid.

(iv) Assist applicants pursuant to this chapter and sections 285 and 678 of the income tax act of 1967, 1967 PA 281, MCL 206.285 and 206.678, to promote, foster, and support qualified productions and the related job creation or retention within this state.

(v) Gather information and conduct inquiries, in the manner and by the methods the office considers appropriate, including, but not limited to, gathering all of the following information:

(A) Information required for the office to comply with this chapter and sections 285 and 678 of the income tax act of 1967, 1967 PA 281, MCL 206.285 and 206.678.

(B) Information regarding applicants that the office considers necessary or appropriate for the purpose of making any designations or certifications.

(C) Information to assist the office with any recommendations or guidance in furtherance of the purposes of this chapter and the tax credit program.

(D) Financial reports, returns, or other records relating to the applicant or the qualified production.

(vi) Require applicants, on written request, to issue any necessary authorization to the appropriate federal, state, or local authority for the release of information described in subparagraph (v).

(vii) Subject to appropriation, employ sufficient personnel for the administration, operation, and related support of the tax credit program and as required to adequately discharge the office's duties and responsibilities under this chapter and sections 285 and 678 of the income tax act of 1967, 1967 PA 281, MCL 206.285 and 206.678.

(viii) Require an applicant to keep proper books of record and accounts in accordance with generally accepted accounting principles consistently applied.

(ix) Require the books, records, and other documents related to the qualified production in the custody or control of the applicant to be open to reasonable inspection by the office for at least 1 year after the completion of the agreed-upon procedures as that term is defined in section 285 of the income tax act of 1967, 1967 PA 281, MCL 206.285. For purposes of this subparagraph, reasonable inspection includes, but is not limited to, both of the following:

(A) Making copies of the books, records, or other documents.

(B) The inspection or appraisal of any of the assets of the applicant or the qualified production.

(x) Take any actions that are necessary or appropriate to protect this state's interest in the event of bankruptcy, default, foreclosure, or noncompliance with the terms and conditions of the tax credit program or an agreement or contract under that program, including selling, leasing, or otherwise disposing of, on terms and conditions determined by the office to be appropriate, any real or personal property that the office receives as a result of the bankruptcy, default, foreclosure, or noncompliance.

(x) (z) All other things necessary or convenient to achieve the objectives and purposes of the office, this chapter, or other laws that relate to the purposes and responsibilities of the office.

(3) The enumeration of a power in this chapter shall does not be construed as a limitation upon limit the general powers of the office. The powers granted under this chapter are in addition to those powers granted by any other law.

(4) The commissioner and the president of the fund shall cooperate in administering the budget, procurement, and related management functions of the office. The fund may provide the office with staff support and other services to assist the office in performing the functions and duties of the office.

(5) State departments, agencies, boards, commissions, and officers and local film offices shall cooperate with the office in the performance of the office's duties under this chapter.
