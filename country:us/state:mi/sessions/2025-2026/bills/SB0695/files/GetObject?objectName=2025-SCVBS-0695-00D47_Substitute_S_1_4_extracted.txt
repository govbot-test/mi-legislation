Section 1:
SUBSTITUTE FOR
A bill to amend 1949 PA 300, entitled
"Michigan vehicle code,"
by amending section 801j (MCL 257.801j), as amended by 2014 PA 171.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF MICHIGAN ENACT:
1 Sec. 801j. (1) Except as otherwise provided in subsection (6),
2 in addition to the required vehicle registration tax under section
3 801(1)(p), a regional transit authority created under the regional
4 transit authority act, 2012 PA 387, MCL 124.541 to 124.558, may
5 charge an additional tax on vehicle registrations issued to
6 residents of a public transit region of not more than $1.20 for
7 each $1,000.00 or fraction of $1,000.00 of the vehicle's list price
8 as used in calculating the tax under section 801(1)(p). The
9 authority may charge the additional tax only upon the approval of a

2
1 majority of the electors in a public transit region voting on the
2 tax at an election held on the regular November election date as
3 provided in section 641(1)(d) 641 of the Michigan election law,
4 1954 PA 116, MCL 168.641.
5 (2) In addition to any other requirements imposed by law, the
6 ballot question proposing authorization of the tax under subsection
7 (1) shall must specify how the proceeds of the tax shall must be
8 expended.
9 (3) The tax collected under this section shall must only be
10 used by the regional transit authority for comprehensive
11 transportation purposes as defined by law for purposes of section 9
12 of article IX of the state constitution of 1963.
13 (4) A proposal for a tax under this section shall must not be
14 placed on the ballot under subsection (1) unless the proposal is
15 adopted by a resolution of the board of directors of the regional
16 transit authority and certified by the board of directors not later
17 than 70 days before the election to the county clerk of each county
18 within the public transit region for inclusion on the ballot.
19 (5) Except as otherwise provided in subsection (6), if a
20 majority of voters in a public transit region approve a tax under
21 subsection (1), no later than 1 year after voter approval, the
22 secretary of state shall collect the tax on all vehicles registered
23 to residents of the public transit region under section 801(1)(p)
24 and shall credit the tax collected to the regional transit
25 authority, minus necessary collection expenses as provided in
26 section 9 of article IX of the state constitution of 1963.
27 Necessary collection expenses incurred by the secretary of state
28 under this subsection shall must be based upon on an established
29 cost allocation methodology.

3
1 (6) This section does not apply to a company test vehicle. As
2 used in this subsection, "company test vehicle" means a vehicle
3 that is owned by a manufacturer and that satisfies 1 or both of the
4 following:
5 (a) The vehicle is part of a product testing program as
6 defined by the United States department of treasury under treas.
7 reg. 1.132-5(n)(2001).
8 (b) The vehicle is furnished by the manufacturer to an
9 employee of the manufacturer for the purpose of testing, evaluating
10 product quality and performance, reporting defects, or suggesting
11 product or production improvements as an ordinary and necessary
12 business expense of the manufacturer.
13 Enacting section 1. This amendatory act takes effect January
14 1, 2028.
15 Enacting section 2. This amendatory act does not take effect
16 unless Senate Bill No. 691 of the 103rd Legislature is enacted into
17 law.
