SUBSTITUTE FOR
A bill to amend 2019 PA 152, entitled
"Lawful internet gaming act,"
by amending sections 14 and 16 (MCL 432.314 and 432.316), section
16 as amended by 2024 PA 142.
THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF MICHIGAN ENACT:
1 Sec. 14. (1) Except for an internet gaming operator that is an
2 Indian tribe, an internet gaming operator is subject to a graduated
3 tax on the adjusted gross receipts received each calendar year by
4 the internet gaming operator from all internet gaming it conducts
5 under this act as set forth below:
6 (a) For adjusted gross receipts less than $4,000,000.00, a tax
7 of 20%.
8 (b) For adjusted gross receipts of $4,000,000.00 or more but

4 (d) For adjusted gross receipts of $10,000,000.00 or more but
5 less than $12,000,000.00, a tax of 26%.
6 (e) For adjusted gross receipts of $12,000,000.00 or more, a
7 tax of 28%.
8 (2) An internet gaming operator that is an Indian tribe is
9 subject to the payment requirements under section 7(1)(f).
10 (3) An internet gaming operator shall pay the tax or payment,
11 as applicable, under subsection (1) or (2) on a monthly basis. The
12 payment for each monthly accounting period is due on the tenth day
13 of the following month.
14 (4) Except as provided in this act and section 12(17) of the
15 Michigan Gaming Control and Revenue Act, 1996 IL 1, MCL 432.212, an

19 gaming operators by this state or any political subdivision of this
20 state, except as provided in this act. This subsection does not
21 impair the contractual rights under an existing development
22 agreement between a city and an internet gaming operator that holds
23 a casino license under the Michigan Gaming Control and Revenue Act,
24 1996 IL 1, MCL 432.201 to 432.226.
25 (5) In addition to payment of the tax and other fees as
26 provided in this act, and to any payment required pursuant to an
27 existing development agreement described in subsection (4), if a

4 Sec. 16. (1) The internet gaming fund is created in the state
5 treasury.
6 (2) The state treasurer may receive money or other assets
7 required to be paid into the fund under this act or from any other

10 fund interest and earnings from fund investments.
11 (3) The board is the administrator of the fund for auditing
12 purposes.

13 (f) (e) All money remaining after expenditures under
14 subdivisions (a), (b), (c), and (d), and (e) to be deposited into
15 the state school aid fund established under section 11 of article
16 IX of the state constitution of 1963.

18 term as defined in section 2 of the Traxler-McCauley-Law-Bowman
19 bingo act, 1972 PA 382, MCL 432.102.
