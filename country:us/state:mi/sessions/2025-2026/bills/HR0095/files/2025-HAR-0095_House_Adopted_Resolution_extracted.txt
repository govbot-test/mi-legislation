Morgan, Greene, Beson, Breen, Dievendorf, MacDonell, Paiz, Rheingans, Rogers and Weiss offered the following resolution: A resolution to express our commitment to strengthening and deepening ties between the state of Michigan and Taiwan. Whereas, Taiwan shares values of freedom, democracy, human rights, rule of law, peace, and prosperity with the United States and the state of Michigan; and Whereas, Taiwan and the United States are key partners in trade. Taiwan imported 3.93 billion dollarsГўВҖВҷ worth of agricultural products from the United States in 2023, and another 3.8 billion dollarsГўВҖВҷ worth of agricultural and related products in 2024. In 2022, the United States Department of Agriculture noted that Taiwan was the fourth-largest export market for U.S. agricultural products on a per capita basis. Furthermore, in 2023, Taiwan was our nationГўВҖВҷs eighth-largest export market for corn and our sixth-largest export market for soybeans. Due to our nationГўВҖВҷs high-quality produce, the United States remains one of TaiwanГўВҖВҷs largest sources of agricultural products, supplying nearly a quarter of TaiwanГўВҖВҷs agricultural imports in 2023; and Whereas, Taiwan and the state of Michigan have enjoyed a long and mutually beneficial relationship. Taiwan was MichiganГўВҖВҷs tenth-largest export market in Asia in 2024, with over 257 million dollarsГўВҖВҷ worth of Michigan goods exported to Taiwan that year. In early March 2024, the state of Michigan opened the Michigan Taiwan Office to promote further investment by Taiwanese firms in our state. Section 4(b)(1) of the act provides: ГўВҖВңWhenever the laws of the United States refer or relate to foreign countries, nations, states, governments, or similar entities, such terms shall include and such laws shall apply with respect to Taiwan.ГўВҖВқ Thus, it is legitimate for Michigan businesses to refer to Taiwan as ГўВҖВңTaiwanГўВҖВқ when conducting business and establishing ties with Taiwan; and Whereas, The United States is actively taking steps to strengthen bilateral trade between the United States and Taiwan. The first agreement under the U.S.-Taiwan Initiative on 21st Century Trade officially came into effect on December 10, 2024, marking a significant milestone. Negotiations regarding a second agreement are ongoing. While this progress is encouraging, an avoidance of double taxation agreement for Taiwan has not yet been enacted, though legislation on this subject has been approved in the United States House of Representatives.
